## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Present perfect with direct object pronouns (*lo*, *la*, *los*, *las*)**
The direct object pronoun goes *before* the auxiliary *haber*, not attached to the past participle.

- *No lo he visto hoy.* — I haven't seen him today.
- *La he visto.* — I have seen her / it (fem.).
- *Ya la he visto también.* — I have already seen it too.
- *Ya la has visto.* — You have already seen it.

**2. Indirect object pronoun *le* with present perfect (decir)**
When "telling" someone, use *le* (to him/to her) before the auxiliary:

- *Le he dicho.* — I have told him/her.
- *Le he dicho la dirección.* — I have told her the address.
- *Le has dicho suficientes veces.* — You have told him/her enough times.

**3. Directions vocabulary in practice**
- *Seguimos derecho dos cuadras.* — We continue straight ahead two blocks.
- *A la izquierda.* — To the left.
- *¿A cuántas cuadras?* — How many blocks away?

**4. *Ya* + present perfect = "already"**
- *Ya la sabe.* — She already knows it.
- *Ya me voy.* — I'm leaving right now.
- *Ya le he dicho.* — I've already told him/her.

**5. *He hablado con ella* — spoken with her**
Drill pattern: *he hablado / has hablado / ha hablado* + prepositional phrase.
- *He hablado con ella por teléfono.* — I've spoken with her by phone.
- *También he hablado con ella.* — I've also spoken with her.

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 27 uses the **informal** *tú* register throughout (*¿Vives cerca?*, *¿La has visto?*). The *usted* forms appear when addressing the father.

### Pronunciation Notes
- *cuadra*: stress on first syllable — KWA-dra. The *qu* before *a/o/u* is pronounced /k/.
- *despacio*: three syllables — des-PA-cio; the *c* before *io* = /s/ (Latin America) or /θ/ (Spain).
- *derecho*: the *ch* is always /tʃ/ as in English "church."
- *excelente*: x = /ks/; stress on the penultimate syllable — ex-se-LEN-te.

## Summary
- **vivir** — regular -ir; drills *vivo / vives*
- **recordar** — stem-changing (o→ue); drills *recuerdo*
- **aprender** — regular -er; drills *aprendo*
- **estudiar** — regular -ar; drills *estudio*
- **seguir** — irregular (e→i stem); drills *seguimos*
- **haber** — irregular auxiliary for present perfect; drills *he / has / ha* + past participles *visto, hablado, dicho, estudiado*
