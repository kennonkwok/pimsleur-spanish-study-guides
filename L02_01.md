## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *quedar* vs. *estar* for location**
This is the key grammatical distinction of Lesson 1. Both verbs can express "to be (somewhere)," but they serve different purposes:

| Use *estar* for | Use *quedar* for |
|---|---|
| People: *¿Dónde estás tú?* | Fixed locations when giving directions: *¿Dónde queda el cine?* |
| Movable objects: *¿Dónde está el libro?* | Businesses, buildings, streets: *Mi trabajo queda lejos.* |

- *¿Dónde queda el metro?* — Where is the subway (located)?
- *No queda lejos.* — It's not located far.
- *Mi trabajo queda cerca.* — My work is (located) nearby.

**2. *tener que* + infinitive (obligation)**
*Tener que* + infinitive means "to have to [do something]." The infinitive that follows never changes form.

- *Tengo que trabajar.* — I have to work.
- *Tenemos que comer.* — We have to eat.
- *Tengo que irme ahora.* — I have to leave now.

**3. *poder* + infinitive (ability)**
*Poder* (o→ue) + infinitive means "can / to be able to." The infinitive may take a clitic pronoun appended to it.

- *No puedo decirte.* — I can't tell you.
- *Puedes decirme dónde queda.* — Can you tell me where it is?
- *No podemos ir a pie.* — We can't go on foot.

**4. Prepositional pronouns: *conmigo* / *contigo***
After most prepositions, Spanish uses regular subject pronouns (*para mí, con él*). But *con* + *mí/ti* forms special merged words:

- *con + mí → conmigo* (with me)
- *con + ti → contigo* (with you)

**5. Time expression: *un cuarto para las [hour]***
"A quarter to [hour]" = *un cuarto para las [number]*. *Para* here means "before/to" the hour.

- *Es un cuarto para las ocho.* — It's a quarter to eight.
- *A un cuarto para las siete.* — At a quarter to seven.

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Ask what time it is | *¿Qué hora es?* |
| Express time (quarter to) | *Es un cuarto para las ocho.* |
| Say you have to do something | *Tengo que trabajar. / Tenemos que comer.* |
| Ask where something is located | *¿Dónde queda el cine?* |
| Can you tell me where... | *¿Puedes decirme dónde queda?* |
| Say something is near/far | *No queda lejos. / Mi trabajo queda lejos.* |
| Express transportation | *Podemos ir en metro. / Prefiero ir a pie.* |
| Ask how long to get there | *¿En cuánto tiempo vamos a llegar?* |
| Answer: in half an hour | *Vamos a llegar en media hora.* |
| Ask if someone wants to go with you | *¿Quiere ir conmigo?* (formal) / *¿Quieres ir conmigo?* (informal) |

### *Gustar* Construction (Review)
*Gustar* works in reverse — the thing liked is the grammatical subject:

- *Me gusta el cine.* — I like the movies. (Lit. "The movies please me.")
- Subject (*el cine*) = 3rd person singular → *gusta*

## Verb Conjugations

### quedar — to be located / to stay
**Type:** regular -ar (used idiomatically for fixed locations)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: quedo | nosotros: quedamos |
| 2nd | tú: quedas | vosotros: quedáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **queda** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: quedan |

Lesson drills: *queda* — *¿Dónde queda el cine?*, *No queda lejos.*

---

### tener — to have

## Summary
- **quedar** — regular -ar; key new use: fixed-location directions (vs. *estar* for people/movable objects)
- **tener** — irregular (*tengo*, e→ie); *tener que* + inf. = obligation
- **poder** — stem-changing (o→ue); *poder* + inf. = ability/can
- **preferir** — stem-changing (e→ie/i)
- **ir** — irregular; *ir a* + inf. = going to (near future)
