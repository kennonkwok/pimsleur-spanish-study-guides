## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *tener prisa* — to be in a hurry**
*Prisa* (hurry) uses *tener*, not *estar*.

- *No tengo prisa.* — I'm not in a hurry.
- *Ahora mismo tengo prisa.* — Right now I'm in a hurry.
- Contrast: *estar en prisa* is NOT used in standard Spanish.

**2. *estar + gerund* for ongoing malfunctions**
The present progressive (*estar* + -ndo) is used to describe what is currently happening, including machine failures.

- *La computadora está fallando.* — The computer is down (malfunctioning).
- *El fax está funcionando bien.* — The fax machine is working well.

**3. *menor / mayor / del medio* — relative age without *más***
These comparatives are irregular and do not use *más*:

- *el/la menor* — the younger / youngest (NOT *más pequeño* in this context)
- *el/la mayor* — the older / oldest
- *el del medio / la del medio* — the middle one

Examples:
- *Mi nieta menor tiene nueve meses.* — My younger granddaughter is nine months old.
- *Mi nieta mayor tiene cuatro años.* — My older granddaughter is four years old.
- *¿Su nieto es el del medio?* — Is your grandson the middle one?

**4. *sabía* — imperfect used for past knowledge (review + extension)**
This lesson extends the *sabía* pattern:

- *¿Sabía que tengo un nieto?* — Did you know that I have a grandson? (Ud. form)
- *No, no lo sabía.* — No, I didn't know that.
- *Sabía que mis hijos ya están casados.* — Did you know my children are already married?
- Note: *sabía* is both yo and él/ella/Ud. in the imperfect — context determines person.

**5. *se casó* — preterite of reflexive *casarse***
*Casarse* = to get married (reflexive). Third-person singular preterite: *se casó*.

- *Se casó el año pasado en verano.* — She/he got married last year in summer.
- *Mis hijos ya están casados.* — My children are already married. (*estar* + past participle for state)

**6. *estar grande* — to be grown up**
In this context, *grande* with *estar* means "grown up," not physically large.

- *Mis hijos están grandes.* — My children are grown up.
- *Ya son grandes.* — They're already grown up.

## Verb Conjugations

### fallar — to fail / to malfunction
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: fallo | nosotros: fallamos |
| 2nd | tú: fallas | vosotros: falláis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: falla | ellos/ellas/Uds.: fallan |

Lesson drills: *La computadora está fallando.* (progressive — estar + gerund *fallando*)

---

## Summary

- **fallar** — regular -ar; used in progressive (*está fallando*) to describe a malfunctioning machine
- **funcionar** — regular -ar; used in progressive (*está funcionando*) to describe a working machine
- **casarse** — regular -ar reflexive; preterite *se casó* = she/he got married; *están casados* = they are married (state)
- **estudiar** — regular -ar; from opening dialogue; *estudia inglés* = she/he studies English
