## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Present perfect — extended drilling across multiple verbs**
Lesson 25 heavily reinforces the present perfect (*haber* + past participle) with several new verbs:

| Spanish | English | Verb | Past participle |
|---|---|---|---|
| *No he comido allí.* | I haven't eaten there. | *comer* | *comido* |
| *No he tenido tiempo.* | I haven't had time. | *tener* | *tenido* |
| *No he terminado.* | I haven't finished. | *terminar* | *terminado* |
| *Hemos hablado con Juan.* | We've spoken with Juan. | *hablar* | *hablado* |
| *Ha cambiado de idea.* | He has changed his mind. | *cambiar* | *cambiado* |
| *No has podido salir.* | You haven't been able to go out. | *poder* | *podido* |

Irregular past participles from this unit:
- *tener* → *tenido* (regular -ido pattern)
- *poder* → *podido* (regular -ido pattern)
- *ver* → *visto* (irregular — from L20 review)

**2. *me gustaría* — "I would like" (conditional of *gustar*)**
*Gustaría* is the conditional of *gustar*. It expresses polite desire. Like *gustar*, it is typically used in 3rd person forms matched to the subject.

- *Me gustaría ir al nuevo restaurante.* — I would like to go to the new restaurant.
- *Me gustaría jugar juntos.* — I would like to play together.

Compare: *quiero* (I want — direct) vs. *me gustaría* (I would like — polite/conditional).

**3. *traer* — "to bring" (irregular verb)**
*Traer* is a common irregular verb. Key irregularity: yo present = *traigo*.

Full present conjugation:
| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| yo | **traigo** | nosotros: traemos |
| tú | traes | vosotros: traéis |
| él/Ud. | trae | ellos/Uds.: traen |

- *Puedo traer la comida.* — I can bring the food.
- *Dije que puedo traer la comida.* — I said that I can bring the food.
- *Yo podría traer.* — I could bring. (conditional)

**4. *dije que* — reported speech with present perfect**
When reporting what was said, the main clause preterite (*dije*) combines with a present perfect in the embedded clause:

- *Dije que ha cambiado de idea.* — I said that he has changed his mind.
- *Me dijiste que quiere ser médico.* — You told me that he wants to be a doctor.

**5. Days of the week — full scheduling context**
Full week of days drilled in context of business travel:
- *el martes* — Tuesday
- *el miércoles* — Wednesday
- *el jueves* — Thursday
- *el viernes* — Friday
- *el fin de semana* — the weekend
- *el domingo* — Sunday
- *el próximo fin de semana* — next weekend

**6. *día festivo* and bank/store situations**
- *Es día festivo.* — It's a holiday.
- *El banco no está abierto.* — The bank isn't open.
- *Las tiendas están abiertas.* — The stores are open.
- *Pero los bancos no.* — But not the banks.

## Verb Conjugations

### traer — to bring
**Type:** irregular (yo: *traigo*; otherwise regular -er endings with *aer* pattern)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **traigo** | nosotros: traemos |
| 2nd | tú: traes | vosotros: traéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: trae | ellos/ellas/Uds.: traen |

Lesson drills: *puedo traer la comida*, *yo podría traer*, *dije que puedo traer la comida*.

---

### comer — to eat
**Type:** regular -er

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: como | nosotros: comemos |
| 2nd | tú: comes | vosotros: coméis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: come | ellos/ellas/Uds.: comen |

Lesson drills: present perfect *no he comido allí*, *no has comido nada*. Past participle *comido* heavily drilled.

---

### regresar — to return (review)
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **regreso** | nosotros: regresamos |
| 2nd | tú: **regresas** | vosotros: regresáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **regresa** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **regresan** |

Lesson drills: *regreso el domingo en la tarde*, *¿cuándo regresas?*, *dije que regreso el domingo en la tarde*.

---

### querer — stem-changing (e→ie), review
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **quiero** | nosotros: queremos |
| 2nd | tú: **quieres** | vosotros: queréis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **quiere** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **quieren** |

Lesson drills: *quieren visitarme en junio*, *quizá podríamos* (note: not *querer* itself but context).

---

### haber — present tense (present perfect auxiliary — review)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **he** | nosotros: hemos |
| 2nd | tú: **has** | vosotros: habéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **ha** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **han** |

Lesson drills: *no he comido*, *no he tenido tiempo*, *no he terminado*, *ha cambiado*, *no has podido salir*, *hemos hablado con Juan*, *han podido*.

## Summary

- **traer** — irregular yo (*traigo*); "to bring" — drilled in *puedo traer la comida*
- **comer** — regular -er; past participle *comido* drilled heavily in present perfect
- **regresar** — regular -ar; reviewed: *regreso*, *regresas*, *regresa*, *regresan* — scheduling context
- **haber** — irregular auxiliary; present perfect extended to: *comido, tenido, terminado, podido, cambiado*
- **gustar** (conditional) — *me gustaría* = "I would like" — polite desire
- **querer** — stem-changing (e→ie); *quieren visitarme* — 3rd person plural drilled
