## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Preterite of *llegar* — *llegué*, *llegaste***
*Llegar* is regular -ar in the preterite (spelling change g→gu before e in *yo* form):
- *Llegué tarde.* — I arrived late.
- *¿Por qué llegaste tarde?* — Why did you arrive late?

**2. *¿qué pasó?* — what happened?**
*Pasar* in preterite (*pasó*) forms this very common question:
- *¿Qué pasó?* — What happened?

**3. Adjective *perdido/perdida* with *estar***
*Estar* (not *ser*) is used with *perdido* because it's a state:
- *Estamos perdidos.* — We're lost. (m or mixed group)
- *No estoy perdida.* — I'm not lost. (f speaker)
- *Yo estoy perdido.* — I'm lost. (m speaker)

**4. *con frecuencia* / *con mucha frecuencia* — frequently / very frequently**
This prepositional phrase functions as an adverb:
- *Con frecuencia nos visitan.* — They frequently visit us.
- *Con mucha frecuencia.* — Very frequently.

**5. *nos visitan* — they visit us**
*Nos* is the 1st person plural object pronoun (indirect and direct):
- *Muchos amigos nos visitan.* — Many friends visit us.
- *Ellos nos visitan.* — They visit us.

**6. *solo* as "only"**
When used as an adverb, *solo* means "only/just":
- *Solo son las doce.* — It's only twelve.
- *Solo es la una y cuarto.* — It's only a quarter past one.

**7. *mil gracias* — many thanks**
*Mil* (thousand) used hyperbolically for strong thanks:
- *Mil gracias.* — Many thanks. / A thousand thanks.

**8. Preterite of irregular verbs reviewed**
This lesson consolidates several preterites from earlier lessons:
- *llegué / llegaste* (*llegar*)
- *dije / dijiste* (*decir*)
- *te lo dije ayer* — I told you yesterday

## Verb Conjugations

### llegar — to arrive
**Type:** regular -ar (spelling change g→gu before e in preterite *yo* form)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: llego | nosotros: llegamos |
| 2nd | tú: llegas | vosotros: llegáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: llega | ellos/ellas/Uds.: llegan |

**Preterite (drilled):**
*yo: llegué, tú: llegaste, él: llegó, nosotros: llegamos, ellos: llegaron*

Lesson drills: *llegué tarde*, *¿por qué llegaste tarde?*, *siempre llegamos tarde*.

## Summary
- **llegar** — regular -ar (g→gu in preterite yo); *llegué/llegaste* drilled; "to arrive"
- **entender** — stem-changing (e→ie); "to understand"
- **viajar** — regular -ar; "to travel"; *viajamos* (nosotros) heavily drilled
- **mostrar** — stem-changing (o→ue); "to show"; clitics: *mostrarme/mostrarte*
- **visitar** — regular -ar; "to visit"; *nos visitan* (they visit us)
- **pasar** — regular -ar; *¿qué pasó?* = what happened? (preterite 3rd sg.)
