## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *mejor* as both comparative and superlative**
Unlike English, Spanish uses the same word *mejor* for both "better" and "best." Context and article determine meaning:

| Form | Example | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Comparative (no article) | *Es mejor cliente.* | She is a better client. |
| Superlative (with article) | *Es el mejor cliente.* | He is the best client. |
| Superlative + *de* | *El mejor cliente de esta empresa.* | The best client of this company. |

- *Mejor* precedes the noun when used as superlative: *nuestro mejor cliente* (NOT *nuestro cliente mejor*).
- It does not change for gender: *el mejor cliente* / *la mejor cliente*.

**2. *difícil de* + infinitive vs. *difícil para mí* + infinitive**
Two constructions for expressing difficulty appear in this lesson:

| Pattern | Example | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| *es difícil de* + inf. | *Este informe es difícil de entender.* | Thing is the subject; difficulty is a property of it |
| *es difícil para mí* + inf. | *Es difícil para mí entender.* | Difficulty is personal; "for me to understand" |

**3. *conseguir* + indirect object clitic (me/te)**
*Conseguir* (to get/obtain) attaches object pronouns as clitics either before the conjugated verb or appended to the infinitive:

- *¿Puedes conseguirme un intérprete?* — Can you get me an interpreter?
- *Puedo conseguirte un intérprete.* — I can get you an interpreter.
- *Voy a conseguir un intérprete.* — I'm going to get an interpreter.

**4. *quisiera* — polite "I would like"**
*Quisiera* is the imperfect subjunctive of *querer*, conventionally used as a softened, polite form of "I would like." It functions like *quiero* but is more courteous:

- *Quisiera pedirte un favor.* — I would like to ask you a favor.
- *Quisiera otro favor.* — I'd like another favor.

**5. *en mi/tu/su lugar* — "in my/your/his place"**
The phrase *en + possessive + lugar* means "instead of [someone]" or "in [someone's] place":

- *Tal vez deberías ir en mi lugar.* — Perhaps you should go in my place.
- *En tu lugar, ¿por qué?* — In your place — why?
- *En su lugar* / *En lugar del señor López* — In his place / Instead of Mr. López.

**6. *toda la tarde/mañana* — "the whole afternoon/morning"**
*Todo/toda* + definite article + time period = "the whole [time period]":

- *Toda la tarde.* — The whole afternoon.
- *Casi toda la mañana.* — Almost the whole morning.

## Verb Conjugations

### durar — to last
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: duro | nosotros: duramos |
| 2nd | tú: duras | vosotros: duráis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **duró** (pret.) / **dura** (pres.) | ellos/ellas/Uds.: duran |

Lesson drills: *duró demasiado*, *no va a durar mucho tiempo*. Preterite *duró* is the form used most.

## Summary

- **durar** — regular -ar; "to last"; lesson drills preterite *duró* and future periphrastic *va a durar*
- **comenzar** — stem-changing (e→ie); preterite *comenzó* drilled
- **terminar** — regular -ar; preterite *terminó* drilled
- **descansar** — regular -ar; "to rest"; *quiero descansar*
- **conseguir** — stem-changing (e→i) + go-verb (*consigo*); key new verb: "to get/obtain"; drilled with clitics *-me* and *-te*
- **pedir** — stem-changing (e→i); "to ask for/request"; used in *quisiera pedirte un favor*
- **entender** — stem-changing (e→ie); "to understand"; drilled in *difícil de entender* and *entiendes casi todo*
- **depender** — regular -er; "to depend"; always + *de*
- **deber** (conditional) — "should"; *deberías* drilled for polite suggestion
