## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *hay que* + infinitive (impersonal obligation)**
*Hay que* expresses an obligation without specifying who must do it — equivalent to "one must" or "it must be."

- *Hay que repararlo.* — It must be repaired. (Someone has to repair it.)
- Contrast with *tener que* + inf., which specifies the subject:
  - *Tengo que repararlo.* — I have to repair it.

**2. Infinitive as subject of a sentence**
A Spanish infinitive can function as the grammatical subject of a clause, equivalent to English gerund or "to + verb" constructions:

- *Repararlo va a ser caro.* — Repairing it is going to be expensive. / To repair it is going to be expensive.
- *Caminar a las tiendas es fácil.* — Walking to the stores is easy.
- *Viajar en enero no es tan caro.* — Traveling in January isn't as expensive.

**3. Preterite of *pedir* (e→i stem-change in preterite)**
*Pedir* is a stem-changing -ir verb. In the preterite, the e→i change applies to the 3rd person singular and plural (not yo/tú/nosotros/vosotros):

| | Preterite |
|---|---|
| yo | **pedí** |
| tú | **pediste** |
| él/ella/Ud. | **pidió** |
| nosotros | **pedimos** |
| vosotros | **pedisteis** |
| ellos/Uds. | **pidieron** |

Lesson drills: *¿Pediste la lista? / La pedí ayer. / Pedí la especialidad de la casa.*

**4. Preterite of *ir* — *fui***
*Ir* and *ser* share identical preterite forms (context disambiguates):

- *Ayer fui a Isote.* — Yesterday I went to Isote.
- *Fue cara, pero deliciosa.* — It was expensive, but delicious. (*ser* preterite)

**5. Direct object pronoun attached to infinitive**
When a direct object pronoun goes with an infinitive, it can attach to the end of the infinitive or precede the conjugated verb:

- *Voy a pedirla inmediatamente.* = *La voy a pedir inmediatamente.* — I'm going to request it immediately.
- *Hay que repararlo.* — It has to be repaired. (pronoun attached to infinitive only, since *hay que* is impersonal)
- *La necesito inmediatamente.* — I need it immediately. (pronoun precedes conjugated verb)

**6. *quiénes* vs. *quién* (plural interrogative)**
When asking "who" about multiple people, use *quiénes*:

- *No sé quiénes más vienen.* — I don't know who else is coming. (several unknowns)
- *No sé quién es.* — I don't know who it is. (one person)

**7. Comparison: *tan … como* (as … as)**
- *Tan + adjective/adverb + como* = "as … as"
- *Viajar en enero no es tan caro como en el verano.* — Traveling in January isn't as expensive as in the summer.

**8. *gente* — singular collective noun**
*Gente* (people) is grammatically singular and takes singular verb agreement:

- *Mucha gente viene.* — Many people are coming. (NOT *vienen*)

## Summary

- **Core grammar** — Two new obligation structures: *hay que* + inf. (impersonal: "one must") vs. *tener que* + inf. (personal: "I/you/etc. must"). Use *hay que* when no subject is specified.
- **Infinitive as subject** — Spanish freely uses the infinitive as a noun (*repararlo, caminar, viajar*) where English uses a gerund or "to + verb."
- **Preterite of *pedir*** — Key e→i stem-change in 3rd person preterite (*pidió, pidieron*); yo/tú follow regular -ir preterite endings (*pedí, pediste*). Drilled heavily: *la pedí ayer, pediste la lista*.
- **Preterite of *ir*** — *fui/fuiste/fue/fuimos/fuisteis/fueron*; shares identical forms with *ser* (context disambiguates). Introduced at lesson's end: *ayer fui a Isote.*
- **Object pronoun placement with infinitives** — Pronoun attaches to end of infinitive (*pedirla, repararlo*) or moves before the conjugated verb (*la voy a pedir*); both are correct.
- **Collective noun *gente*** — Always singular, takes singular verb: *mucha gente viene* (not *vienen*).
- **quiénes vs. quién** — Use plural *quiénes* when asking about multiple unknown people: *no sé quiénes más vienen*.
- **Key vocab** — *el lapicero* (pen), *el estacionamiento* (parking garage), *los invitados* (guests), *los ingenieros* (engineers), *inmediatamente*, *a propósito* (by the way), *la especialidad de la casa*.
