## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *tanto/a/os/as* — "so much / so many"**
Introduced explicitly in the lesson intro. Agrees in gender and number with the noun it modifies:

| Form | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *tanto* | before masculine singular noun | *Hay tanto espacio.* |
| *tanta* | before feminine singular noun | *Hay tanta gente.* |
| *tantos* | before masculine plural noun | *Tantos problemas.* |
| *tantas* | before feminine plural noun | *Hay tantas flores.* |

**2. *se me olvidó* — the "involuntary se" construction**
This construction expresses unintentional actions (forgetting, dropping, running out of). The thing forgotten is the grammatical subject:

- *Se me olvidó la dirección.* — I forgot the address. (Lit. "The address forgot itself to me.")
- *Se me olvidó mi celular.* — I forgot my cell phone.
- *Se me olvidó comprarla.* — I forgot to buy it.

The verb agrees with the forgotten item (singular → *olvidó*; plural → *olvidaron*).

**3. *dejar* in the preterite — "I left [something] somewhere"**
Distinct from *olvidar* (to forget) — *dejar* implies intentionally or inadvertently placing something:

- *Dejé mi tarjeta en casa.* — I left my card at home.
- *Lo dejé en el carro.* — I left it in the car.
- *Lo dejé en la mesa.* — I left it on the table.

**4. *jugar a* + sport**
*Jugar* (u→ue stem-change) always takes *a* before a sport, which contracts with *el* to *al*:

- *Juego al tenis.* — I play tennis.
- *Juego al golf.* — I play golf.
- *Practica ella algún deporte?* — Does she practice any sport?

**5. Frequency adverbs: *de vez en cuando* and *rara vez***
- *De vez en cuando juego al golf.* — From time to time I play golf.
- *Rara vez tengo tiempo.* — I rarely have time.
- *Rara vez tengo tiempo para jugar al golf.* — I rarely have time to play golf.

**6. Reflexive direct object pronouns with infinitives**
When *perderse* takes a direct object, the pronoun attaches to the infinitive and must agree:

- *No quiero perdérmela.* — I don't want to miss it (fem.: *la película*).
- *No deberías perdértelo.* — You shouldn't miss it (masc.: *Xochimilco*).

## Verb Conjugations

### jugar — to play (a sport/game)
**Type:** stem-changing (u→ue) — stem changes in all forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **juego** | nosotros: jugamos |
| 2nd | tú: **juegas** | vosotros: jugáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **juega** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **juegan** |

Lesson drills: *juego al tenis*, *juego al golf*, *juega al golf* (she plays golf).

## Summary

- **jugar** — stem-changing (u→ue); always *jugar a* + sport
- **practicar** — regular -ar (c→qu spelling change); *practicar un deporte*
- **costar** — stem-changing (o→ue); *¿cuánto cuesta?*
- **mostrar** — stem-changing (o→ue); *mostrarle* = to show him/her
- **enviar** — regular -ar with stress shift; *enviarles flores*
- **dejar** — regular -ar; preterite *dejé* = I left (behind)
- **traer** — irregular (*go*-verb, preterite *traje*); *voy a traerlo*
- **perder** — stem-changing (e→ie); *perderse* = to miss something
