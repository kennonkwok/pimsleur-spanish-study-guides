## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *criarse* — "to grow up" (reflexive)**
Used for where someone was raised/grew up:

- *Me crié en Colorado.* — I grew up in Colorado.
- *Se crió en Roma.* — She grew up in Rome.
- Preterite: *me crié, te criaste, se crió, nos criamos, se criaron*

**2. *conocerse* — "to meet each other" (reciprocal reflexive)**
The reciprocal reflexive expresses actions done to/with each other:

- *¿Dónde se conocieron?* — Where did you all meet each other?
- *Nos conocimos en la universidad.* — We met each other at the university.
- Uses 1st pl. (*nos*) and 3rd pl. (*se*) reflexive forms

**3. Imperfect for past habits and simultaneous background actions**
The imperfect describes what was ongoing or habitual in the past:

- *Ella estudiaba arquitectura.* — She was studying / used to study architecture.
- *Ambos estudiábamos arquitectura.* — We were both studying architecture.
- *Esquiaba cuando era más joven.* — I used to ski when I was younger.

**4. *me cayó* — involuntary *se* with *caer***
When something spills or falls on someone unintentionally, *caer* is used with an indirect object:

- *Me cayó vino en los pantalones.* — I spilled wine on the pants. (Lit. "Wine fell on me on the pants.")
- The subject is the thing that fell (*vino*), hence singular *cayó*.

**5. *se me rompieron* — involuntary *se* (plural)**
The involuntary *se* with *romper*; the verb agrees with the thing broken:

- *Se me rompieron los lentes.* — My glasses broke. (Lit. "The glasses broke themselves on me.")
- Plural subject (*lentes*) → plural verb (*rompieron*)
- Compare: *se me olvidó* (singular)

**6. Past counterfactual: *si no hubiera sido* + *habría* + past participle**
For hypotheticals about the past ("if it hadn't been…, I would have…"):

| Part | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *si* clause | *si* + past subjunctive (*hubiera/hubiese*) | *Si no hubiera sido tan caro* |
| Result clause | conditional perfect (*habría/habría*) + past participle | *Lo habría comprado.* |

- *Si no hubiera sido tan caro, lo habría comprado para mi madre.* — If it hadn't been so expensive, I would have bought it for my mother.
- *Si el florero no hubiera sido tan bonito, no lo habría comprado.* — If the vase hadn't been so pretty, I wouldn't have bought it.

## Verb Conjugations

### criarse — to grow up
**Type:** regular -ar, reflexive (accent in yo/tú/él/ellos forms: *cría, crías, cría, crían*)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me **crío** | nosotros: nos criamos |
| 2nd | tú: te **crías** | vosotros: os criáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: se **cría** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se **crían** |

Preterite: *me crié, te criaste, **se crió**, nos criamos, se criaron*
Lesson drills: *me crié en Colorado*, *se crió en Roma*, *mi esposo se crió en Valle Nevado*.

---

### conocer — to know (people/places) / to meet
**Type:** *go*-verb irregularity in yo (*conozco*); reciprocal reflexive *conocerse* = to meet each other

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **conozco** | nosotros: conocemos |
| 2nd | tú: conoces | vosotros: conocéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: conoce | ellos/ellas/Uds.: conocen |

Preterite: *conocí, conociste, conoció, conocimos, conocisteis, conocieron*
Lesson drills: *¿dónde se conocieron?*, *nos conocimos en la universidad*.

---

### lastimarse — to injure oneself
**Type:** regular -ar, reflexive

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me lastimo | nosotros: nos lastimamos |
| 2nd | tú: te lastimas | vosotros: os lastimáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: se lastima | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se lastiman |

Preterite: *me lastimé, te lastimaste, se lastimó, nos lastimamos…*
Lesson drills: *me lastimé la rodilla*, *me lastimé la rodilla muy gravemente*.

### caerse — to fall / to spill (on someone)
**Type:** irregular; *go*-verb yo (*me caigo*); irregular preterite (*caí*)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me **caigo** | nosotros: nos caemos |
| 2nd | tú: te caes | vosotros: os caéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: se cae | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se caen |

Preterite: *me caí, te caíste, se cayó, nos caímos, se cayeron*
Involuntary: *me cayó vino* = wine spilled on me (3rd sg. subject = *vino*)
Lesson drills: *hace unos años me caí*, *me cayó vino en los pantalones*.

---

### romper — to break
**Type:** regular -er (past participle irregular: *roto*)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: rompo | nosotros: rompemos |
| 2nd | tú: rompes | vosotros: rompéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: rompe | ellos/ellas/Uds.: rompen |

Past participle: *roto* (irregular)
Involuntary reflexive: *se me rompieron los lentes* — my glasses broke
Lesson drills: *se me rompieron los lentes*, *cuando me caí*.

---

### regatear — to bargain / haggle
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|

| 2nd | tú: **regateas** | vosotros: regateáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: regateas | ellos/ellas/Uds.: regatean |

Lesson drills: *quizás puedas regatear* (subjunctive *puedas* triggers infinitive).

## Summary

- **criarse** — regular -ar reflexive; preterite *me crié / se crió* = I/she grew up
- **conocer** — *go* yo (*conozco*); *conocerse* = to meet each other; *nos conocimos*
- **lastimarse** — regular -ar reflexive; *me lastimé la rodilla* = I injured my knee
- **caerse** — irregular (*caigo*, preterite *me caí*); involuntary: *me cayó vino* = wine spilled on me
- **romper** — regular -er; irregular past participle *roto*; *se me rompieron los lentes* = my glasses broke
- **regatear** — regular -ar; *quizás puedas regatear* = maybe you can bargain
