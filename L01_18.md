## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *Ser* vs. *Estar* — two ways to say "to be"**
This is one of the most important distinctions in Spanish. Both mean "to be" but are not interchangeable.

*Ser* is used for:
- Nationality and origin: *Somos norteamericanos.* — We are North Americans.
- Identity and permanent characteristics: *Soy norteamericano.* — I am North American.

*Estar* is used for:
- Location: *Estamos en México.* — We are in Mexico.
- Current/temporary states: *Estoy listo.* — I am ready.

Key contrast from this lesson:
- *Somos norteamericanos.* (identity — ser) ✓
- *Estamos en México.* (location — estar) ✓

**2. *Listo/a* — ready (adjective with gender agreement)**
*Listo* agrees with the gender of the subject:
- *Estoy listo.* — I'm ready. (male speaker)
- *Estoy lista.* — I'm ready. (female speaker)
- *Ya estamos listos.* — We're ready right now. (mixed or male group)

**3. *Ya* — right now / already**
*Ya* signals that something is happening immediately or is already the case.
- *Ya me voy.* — I'm leaving right now.
- *Ya nos vamos.* — We're leaving right now.
- *Ya estamos listos.* — We're ready now.

**4. Reflexive departure: *irse***
*Irse* (to leave/go away) uses reflexive pronouns that match the subject:
- *me voy* — I'm leaving
- *te vas* — you're leaving
- *se va* — he/she is leaving
- *nos vamos* — we're leaving
- *se van* — they are leaving

Contrast with *ir* (to go toward a destination): *Voy al restaurante.* vs. *Me voy.* (I'm leaving/heading out).

**5. *Ir a + infinitive* — near future**
Express a future action using the present tense of *ir* + *a* + infinitive:
- *Vamos a comer.* — We are going to eat.
- *Van a comer juntos.* — They are going to eat together.

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| State readiness (male/female) | *Estoy listo.* / *Estoy lista.* |
| Signal imminent departure | *Ya me voy.* / *Ya nos vamos.* |
| Distinguish ser vs. estar | *Somos norteamericanos.* / *Estamos en México.* |
| Ask how many people | *¿Cuántas personas tienes?* |
| State enough | *Hay suficiente tiempo.* |
| Ask if going alone | *¿Va solo?* |
| State going with someone | *Va con Valeria.* |
| Near future | *Vamos a comer.* |

### Formal vs. Informal
- *listo/lista* is used with *estar* — applies to any register
- *Ya nos vamos* is neutral register; works in casual and polite contexts

### Pronunciation Notes
- *Suficiente*: four syllables — su-fi-CIEN-te; the *ie* is a diphthong.
- *Estamos*: stress on second syllable — es-TA-mos.
- *Somos*: stress on first syllable — SO-mos.
- *Ya*: short, sharp /ja/ sound — not two syllables.
- *Listo/Lista*: stress on first syllable — LIS-to / LIS-ta.

## Verb Conjugations

### estar — to be (state, location, temporary condition)
**Type:** irregular

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **estoy** | nosotros: **estamos** |
| 2nd | tú: estás | vosotros: estáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: está | ellos/ellas/Uds.: están |

Note: all forms except *yo* carry accent marks.
Lesson 18 drills: *estoy listo/a*, *estamos en México*, *estamos listos*.

---

### ser — to be (identity, origin, permanent characteristics)
**Type:** irregular

| | Singular | Plural |

| 2nd | tú: eres | vosotros: sois |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: es | ellos/ellas/Uds.: son |

Lesson 18 drills: *somos norteamericanos*, *soy norteamericano/a*.

---

### ir — to go
**Type:** irregular

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: voy | nosotros: **vamos** |
| 2nd | tú: vas | vosotros: vais |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: va | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **van** |

Lesson 18 drills: *va* (he/she goes), *van* (they go), *vamos a comer* (we're going to eat).

### irse — to leave, to go away
**Type:** irregular (same conjugation as *ir*, with reflexive pronouns)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **me voy** | nosotros: **nos vamos** |
| 2nd | tú: te vas | vosotros: os vais |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: se va | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se van |

Lesson 18 drills: *me voy* (I'm leaving), *ya me voy* (I'm leaving right now), *nos vamos* (we're leaving), *ya nos vamos*.

### comer — to eat
**Type:** regular -er

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: como | nosotros: **comemos** |
| 2nd | tú: comes | vosotros: coméis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **come** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: comen |

Lesson 18 drills: *come carne* (he/she eats meat), *comemos carne* (we eat meat), *no comemos carne*.

## Summary
- **estar** — irregular; used for location and temporary states (*estamos en México*, *estoy listo*)
- **ser** — irregular; used for identity and origin (*somos norteamericanos*)
- **ir** — irregular; *vamos a + infinitive* = near future
- **irse** — reflexive departure; *me voy / nos vamos* = I'm leaving / we're leaving; *ya* intensifies immediacy
- **comer** — regular -er; *come* / *comemos*
