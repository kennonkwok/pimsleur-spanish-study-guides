## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Preterite of *ser/estar* — *fue***
The preterite (past tense) of both *ser* and *estar* is *fue* (he/she/it was). Context determines meaning.
- *El almuerzo fue delicioso.* — The lunch was delicious.
- *La comida fue tan deliciosa.* — The meal was so delicious.

**2. Present Perfect — *haber* + past participle**
Used for actions recently completed or connected to the present:
- *He comido demasiado.* — I have eaten too much.
- *No hemos terminado.* — We haven't finished.
- *Has terminado el trabajo.* — You've finished the work.
- *La lluvia ha parado.* — The rain has stopped.

Formation: present tense of *haber* + past participle (-ado / -ido)

| Subject | haber | Example |
|---|---|---|
| yo | he | *he comido* |
| tú | has | *has terminado* |
| él/ella/Ud. | ha | *ha cambiado* |
| nosotros | hemos | *no hemos terminado* |
| ellos/Uds. | han | *han llegado* |

**3. Conditional — *podría*, *deberías***
The conditional expresses "could," "would," or "should":
- *Yo podría ir en autobús.* — I could go by bus.
- *No deberías ir en moto.* — You shouldn't go by motorcycle.

**4. *Hay que* + infinitive (impersonal obligation)**
"One must / You have to" — no subject, general obligation:
- *Hay que ir al centro.* — One must go into town.

**5. Double object pronoun order — *me lo dijo***
When both indirect and direct object pronouns appear together, the indirect comes first:
- *Me lo dijo.* — He told it to me. (Lit. "To me it he said")
- *Me lo dijo ayer.* — He told me yesterday.
- *Me lo dijo por teléfono.* — He told me on the phone.

**6. *ni … ni* and *tampoco***
- *Ni a pie ni en autobús.* — Neither on foot nor by bus.
- *Y tampoco en moto.* — And not by motorcycle either.

**7. Infinitive + object clitic**
Object pronouns attach to infinitives:
- *Voy a llevarte.* — I'm going to take you.
- *Para comenzarlo y terminarlo.* — To start it and finish it.

## Verb Conjugations

### haber — auxiliary verb (present perfect)
**Type:** irregular auxiliary; used only in compound tenses in this context

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **he** | nosotros: **hemos** |
| 2nd | tú: **has** | vosotros: habéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **ha** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **han** |

Lesson drills: *he comido*, *has terminado*, *no hemos terminado*, *ha cambiado*.

## Summary
- **haber** — irregular auxiliary; present perfect = *haber* + past participle (*he comido*, *hemos terminado*)
- **poder** — stem-changing (o→ue); conditional *podría* = "could"
- **deber** — regular -er; conditional *debería* = "should"
- **llevar** — regular -ar; here used as "to take (someone somewhere)"
- **recordar** — stem-changing (o→ue); *acordarse* (reflexive) drilled as *¿te acuerdas?*
- **comenzar** — stem-changing (e→ie); "to start/begin"
- **regresar** — regular -ar; "to return"
