## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Pluperfect (past perfect): *haber* (imperfect) + past participle**
The pluperfect expresses an action that had already been completed before another past event. It is formed with the imperfect of *haber* + past participle.

| Imperfect of *haber* | |
|---|---|
| había | habíamos |
| habías | habíais |
| había | habían |

- *Había dejado mis tarjetas en casa.* — I had left my cards at home.
- *Las había dejado en un taxi.* — I had left them in a taxi.
- *Ya había visto la película.* — I had already seen the film.
- *Mi novio no la había visto.* — My boyfriend had not seen it.
- *No lo había visto antes.* — I had not seen it before.

The key lesson contrast: *había dejado* (pluperfect) vs. *dejé* (simple preterite). Use pluperfect when one past event precedes another past event.

**2. *ya no* — no longer**
*Ya* means "already" in affirmative contexts but combines with *no* to mean "no longer / not anymore":
- *Ya no tenía mi billetera.* — I no longer had my wallet. (I used to have it, then I didn't.)
- *Ya no tenía mis llaves.* — I no longer had my keys.

Compare: *Ya tenía mi billetera* = I already had my wallet.

**3. *así que* — so / therefore**
Connecting clause expressing consequence:
- *Así que estábamos decepcionados.* — So we were disappointed.

**4. *tener muchas ganas de* + infinitive — to look forward to / to be eager to**
*Ganas* (pl.) = desire, eagerness. The expression *tener ganas de* + inf. is a fixed idiom:
- *Tenemos muchas ganas de ir.* — We very much look forward to going.
- *Tengo muchas ganas de ir.* — I'm very eager to go.

**5. Subjunctive with *quizás* and *ojalá***
Both expressions of uncertainty/hope trigger the subjunctive:
- *Quizás haya mucho tráfico.* — There may be a lot of traffic. (*haya* = subjunctive of *haber*)
- *Ojalá que no llueva.* — Hopefully it doesn't rain. (*llueva* = subjunctive of *llover*)

**6. Gender agreement with *decepcionado/a***
The adjective changes to match the subject:
- *Estábamos decepcionados.* — We (m/mixed) were disappointed.
- *Estaba decepcionado.* — He was disappointed.
- *Estaba decepcionada.* — She (I, f) was disappointed.

**7. *preguntarle a* — to ask (someone)**
Spanish uses indirect object pronoun + *a* + noun for clarity:
- *Tengo que preguntarle a mi esposa.* — I have to ask my wife. (Lit. "I have to ask to her, to my wife.")

## Summary

- **Pluperfect** (*había + past participle*) is the central new structure: used to describe what had already happened before another past event. Key examples: *había dejado*, *había visto*.
- **ya no** = "no longer" — pairs with imperfect to describe a state that ceased: *ya no tenía mi billetera*.
- **dormir** — stem-changing -ir verb (o→ue/u); preterite *dormí* is the key drilled form.
- **ver** — irregular past participle *visto*; preterite nosotros *vimos* is heavily drilled.
- **ganar** — regular -ar; preterite 3rd person *ganó / ganaron* drilled for match results.
- **dejar** — regular -ar; regular past participle *dejado*; used exclusively in pluperfect this lesson.
- **estar** — imperfect *estaba / estábamos* used for emotional states (disappointment) in the past.
- **tener muchas ganas de** + inf. — idiomatic expression for eagerness; drilled repeatedly in final scenarios.
- **buenísimo** — absolute superlative exclamation introduced as the preferred word for "great" going forward.
- **quizás + subjunctive** and **ojalá que + subjunctive** — introduced for expressing uncertainty and hope; subjunctive forms (*haya*, *llueva*) not yet broken down but will recur.
