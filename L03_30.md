## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Direct object pronouns attached to infinitives**
When an infinitive has a direct object, the pronoun attaches to the end of the infinitive. The pronoun must agree in gender and number with the noun it replaces:

| Antecedent | Pronoun | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *las flores* (f pl) | *-las* | *Voy a ponerlas en la mesa.* |
| *la flor* (f sg) | *-la* | *Voy a ponerla en la mesa.* |
| *el vino* (m sg) | *-lo* | *Voy a traerlo.* |
| *la botella* (f sg) | *-la* | *Voy a traerla.* |

Note: An accent mark is often added when attaching a pronoun shifts stress: *ponerlas* (no shift needed), but *tráerla → traerla* (accent not needed here).

**2. *enfrente de* — "in front of"**
- *Tu carro está enfrente de la casa.* — Your car is in front of the house.
- Distinct from *delante de* (directly in front of, positional) and *frente a* (facing).

**3. Reported speech with *dijo que* + conditional**
*Decir* in the preterite introduces reported speech. If the main verb was advice, the conditional is used in the reported clause:

- *La vendedora dijo que debería comprar un CD.* — The saleswoman said I should buy a CD.

**4. *ojalá* + two subjunctive clauses**
*Ojalá* can govern multiple subjunctive verbs in a single sentence:

- *Ojalá puedas regresar pronto y podamos ir juntos.* — Hopefully you can return soon and we can go together.
  - *puedas* = 2nd sg. present subj. of *poder*
  - *podamos* = 1st pl. present subj. of *poder*

**5. Post-nominal possessives — *un amigo nuestro***
When a possessive is used as emphasis or with an indefinite article, it follows the noun:

- *Un amigo nuestro.* — A friend of ours. (emphasis: *ours*, not just any friend)
- *Un amigo mío.* — A friend of mine.
- Compare: *nuestro amigo* (the friend we've mentioned) vs. *un amigo nuestro* (one of our friends).

**6. *¿qué...?* as an exclamation**
*Qué* + noun or adjective forms an exclamation:
- *¡Qué suerte!* — What luck!
- *¡Qué buena despedida!* — What a great send-off!
- *¡Qué lindas flores!* — What lovely flowers!

## Verb Conjugations

### poner — to put / to place
**Type:** irregular (*go*-verb yo: *pongo*); reflexive *ponerse* = to put on (clothing)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **pongo** | nosotros: ponemos |
| 2nd | tú: pones | vosotros: ponéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: pone | ellos/ellas/Uds.: ponen |

Direct object pronoun attachment: *poner + las → ponerlas*, *poner + la → ponerla*, *poner + lo → ponerlo*
Lesson drills: *voy a poner las flores en la mesa*, *voy a ponerlas en la mesa*, *¿dónde debería poner el vino?*

---

### decir — to say / to tell
**Type:** highly irregular (*go*-verb yo: *digo*; stem-changing e→i; irregular preterite *dij-*)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **digo** | nosotros: decimos |
| 2nd | tú: **dices** | vosotros: decís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **dice** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **dicen** |

Preterite: *dije, dijiste, **dijo**, dijimos, dijisteis, dijeron*
Lesson drills: *la vendedora dijo que debería comprar un CD*.

---

### llegar — to arrive
**Type:** regular -ar (spelling change g→gu before e: preterite yo *llegué*)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: llego | nosotros: llegamos |
| 2nd | tú: llegas | vosotros: llegáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **llega** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: llegan |

Preterite (1st sg.): *llegué*
Lesson drills: *llegué demasiado temprano*, *la gente normalmente llega un poquito tarde*, *llegaste exactamente bien*.

## Summary

- **poner** — irregular (*go* yo: *pongo*); direct object pronouns attach to infinitive: *ponerlas*, *ponerla*, *ponerlo*
- **decir** — highly irregular (*go* yo: *digo*; e→i; preterite *dijo*); *la vendedora dijo que...*
- **llegar** — regular -ar (g→gu in preterite yo: *llegué*); *llegué demasiado temprano*
- **poder** — stem-changing (o→ue); subjunctive *puedas/podamos* after *ojalá*
