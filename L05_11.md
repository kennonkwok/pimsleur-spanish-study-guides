## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *si tuviera* — present counterfactual with *tener***
*Tuviera* is the imperfect subjunctive of *tener*, used in present/future counterfactual *si* clauses:

- *Me quedaría una semana más si tuviera tiempo.* — I would stay a week longer if I had time.
- *Si tuviera mis zapatos para caminar aquí, podría caminar más.* — If I had my walking shoes here, I could walk more.
- Pattern: *si* + imperfect subjunctive (*tuviera*) + conditional (*quedaría/podría*)

| Tener — Imperfect Subjunctive |
|---|
| *tuviera / tuvieras / tuviera / tuviéramos / tuvierais / tuvieran* |

**2. *no debí/debía haber* + past participle — "I should not have…"**
To express past obligation not fulfilled, use *deber* in past tense + *haber* + past participle:

| Form | Example | Nuance |
|---|---|---|
| *debí haber comido menos* | I should have eaten less | preterite *deber* — more definite past judgment |
| *no debía haber comido tanto* | I should not have eaten so much | imperfect *deber* — slightly more reflective/regretful |
| *no debía haberlos comprado* | I should not have bought them | pronouns attach to infinitive *haberlos* |

- Compare to past counterfactual: *si no hubiera comido tanto* (hypothetical) vs. *no debí haber comido tanto* (expression of regret/obligation).

**3. *se necesitan* — impersonal plural ("one needs / are needed")**
When the thing needed is plural, the impersonal *se* verb takes the plural form:

- *Se necesitan zapatos cómodos.* — One needs comfortable shoes. / Comfortable shoes are needed.
- Compare (singular): *Se necesita una reservación.* — A reservation is needed.
- The verb agrees with the noun following it, not with a hidden subject.

**4. *buzón de voz* and voicemail expressions**
- *Me contestó su buzón de voz.* — Her voicemail answered me. (lit. "her voicemail answered me")
- *Le dejé un mensaje.* — I left him/her a message.
- *Llámeme, por favor.* — Call me, please. (formal)
- *Habla [name].* — [Name] speaking. (standard phone self-identification, name last)

**5. *desde* — "from" (place or starting point)**
- *Puedes trabajar desde el hotel.* — You can work from the hotel.
- *Si no puedes trabajar desde el hotel…* — If you can't work from the hotel…
- *Desde* = from a fixed reference point (contrast *de*, which is more general).

## Verb Conjugations

### quedarse — to stay / to remain
**Type:** regular -ar, reflexive

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me quedo | nosotros: nos quedamos |
| 2nd | tú: te quedas | vosotros: os quedáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: se queda | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se quedan |

Conditional: *me quedaría, te quedarías, se quedaría, nos quedaríamos…*
Lesson drills: *me quedaría una semana más si tuviera tiempo*.

---

### contestar — to answer
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: contesto | nosotros: contestamos |
| 2nd | tú: contestas | vosotros: contestáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **contesta** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: contestan |

Preterite: *contesté, contestaste, **contestó**, contestamos…*
Lesson drills: *me contestó su buzón de voz* — her voicemail answered me.

---

### dejar — to leave / to let
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: dejo | nosotros: dejamos |
| 2nd | tú: dejas | vosotros: dejáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: deja | ellos/ellas/Uds.: dejan |

Preterite: *dejé, dejaste, dejó, dejamos…*

### tener — to have
**Type:** irregular; imperfect subjunctive drilled in this lesson

Imperfect subjunctive: *tuviera, tuvieras, tuviera, tuviéramos, tuvierais, tuvieran*
Lesson drills: *si tuviera tiempo*, *si tuviera mis zapatos para caminar aquí*.

## Summary

- **quedarse** — regular -ar reflexive; conditional *me quedaría* = I would stay; *si tuviera* (past subj.) in *si* clause
- **contestar** — regular -ar; *me contestó su buzón de voz* = her voicemail answered me
- **dejar** — regular -ar; *le dejé un mensaje* = I left him a message
- **tener** — irregular; imperfect subjunctive *tuviera* in present counterfactual *si* clause
- **no debí/debía haber** + past participle = I should not have done; pronouns attach to *haber*: *haberlos comprado*
- **se necesitan** = one needs / are needed (plural); verb agrees with the noun
- **desde** = from (a fixed point); *trabajar desde el hotel* = work from the hotel
