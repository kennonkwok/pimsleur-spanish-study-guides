## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *tener muchas ganas de* + infinitive — "really want to / can't wait to"**
*Tener ganas de* expresses eagerness or desire. *Muchas* intensifies it:

- *Tengo muchas ganas de irme de vacaciones.* — I can't wait to go on vacation.
- *Tengo ganas de comer.* — I feel like eating.
- *No tengo ganas de trabajar.* — I don't feel like working.

**2. *se ve* vs. *luce* — "it looks"**
Two ways to express appearance:

| Expression | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *se ve* | reflexive *verse* | *La casa se ve muy bonita.* — The house looks very nice. |
| *luce* | *lucir* (3rd sg.) | *Luce muy bonita.* — It looks very nice. |

Both are common. *Se ve* is used more broadly; *lucir* is slightly more elevated.

**3. *aunque* + subjunctive vs. indicative (explicit contrast)**
This lesson explicitly drills the distinction:

| Mood | Interpretation | When to use | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Subjunctive (*llueva*) | "even if" — uncertain/possible | speaker doesn't know for sure | *Aunque llueva mucho, vamos a pasarla bien.* |
| Indicative (*llueve*) | "although/even though" — accepted fact | speaker treats it as certain | *Aunque llueve mucho en junio, vamos.* |

**4. *si no hubiera hecho tanto calor* — past counterfactual with weather**
Weather in Spanish uses *hacer* (to make/do), so the past counterfactual uses *hubiera hecho*:

- *Nos habría ido mejor si no hubiera hecho tanto calor.* — It would have gone better for us if it hadn't been so hot.
- *hubiera hecho* = past subjunctive of *haber* + past participle of *hacer* (*hecho*)
- Compare: *si no hubiera sido tan caro* (ser) — the structure is the same

**5. *irle (bien/mal)* — impersonal "to go (well/badly) for someone"**
*Irle* expresses how things are going for a person:

- *Me fue bien.* — It went well for me.
- *Nos habría ido mejor.* — It would have gone better for us.
- *¿Cómo te va?* — How's it going for you?
- Pattern: IO pronoun (*me/te/le/nos*) + *ir* in relevant tense

**6. Weather with *hacer* — tense review**

| Tense | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Present | *hace calor* | *Hoy hace calor.* |
| Imperfect | *hacía calor* | *Hacía demasiado calor.* (ongoing past) |
| Preterite | *hizo calor* | *Ese día hizo mucho calor.* (bounded event) |
| Conditional perf. | *habría hecho calor* | *Habría hecho calor.* |
| Past subjunctive | *hubiera hecho* | *Si no hubiera hecho tanto calor…* |

## Verb Conjugations

### lucir — to look / to shine
**Type:** irregular yo (*luzco*); otherwise regular -ir pattern

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **luzco** | nosotros: lucimos |
| 2nd | tú: luces | vosotros: lucís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **luce** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: lucen |

Lesson drills: *luce muy bonita*, *el hotel se ve muy bonito* (alternative with *verse*).

---

### hacer — to do / to make (weather)
**Type:** highly irregular; weather use is impersonal (3rd sg. only)

Weather forms drilled:

| Tense | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Imperfect | *hacía* | *Hacía demasiado calor.* |
| Preterite | *hizo* | *Hizo mucho calor ese día.* |
| Past subjunctive | *hubiera hecho* | *Si no hubiera hecho tanto calor.* |

Lesson drills: *hacía demasiado calor*, *si no hubiera hecho tanto calor, nos habría ido mejor*.

---

### quedar(se) — to stay / remain
**Type:** regular -ar, reflexive

Preterite (drilled): *me quedé, te quedaste, se quedó, **nos quedamos**, se quedaron*
Lesson drills: *nos quedamos en Villas Eco Arenal*, *vamos a quedarnos en ese hotel*.

## Summary

- **lucir** — irregular yo (*luzco*); *luce muy bonita* = it looks very nice; synonymous with *se ve*
- **hacer** (weather) — impersonal; imperfect *hacía calor*; past subj. *hubiera hecho*
- **quedarse** — regular -ar reflexive; preterite *nos quedamos* = we stayed
- ***tener muchas ganas de*** + inf. = really want to / can't wait to
- ***se ve*** = it looks (reflexive *verse*); interchangeable with *luce* for appearance
- ***aunque*** + subjunctive = "even if" (uncertain); + indicative = "although/even though" (fact)
- ***nos habría ido mejor*** = it would have gone better for us (*irle* = to go for someone)
- ***si no hubiera hecho tanto calor*** — past counterfactual with weather *hacer*
