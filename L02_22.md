## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Preterite of *decir*: *dijo* / *me dijo* ("said" / "told me")**
*Decir* (to say/tell) has an irregular preterite with a *j*-stem.

- *El hombre dijo que funciona bien.* — The man said that it works well.
- *El hombre me dijo eso.* — The man told me that.
- *Me dijo eso hace unos minutos.* — He told me that a few minutes ago.

The complete preterite of *decir*:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | dije | dijimos |
| 2nd | dijiste | dijisteis |
| 3rd | **dijo** | dijeron |

**2. *jugar* — stem-changing (u→ue)**
*Jugar* is unique: it's the only common verb with a u→ue stem change (not o→ue). The stem changes in all forms except *nosotros/vosotros*.

- *Él/ella juega tenis.* — He/she plays tennis.
- *¿Juega usted tenis?* — Do you (formal) play tennis?
- *¿Tú juegas?* — Do you play?
- *Jugamos juntos.* — We play together. (no stem change)

**3. Direct object pronouns: *lo*, *la*, *los*, *las***
A pronoun replaces the direct object noun. It matches the gender of the noun replaced.

- *Lo terminó.* — He finished it. (referring to masculine *el carro*)
- *No puedo encontrarlo.* — I can't find it. (attached to infinitive)
- *Saberla.* — To know it. (referring to feminine *la dirección*)

**4. *desde* + time point — "since"**
*Desde* marks the starting point of an ongoing action.

- *Vivo aquí desde febrero.* — I've lived here since February.
- *Desde dos meses.* — For two months (since two months ago).

**5. *saber* vs. *conocer* — two verbs meaning "to know"**
| Verb | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *saber* | to know facts, information, how to | *Debería saberla.* (the address) |
| *conocer* | to know people, places (be acquainted with) | *¿Conoces a María Pérez?* (L23 preview) |

Both have irregular yo forms: *sé* (saber) / *conozco* (conocer).

**6. *tuve suerte* — "I was lucky" (lit. "I had luck")**
Spanish expresses "to be lucky" as *tener suerte* (to have luck).

- *Tuve suerte.* — I was lucky. (preterite = specific past event)
- *¡Qué suerte!* — What luck!
- *¡Buena suerte!* — Good luck!

## Verb Conjugations

### funcionar — to work / to function
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: funciono | nosotros: funcionamos |
| 2nd | tú: funcionas | vosotros: funcionáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **funciona** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: funcionan |

Lesson drills: *no funciona*, *funciona bien*, *no funciona bien*, *funciona bien ahora*.

---

### jugar — to play (sport/game)
**Type:** stem-changing (u→ue) — changes in all forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **juego** | nosotros: jugamos |
| 2nd | tú: **juegas** | vosotros: jugáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **juega** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **juegan** |

Lesson drills: *juega tenis*, *¿juega tenis?*, *tú juegas*, *no juego bien*, *me gusta jugar voleibol*, *prefiero jugar voleibol*.

---

### saber — to know (facts/information)
**Type:** irregular (yo: *sé*; otherwise regular -er)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **sé** | nosotros: sabemos |
| 2nd | tú: sabes | vosotros: sabéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: sabe | ellos/ellas/Uds.: saben |

Lesson drills: *debería saberla*, *alguien tiene que saberlo*.

---

## Summary

- **funcionar** — regular -ar; key new verb: "to work/function" — drilled for car and cell phone contexts
- **jugar** — stem-changing (u→ue); *juego/juegas/juega/juegan* — sports vocabulary: tennis and volleyball
- **saber** — irregular yo (*sé*); to know facts; contrasted conceptually with *conocer*
- **decir** (preterite) — irregular *dij-* stem: *dijo* / *me dijo* — reported speech pattern introduced
