## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Days of the week as time expressions**
In Spanish, days of the week are not capitalized and use the definite article for specific day references:

| Expression | Meaning |
|---|---|
| *el sábado* | on Saturday (specific) |
| *el lunes* | on Monday (specific) |
| *antes del lunes* | before Monday |
| *el sábado en la noche* | on Saturday evening |

No preposition is needed — the article carries the meaning of "on."

**2. *saber* — to know a fact**
*Saber* is used for knowing facts, information, or how to do something. It is irregular only in *yo*:

- *No sé si puedo.* — I don't know if I can.
- *No sé si tengo tiempo.* — I don't know if I have time.

Contrast with *conocer* (to know a person/place, to be acquainted with).

**3. *comenzar* (e→ie) — to start/begin**
Stem-changing verb; *e* changes to *ie* in all forms except *nosotros/vosotros*:

- *La película comienza a las siete.* — The film starts at seven.
- *Comienza temprano.* — It starts early.

**4. *terminar* — to end**
Regular -ar; used alongside *comenzar* in the film scenario:

- *¿A qué hora termina?* — At what time does it end?
- *La película termina demasiado tarde.* — The film ends too late.

**5. No hay tiempo para + infinitive**
*No hay* (there isn't) + *tiempo* (time) + *para* (in order to) + infinitive:

- *No hay tiempo para verla.* — There's no time to see it.

**6. *me ha dicho que* — present perfect of *decir***
*Decir* has an irregular past participle: *dicho* (not *decido*).

- *Mi padre me ha dicho que es muy buena.* — My father has told me it's very good.
- *Mi hija me ha dicho que quiere un sombrero bonito.* — My daughter has told me she wants a pretty hat.

## Verb Conjugations

### saber — to know (a fact)
**Type:** irregular (*yo* only: *sé*)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **sé** | nosotros: sabemos |
| 2nd | tú: sabes | vosotros: sabéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **sabe** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: saben |

Lesson drills: *no sé si puedo*, *no sé si tengo tiempo*, *sabe, señor* (shopkeeper using 3rd-person formal).

---

### comenzar — to start / to begin
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie) — changes in all forms except nosotros/vosotros; also spelling change *z→c* before *e* in preterite

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **comienzo** | nosotros: comenzamos |
| 2nd | tú: **comienzas** | vosotros: comenzáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **comienza** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **comienzan** |

Lesson drills: *la película comienza a las siete*, *comienza temprano*, *si la película comienza temprano*.

---

### terminar — to end / to finish
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: termino | nosotros: terminamos |
| 2nd | tú: terminas | vosotros: termináis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **termina** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: terminan |

Lesson drills: *¿a qué hora termina?*, *la película termina demasiado tarde*.

---

### decir — to say / to tell
**Type:** irregular (stem-changing e→i; *go*-verb in yo; irregular past participle *dicho*)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **digo** | nosotros: decimos |
| 2nd | tú: **dices** | vosotros: decís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **dice** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **dicen** |

Irregular past participle: **dicho**

Lesson drills: *mi padre me ha dicho que*, *mi hija me ha dicho que*, *¿cómo se dice?* (ongoing formula), *no me digas* (introduced later in lessons).

## Summary
- **saber** — irregular (*yo sé*); "to know a fact/if"; contrasts with *conocer*
- **comenzar** — stem-changing (e→ie); "to start"; used for film/event times
- **terminar** — regular -ar; "to end"; used alongside *comenzar*
- **decir** — irregular (*digo*, e→i, irreg. p.p. *dicho*); present perfect *ha dicho* drilled
