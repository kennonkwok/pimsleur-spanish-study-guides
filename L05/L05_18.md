## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *cuando* + present subjunctive — "when" + future event**
When *cuando* refers to a future event (not a present habit or past fact), the verb that follows uses the present subjunctive:

| Context | Mood | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Future ("when I retire someday") | Present subjunctive | *cuando me **jubile*** |
| Habit ("when I retire every year...") | Indicative | *cuando me jubilo* (hypothetical habit) |

- *Me gustaría viajar cuando me jubile.* — I'd like to travel when I retire.
- *¿Qué va a hacer cuando se jubile?* — What are you going to do when you retire?
- *No sé lo que voy a hacer cuando me jubile.* — I don't know what I'm going to do when I retire.

**2. *quizás* + present subjunctive — expressing possibility**
*Quizás* (maybe/perhaps) can take either subjunctive (more uncertain) or indicative (more certain):

- *Quizás me vaya a vivir cerca del mar.* — Maybe I'll go off to live by the sea. (subjunctive — uncertain)
- *vaya* = present subjunctive of *ir* (1st sg.)
- Compare *quizás puedas* (L5/8), *quizás pueda* (L5/13)

**3. *lo que* — "what" as a relative pronoun**
*Lo que* = "the thing that" / "what" in embedded clauses:

- *No sé lo que voy a hacer.* — I don't know what I'm going to do.
- *No sé lo que voy a hacer cuando me jubile.* — I don't know what I'm going to do when I retire.
- Compare: *¿Qué vas a hacer?* (direct question) vs. *No sé lo que vas a hacer.* (indirect/embedded)

**4. *he estado pensando* — present perfect progressive**
Formed with *haber* (present) + *estado* + present participle (-ndo):

- *He estado pensando en lo que voy a hacer cuando me jubile.* — I've been thinking about what I'm going to do when I retire.
- *He estado pensando en Belice.* — I've been thinking about Belize.
- Emphasizes ongoing/continuous action that started in the past and continues to the present.
- Compare present perfect: *He pensado* = I've thought (completed); *He estado pensando* = I've been thinking (ongoing)

**5. *lo que* in embedded clauses vs. *qué* in direct questions**

| Type | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Direct question | *¿qué?* | *¿Qué vas a hacer?* |
| Embedded (after *no sé*, *me pregunto*…) | *lo que* | *No sé lo que vas a hacer.* |

**6. *se enfermó del estómago* — preterite of *enfermarse* + body part**
*Enfermarse de* + body part = to get sick with (that body part):
- *Se enfermó del estómago.* — She got an upset stomach.
- *Se enfermó.* — She got sick.
- *estar enfermo* = to be sick (state); *enfermarse* = to get sick (change of state)

## Verb Conjugations

### enfermarse — to get sick
**Type:** regular -ar reflexive

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me enfermo | nosotros: nos enfermamos |
| 2nd | tú: te enfermas | vosotros: os enfermáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: se enferma | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se enferman |

Preterite: *me enfermé, te enfermaste, **se enfermó**, nos enfermamos…*
Lesson drills: *mi esposa se enfermó del estómago*, *mi esposo se enfermó*.

### ir — to go
**Type:** highly irregular; present subjunctive *vaya* drilled in this lesson

Present subjunctive: *vaya, vayas, vaya, vayamos, vayáis, vayan*
Lesson drills: *quizás me vaya a vivir cerca del mar* — maybe I'll go off to live near the sea.

---

### pensar — to think
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **pienso** | nosotros: pensamos |
| 2nd | tú: **piensas** | vosotros: pensáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **piensa** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **piensan** |

Present perfect: *he pensado*
Present perfect progressive: *he estado pensando*
Lesson drills: *no lo he pensado*, *he estado pensando en lo que voy a hacer*.

## Summary

- **enfermarse** — regular -ar reflexive; preterite *se enfermó del estómago* = got an upset stomach
- **explorar** — regular -ar; *tuve que explorar* = I had to explore
- **ir** — irregular; present subjunctive *vaya*; *quizás me vaya a vivir* = maybe I'll go off to live
- **pensar** — stem-changing (e→ie); *he estado pensando en* = I've been thinking about
- ***cuando* + present subjunctive** = "when" + future event: *cuando me jubile* = when I retire
- ***lo que*** = what (relative pronoun): *no sé lo que voy a hacer* = I don't know what I'm going to do
- ***he estado* + -ndo** = present perfect progressive: I've been doing; emphasizes ongoing action
- ***jubilado/a*** = retired (adjective); *muchos norteamericanos jubilados* = many retired Americans
