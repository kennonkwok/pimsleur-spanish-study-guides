## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Reflexive preterite of *criarse* — "I/she/we grew up"**
*Criarse* is the reflexive of *criar* (to raise). In the preterite, reflexive pronouns precede the verb:

- *Me crié.* — I grew up.
- *Se crio.* — She/he grew up.
- *Nos criamos.* — We grew up.

Note: *me crié* carries a written accent on the final *e* to mark the preterite stress. *Se crio* does not need one (the natural stress falls on the last syllable).

**2. Reciprocal reflexive *conocerse* — "we/they met each other"**
*Conocerse* expresses a mutual action. The preterite is:

- *Nos conocimos en la universidad.* — We met each other at the university.
- *¿Dónde se conocieron?* — Where did you (plural) / they meet each other?

**3. Impersonal reflexive for accidents: *se me olvidó*, *me caí*, *se cayó***
Spanish uses reflexive constructions to express unintended actions:

- *Se me olvidó.* — I forgot it. (Lit. "It forgot itself to me.") — takes the IO pronoun of the person
- *Me caí.* — I fell.
- *Se cayó.* — She/he fell.
- *Me lastimé la rodilla.* — I injured my knee.

**4. Conditional: *compraría* — "I would buy"**
The conditional is formed by adding endings to the full infinitive (-ía, -ías, -ía, -íamos, -íais, -ían):

- *Lo compraría.* — I would buy it.
- *Si no fuera tan caro, lo compraría.* — If it weren't so expensive, I would buy it.

**5. Conditional perfect: *habría venido*, *habría votado***
*Habría* + past participle = "would have [done]":

- *Habría venido conmigo.* — She would have come with me.
- *Si fuera norteamericana, también habría votado por él.* — If I were American, I would have voted for him too.

**6. *Ojalá* + subjunctive**
*Ojalá* expresses hope and always triggers the subjunctive:

- *Ojalá mi esposa pueda venir la semana entrante.* — Hopefully my wife can come next week.

**7. *luego* vs. *entonces* for sequencing**
Both mean "then" but in different senses:
- *Luego* = then (next in a series of events)
- *Entonces* = then (at that point in time / therefore)
- *Primero fui a Pueblo Viejo. Luego fui a la iglesia.* — First I went to Pueblo Viejo. Then I went to the church.

**Type:** regular -ar (spelling change: *í* accent in yo/él preterite due to hiatus)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|

| | Singular | Plural |

## Summary

- **criarse** — regular -ar reflexive; preterite *me crié / se crio*; used for "grew up (was raised)"
- **conocerse** — irregular yo present (*conozco*); preterite reciprocal reflexive *nos conocimos / se conocieron* = "we/they met each other"
- **caerse** — irregular (*caigo*, preterite *caí/cayó*); reflexive fall: *me caí*, *se cayó*
- **lastimarse** — regular -ar reflexive; *me lastimé la rodilla* = I injured my knee
- **votar** — regular -ar; *votar por* = to vote for; preterite *voté*
- **comprar** — regular -ar; conditional *compraría* = I would buy
