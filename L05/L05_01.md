## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Estar + gerund (present progressive)**
To express an action in progress right now, use *estar* (conjugated) + gerund (-ando/-iendo):

- *Ahora mismo está viendo fútbol en la tele.* — Right now he's watching soccer on TV.
- *¿En qué hotel se está quedando?* — What hotel are you staying at? (lit. "in which hotel are you staying?")
- Gerund formation: *ver → viendo*, *quedar → quedando*, *comprar → comprando*

**2. Quizás + subjunctive**
*Quizás* (maybe/perhaps) may take the subjunctive when the speaker intends uncertainty:

- *Quizás haya algo para ella también.* — There may be something for her too.
- *Haya* = present subjunctive of *haber* (to have/there to be).
- When certainty is implied, indicative is also possible, but subjunctive is more common in speech.

**3. Word order with multiple adjectives**
When two descriptive adjectives modify a noun, one often precedes (subjective/affective) and one follows (objective/classifying):

- *Una bonita bufanda roja.* — A pretty red scarf. (*bonita* precedes; *roja* follows)
- *Mi traje de baño nuevo.* vs. *Mi nuevo traje de baño.* — *nuevo* can go before or after; before = replacement connotation.

**4. Infinitives with clitic pronouns**
Object pronouns can attach to an infinitive as a suffix:

- *Voy a pensarlo.* — I'm going to think about it. (*lo* = it)
- *Puedo ayudarte.* — I can help you. (*te* = you)
- *Para comprar regalos.* — In order to buy gifts. (*para* + infinitive = in order to)

**5. Omitting the definite article with country names**
In informal speech, Spanish speakers often drop *los* before *Estados Unidos*:

- *El equipo de Estados Unidos.* — The US team. (not *los Estados Unidos* in this context)

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|

| Say someone likes to do something | *A mi hija le gusta nadar.* |

| Ask about clothing size | *¿De qué talla?* |

## Verb Conjugations

### ver — to see / to watch
**Type:** irregular

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **veo** | nosotros: vemos |
| 2nd | tú: **ves** | vosotros: veis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **ve** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: ven |

Lesson drills: *lo ve en la tele*, *lo veo de vez en cuando*, *mi hijo ve fútbol conmigo*, *el menor también ve mucho fútbol*.

---

### pescar — to fish
**Type:** regular -ar (spelling change c→qu before *e* in preterite only; present is fully regular)

| | Singular | Plural |

| 2nd | tú: pescas | vosotros: pescáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: pesca | ellos/ellas/Uds.: pescan |

Lesson drills: *le gusta pescar*, *tú puedes pescar*, *me gusta pescar y nadar*.

## Summary

- **ver** — irregular; key verb for watching TV/matches: *ve*, *veo*, *vemos*; also used with *estar viendo* (progressive)
- **pescar** — regular -ar; used in infinitive with *gustar* and *poder* constructions
- **nadar** — regular -ar; used in infinitive with *gustar* and *poder* constructions
- **pensar** — stem-changing (e→ie); lesson uses it as infinitive via *voy a pensarlo*
- **decidir** — regular -ir; used as infinitive: *puedes decidir*
