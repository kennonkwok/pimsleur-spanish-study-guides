## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *qué pena con usted* — "how embarrassing" (formal apology)**
*La pena* has several meanings depending on context:
- *¡Qué pena con usted!* — How embarrassing! (formal apology for an awkward situation)
- *¡Qué pena!* — What a shame! / How unfortunate!
- *vale la pena* — it's worthwhile (from L5/13)
- *dar pena* — to make someone feel sorry/embarrassed

**2. *le parece bien* — *parecer* used like *gustar***
*Parecer* (to seem) follows the same pattern as *gustar*: the thing that seems = grammatical subject, the person to whom it seems = indirect object:

- *¿Le parece bien?* — Does it seem fine to you? / Is that okay with you?
- *Me parece bien.* — It seems fine to me. / That works for me.
- *¿Te parece?* — Does that seem okay? (informal)
- *Parece que…* — It seems that… (impersonal, no IO needed)

**3. *en vez de* — "instead of"**
*En vez de* + noun or infinitive = instead of:

- *En vez de café, voy a tomar un té.* — Instead of coffee, I'm going to have tea.
- *En vez de una cama matrimonial.* — Instead of a double bed.
- Synonym: *en lugar de* (slightly more formal)

**4. *le dieron su habitación a otra persona* — indirect object with plural *dar***
When *dar* is used in 3rd pl. preterite with an indirect object:

- *Le dieron su habitación a otra persona.* — They gave your room to another person.
- *dieron* = preterite of *dar* (3rd pl.)
- *Le* = to him/her/you (IO); *a otra persona* clarifies who received it
- Compare singular (L5/19): *el médico le dio una receta* = the doctor gave her a prescription

**5. *para compensarla* — *para* + infinitive + direct object pronoun**
*Para* + infinitive expresses purpose; the DO pronoun attaches to the infinitive:

- *Para compensarla.* — In order to compensate you. (*la* = feminine direct object referring to the guest)
- *Para ayudarle.* — In order to help you. (formal)
- The pronoun choice matches the gender/number of the person being compensated.

## Verb Conjugations

### resfriarse — to catch a cold
**Type:** regular -ar reflexive with accent shift in present (í stressed in singular + 3rd pl.)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me **resfrio** | nosotros: nos resfriamos |
| 2nd | tú: te **resfrías** | vosotros: os resfriáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: se **resfría** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se **resfrian** |

Present progressive drilled: *me estoy resfriando* — I'm getting a cold.

---

### parecer — to seem / appear
**Type:** irregular yo (*parezco*); otherwise regular -er; used like *gustar*

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **parezco** | nosotros: parecemos |
| 2nd | tú: pareces | vosotros: parecéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **parece** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: parecen |

Lesson drills: *¿le parece bien?*, *me parece bien*, *parece que le dieron su habitación a otra persona*.

## Summary

- **resfriarse** — regular -ar reflexive with accent shift; *me estoy resfriando* = I'm getting a cold
- **parecer** — irregular yo (*parezco*); used like *gustar*; *¿le parece bien?* = does that work for you?
- **compensar** — regular -ar; *para compensarla* = in order to compensate you (f.)
- ***en vez de*** = instead of; *en vez de café* = instead of coffee
- ***qué pena con usted*** = how embarrassing (formal hotel/service apology)
- ***le dieron su habitación a otra persona*** — 3rd pl. *dar* preterite with IO *le*
- ***cama individual*** = single bed; ***cama matrimonial*** = double bed
- ***de acuerdo*** = agreed / okay; universal acceptance expression
