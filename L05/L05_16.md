## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *ya quiero que llegue* — *querer que* + subjunctive for desires about future arrival**
*Querer que* + present subjunctive expresses wanting something to happen (different subject):

- *Ya quiero que llegue la primavera.* — I already want spring to arrive. (I can't wait for spring)
- *Ya quiero que llegue el verano.* — I can't wait for summer to arrive.
- *llegue* = present subjunctive of *llegar* (3rd sg.)
- *Ya* here intensifies: "I already/really want it now"

**2. *se te olvidó* — involuntary *se* extended to second person**
The involuntary *se* construction (first drilled with *se me olvidó*) applies to all persons:

| Person | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| I forgot | *se me olvidó* | *Se me olvidó el paraguas.* |
| You forgot (informal) | *se te olvidó* | *Se te olvidó tu paraguas.* |
| He/she/you (formal) forgot | *se le olvidó* | *Se le olvidó la reunión.* |
| We forgot | *se nos olvidó* | *Se nos olvidó llamar.* |

- The verb agrees with what was forgotten (the grammatical subject): *se te olvidaron los zapatos* (plural).

**3. Informal imperatives: *mira* and *espera***
Reviewed in this lesson:

| Infinitive | Formal (Ud.) | Informal (tú) |
|---|---|---|
| *mirar* | *mire* | *mira* |
| *esperar* | *espere* | *espera* |
| *quedarse* | *quédese* | *quédate* |

- *Mira este mensaje de texto.* — Look at this text message. (informal)
- *Espera, se te olvidó el paraguas.* — Wait, you forgot your umbrella. (informal)

**4. *caer* for rainfall — *cayó un aguacero***
*Caer* (to fall) is used for rain/downpours:

- *Cayó un fuerte aguacero.* — A heavy downpour fell.
- *Esta mañana cayó un aguacero muy fuerte.* — This morning a very heavy downpour fell.
- Word order shifts when the adjective is intensified: *un fuerte aguacero* → *un aguacero muy fuerte*

**5. *quédese con el vuelto* — "keep the change"**
- *el cambio* = coins available to make change (do you have change?)
- *el vuelto* = the change you receive back after paying
- *Quédese con el vuelto.* — Keep the change. (formal)
- *Quédate con el vuelto.* — Keep the change. (informal)

**6. *la semana entrante* — "next week"**
Three common ways to say "next week":
- *la semana entrante* (the coming/entering week)
- *la semana que viene* (the week that comes)
- *la próxima semana* (the next/upcoming week)

## Verb Conjugations

### cuidar — to take care of
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: cuido | nosotros: cuidamos |
| 2nd | tú: cuidas | vosotros: cuidáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: cuida | ellos/ellas/Uds.: cuidan |

Lesson drills: *mi hermana lo va a cuidar*, *¿quién cuida tu perro?*, *cuidarse* = to take care of oneself.

---

### enviar — to send
**Type:** regular -ar with accent shift in present (i is stressed in all singular + 3rd pl.)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **envío** | nosotros: enviamos |
| 2nd | tú: **envías** | vosotros: enviáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **envía** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **envían** |

Preterite: *envié, enviaste, **envió**, enviamos…*
Lesson drills: *me envió una foto* — he sent me a photo.

---

### llegar — to arrive (present subjunctive)
**Type:** regular -ar (g→gu before e: preterite yo *llegué*, subjunctive *llegue*)

Present subjunctive (drilled): *llegue, llegues, **llegue**, lleguemos, lleguéis, lleguen*
Lesson drills: *ya quiero que llegue la primavera*, *ya quiero que llegue el verano*.

## Summary

- **cuidar** — regular -ar; *lo va a cuidar* = she's going to take care of it
- **enviar** — -ar with accent shift; preterite *envió* = he/she sent
- **llegar** — subjunctive *llegue* = arrives (after *querer que*, *esperar que*, *ojalá*)
- ***se te olvidó*** — involuntary *se* (2nd person): you forgot; *se me/te/le/nos olvidó*
- ***ya quiero que llegue*** = I can't wait for [it] to arrive; present subjunctive after *querer que*
- ***cayó un aguacero*** = a downpour fell; *caer* for rainfall
- ***quédese con el vuelto*** = keep the change; *el cambio* = coins, *el vuelto* = change back
- Informal imperatives: *mira* (look), *espera* (wait); formal: *mire*, *espere*
