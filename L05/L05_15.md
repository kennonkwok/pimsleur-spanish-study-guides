## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *aunque haya* — present subjunctive of impersonal *haber***
The impersonal *hay* (there is/are) has a present subjunctive form *haya*:

- *Aunque haya mucha gente, vamos a disfrutar.* — Even if there are many people, we're going to enjoy it.
- Compare: *Aunque habrá mucha gente* (future, indicative — certain fact)
- *haya* is also used after *espero que*, *ojalá*, etc.: *Espero que haya tiempo.*

**2. *me equivoqué* — *equivocarse* (preterite with c→qu)**
*Equivocarse* = to be mistaken/wrong:

- *Me equivoqué.* — I was mistaken. / I made a mistake.
- Preterite spelling change: c → qu before *-é*: *me equivoqué* (not *equivocé*)
- *Perdón, me equivoqué.* — Excuse me, I was mistaken.
- Compare: *estar equivocado* = to be wrong (state): *Estoy equivocado.*

**3. *valieron la pena* — past of *valer la pena***
*Valer la pena* conjugates to agree with what is worth it:

| Subject | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| One thing | *vale la pena* (present) / *valió la pena* (pret.) | *El viaje valió la pena.* |
| Multiple things | *valen la pena* / *valieron la pena* | *Los dos lugares valieron la pena.* |

**4. *ya casi se acaba* — *acabarse* for "running out / coming to an end"**
*Acabarse* is the reflexive of *acabar*, used when something is ending or running out:

- *El invierno ya casi se acaba.* — Winter is almost over.
- *Se acabó el café.* — The coffee ran out. / The coffee is gone.
- *Se acabó la fiesta.* — The party is over.
- The subject is the thing that's ending; the reflexive *se* marks the involuntary completion.

**5. *están rebajados/as* — "on sale"**
*Rebajar* = to reduce/mark down. The result state uses *estar* + past participle:

- *Las botas están rebajadas.* — The boots are on sale.
- *Porque el invierno ya casi se acaba, muchas cosas están rebajadas.* — Because winter is almost over, many things are on sale.
- Gender/number agreement: *rebajado/a/os/as*

**6. *pero* vs. *perro* — the single-r tap vs. double-r trill**
Critical pronunciation distinction:
- *pero* [pe-ro] — single *r* = brief tap (like a flap in "butter") — means "but"
- *perro* [pe-ro] — double *rr* = sustained trill — means "dog"
- Also applies at word boundaries: *al rey* vs. *el rey*, *mar* vs. *marr-* in *marrón*

## Verb Conjugations

### equivocarse — to be mistaken / to make a mistake
**Type:** regular -ar reflexive; spelling change c→qu before *-e* (preterite yo)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me equivoco | nosotros: nos equivocamos |
| 2nd | tú: te equivocas | vosotros: os equivocáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: se equivoca | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se equivocan |

Preterite: ***me equivoqué**, te equivocaste, se equivocó, nos equivocamos…*
Lesson drills: *me equivoqué*, *perdón, me equivoqué*, *tiene razón, me equivoqué*.

---

### acabarse — to end / run out
**Type:** regular -ar reflexive; thing that ends = grammatical subject

| 3rd sg. | se acaba | *El invierno ya casi se acaba.* |
|---|---|---|
| 3rd pl. | se acaban | *Se acaban las vacaciones.* |

Preterite: *se acabó* (sg.) / *se acabaron* (pl.)
Lesson drills: *el invierno ya casi se acaba*, *se acabó el café*.

---

### valer — to be worth
**Type:** irregular yo (*valgo*); preterite *valió/valieron*

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **valgo** | nosotros: valemos |
| 2nd | tú: vales | vosotros: valéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: vale | ellos/ellas/Uds.: valen |

Preterite: *valí, valiste, **valió**, valimos, …, **valieron***
Lesson drills: *los dos lugares valieron la pena*, *vale la pena comprar zapatos nuevos*.

## Summary

- **equivocarse** — regular -ar reflexive (c→qu in preterite yo); *me equivoqué* = I was mistaken
- **acabarse** — regular -ar reflexive; *ya casi se acaba* = it's almost over; *se acabó* = it's done
- **valer** — irregular yo (*valgo*); *valió/valieron la pena* = was/were worth it
- ***aunque haya*** = even if there are; *haya* = present subjunctive of impersonal *haber*
- ***rebajado/a*** = on sale; *están rebajadas* = they're on sale (seasonal clearance context)
- ***ir de compras*** = to go shopping; *pronto voy a ir de compras*
- ***pero* vs. *perro*** — single r (tap) vs. double rr (trill); "but" vs. "dog"
- ***tenía*** = I used to have (imperfect of *tener*); past habit/state
