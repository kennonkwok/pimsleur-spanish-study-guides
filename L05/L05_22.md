## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Involuntary *se* extended to *nos* — "we had a flat tire"**
The involuntary *se* construction (previously drilled with *me* and *te*) applies to all persons. With *nos*, it expresses something that happened to "us" unintentionally:

| Person | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| I (dropped) | *se me cayó* | *Se me cayó el tenedor.* |
| He/she (dropped) | *se le cayó* | *Se le cayó el celular.* |
| We (had a flat tire) | *se nos pinchó* | *Se nos pinchó una llanta.* |
| We (it broke) | *se nos rompió* | *Se nos rompió el parabrisas.* |

- *Se nos pinchó una llanta en las montañas.* — We had a flat tire in the mountains.
- Verb agrees with what happened (*una llanta* = singular → *pinchó*).

**2. *se me rompió* (singular) vs. *se me rompieron* (plural)**
The verb in the involuntary *se* construction agrees with the grammatical subject (the thing that broke/fell):

- *Se me rompió el celular.* — My phone broke. (singular subject)
- *Se me rompieron los lentes.* — My glasses broke. (plural subject — review from L5/8)
- *Se me cayó el tenedor.* — I dropped the fork. (singular)
- *Se me cayeron las llaves.* — I dropped the keys. (plural)

**3. *no sé lo que habría hecho si hubiera estado sola* — embedded past counterfactual**
A complex sentence combining *lo que* (what) with a past counterfactual:

| Part | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Main clause | *no sé lo que* + conditional perfect | *No sé lo que habría hecho* |
| *si* clause | *si* + past subjunctive (*hubiera* + past part.) | *si hubiera estado sola* |

- *No sé lo que habría hecho si hubiera estado sola.* — I don't know what I would have done if I had been alone.
- *hubiera estado* = past subjunctive of *estar* (1st sg.)

**4. *darnos* / *darme* / *dármela* — infinitive + object pronouns**
When *dar* takes direct and indirect object pronouns in an infinitive context:

- *¿Puede darnos esa mesa?* — Can you give us that table?
- *¿Puedes darme su dirección?* — Can you give me his address?
- *Puedo dármela.* — I can give it to you. (IO *me* + DO *la*, attached to infinitive)
- Order: IO before DO, both after the infinitive.

**5. *grave* — "serious" in medical/severity contexts**
*Grave* modifies severity, not literally "grave" as in burial:
- *No fue grave.* — It wasn't serious.
- *Me lastimé la rodilla muy gravemente.* — I injured my knee very seriously. (review L5/8)
- *una enfermedad grave* = a serious illness
- *un error grave* = a serious mistake

## Verb Conjugations

### pinchar — to puncture / go flat
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: pincho | nosotros: pinchamos |
| 2nd | tú: pinchas | vosotros: pincháis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: pincha | ellos/ellas/Uds.: pinchan |

Preterite: *pinché, pinchaste, **pinchó**, pinchamos…*
Involuntary: *se nos pinchó una llanta* — we had a flat tire (lit. "a tire punctured itself on us")

---

### invitar — to invite
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: invito | nosotros: invitamos |
| 2nd | tú: invitas | vosotros: invitáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **invita** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **invitan** |

Preterite: *invité, invitaste, **invitó**, invitamos, …, **invitaron***
Lesson drills: *nos invitó a su fiesta*, *me invitaron a una graduación*, *me invitó a la graduación*.

---

### venir — to come (preterite review)
Preterite: *vine, **viniste**, vino, vinimos, vinisteis, vinieron*
Lesson drills: *viniste para ir a una graduación* — you came to go to a graduation.

## Summary

- **pinchar** — regular -ar; involuntary *se nos pinchó una llanta* = we had a flat tire
- **invitar** — regular -ar; *nos invitó* = he invited us; *me invitaron* = they invited me
- **venir** — preterite *viniste* = you came (informal 2nd sg.)
- ***se nos pinchó*** — involuntary *se* extended to *nos*: it happened to us unintentionally
- ***se me rompió*** (sg.) vs. ***se me rompieron*** (pl.) — verb agrees with grammatical subject
- ***si hubiera estado sola*** — past counterfactual with *estar*; *hubiera estado* = had been
- ***grave*** = serious (severity); *no fue grave* = it wasn't serious
- ***darnos / darme*** — infinitive with attached IO pronoun for "give us/me"
