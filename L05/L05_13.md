## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *aunque* + subjunctive — "even if" (possibility)**
*Aunque* means "even if" (subjunctive) or "although/even though" (indicative):

| Mood | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Subjunctive | even if (uncertain/possible) | *Aunque llueva, vamos a pasarla bien.* |
| Indicative | even though (known fact) | *Aunque llueve mucho, vamos.* |

- *Aunque llueva mucho, vamos a disfrutarlo.* — Even if it rains a lot, we're going to enjoy it.
- *llueva* = present subjunctive of *llover* (o→ue)

**2. *no habrá* — future of *haber* (impersonal)**
The impersonal *haber* has a future form *habrá* (from *hay*):

| Tense | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Present | *hay* | *Hay mucha gente.* |
| Future | *habrá* | *No habrá mucha gente.* |
| Past (preterite) | *hubo* | *Hubo un accidente.* |
| Past (imperfect) | *había* | *Había mucha gente.* |

- *En junio no habrá mucha gente.* — In June there won't be many people.
- *Habrá mucho tráfico.* — There will be a lot of traffic.

**3. *desde hace* + time — ongoing duration ("for X days")**
To express how long something has been happening (and still is), use *desde hace* + time:

- *Desde hace tres días tengo dolor de muelas.* — I've had a toothache for three days.
- Compare: *hace tres días* (preterite) = three days ago (completed past)
- *Desde hace* + present tense = for [duration] up to now (still going on)

**4. *se permiten / se permite* — impersonal *se* with singular/plural**
When using impersonal *se* for what is or isn't permitted, the verb agrees with the noun subject:

- *Se permiten mascotas.* — Pets are permitted. (plural *mascotas* → *permiten*)
- *No se permite fumar.* — Smoking is not permitted. (infinitive *fumar* = singular → *permite*)
- *Se alquilan apartamentos.* — Apartments are rented.

**5. *quedar* for location (Latin American usage)**
In much of Latin America, *quedar* is used instead of *estar* for permanent or semi-permanent location:

- *El apartamento queda cerca de la playa.* — The apartment is located near the beach.
- *¿Dónde queda la oficina?* — Where is the office located?
- This is regional; *estar* is also always correct for location.

**6. *la vamos a pasar bien* — "we're going to enjoy it"**
*Pasar bien* literally means "to pass/spend [time] well." When *la* is added, it refers to the vacation or trip:

- *La vamos a pasar bien.* — We're going to have a great time. / We're going to enjoy it.
- *¿La pasaron bien?* — Did you have a good time?

## Summary

- **reservar** — regular -ar; *nadie lo ha reservado todavía* = nobody has reserved it yet
- **descansar** — regular -ar; *solo quiero descansar* = I just want to relax
- **fumar** — regular -ar; *no se permite fumar* = smoking is not permitted
- **llover** — impersonal, o→ue; present subjunctive *llueva*; *aunque llueva* = even if it rains
- ***aunque*** + subjunctive = even if (possible); + indicative = even though (fact)
- ***no habrá*** = there won't be; future of impersonal *haber*
- ***desde hace*** + present = for [duration] (ongoing): *desde hace tres días tengo…*
- ***se permiten/se permite*** = are/is permitted; verb agrees with noun or infinitive
- ***quedar*** = to be located (Latin American usage); *el apartamento queda cerca de la playa*
