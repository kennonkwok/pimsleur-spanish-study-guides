## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Pluperfect tense: *haber* (imperfect) + past participle**
The pluperfect expresses an action completed *before* another past action — "had done."
Form: imperfect of *haber* (*había, habías, había, habíamos, habíais, habían*) + past participle.

- *Le habían dado mi habitación a otra persona.* — They had given my room to another person.
- *Me la había dado.* — He/she had given it to me (referring to a handbag).

| Person | Imperfect of haber |
|---|---|
| yo | había |
| tú | habías |
| él/ella/Ud. | había |
| nosotros | habíamos |
| vosotros | habíais |
| ellos/ellas/Uds. | **habían** |

**2. Informal commands: affirmative vs. negative (tú)**
Affirmative *tú* commands use the 3rd-person singular present indicative form (no final *s*):
- *dobla* (turn), *camina* (walk)

Negative *tú* commands use the present subjunctive + *no*:
- *no dobles* (don't turn), *no camines* (don't walk)

The lesson contrasts formal (*-e/-en*) and informal commands:
| Verb | Formal (Ud.) | Informal affirm. (tú) | Informal neg. (tú) |
|---|---|---|---|
| doblar | *doble* | *dobla* | *no dobles* |
| caminar | *camine* | *camina* | *no camines* |

**3. *al* + infinitive = "upon/when doing"**
*Al* (= *a + el*) before an infinitive expresses a simultaneous or immediately prior action.
- *Al salir del hotel, doble a la izquierda.* — Upon leaving the hotel, turn left.

**4. *más cercano/a* vs. *muy cerca***
- *cercano/a* = adjective modifying a noun → *la tienda más cercana* (the nearest store)
- *cerca* = adverb for location → *Hay una muy cerca de aquí.* (There's one very close to here.)

**5. *ya tengo* — "I already have (them)"**
*Ya* before a verb in the present can mean "already" — the object is implicit from context.
- *Ya tengo.* — I already have (them). [toothpaste and toothbrush implied]

**6. Indirect object pronoun review: *le* / *me***
- *Le ofrecemos el desayuno gratis.* — We offer you (formal) breakfast free.
- *Para compensarme por las molestias.* — To compensate me for the inconveniences.

## Verb Conjugations

### perder — to lose
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie) in present indicative; regular in preterite

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **pierdo** | nosotros: perdemos |
| 2nd | tú: **pierdes** | vosotros: perdéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **pierde** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **pierden** |

Lesson drills (preterite): *perdió* (she/he/it lost), *perdí* (I lost), *tuve que comprar* (I had to buy).

## Summary

- **perder** — stem-changing (e→ie); key new vocabulary; lesson drills preterite forms (*perdió*, *perdí*)
- **traer** — irregular (*traigo*); used as *ir a* + infinitive + clitic: *van a traerlo*
- **pedir** — stem-changing (e→i); used in *voy a pedir*; same verb for "order" in a restaurant
- **ofrecer** — irregular yo (*ofrezco*); lesson drills present (*ofrecemos*) and preterite (*ofrecieron*)
- **doblar** — regular -ar; important contrast: formal *doble* vs. informal *dobla* vs. negative *no dobles*
- **caminar** — regular -ar; same command pattern contrast as *doblar*
