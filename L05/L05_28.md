## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *¿cómo amaneció?* — "how are you this morning?"**
*Amanecer* (to dawn, to wake up) used as a morning greeting:

- *¿Cómo amaneció?* — How are you this morning? (formal; lit. "how did you wake up?")
- *¿Cómo amaneciste?* — How are you this morning? (informal)
- Common response: *Bien, gracias.* — Fine, thank you.
- Less common than *buenos días* but widely understood in Latin America

**2. *ya mero* — "any minute now / just about"**
*Ya mero* is a common Latin American expression meaning something is about to happen:

- *Ya mero viene mi esposo.* — My husband is coming any minute now.
- *Ya* alone = already; *ya mero* = on the verge of
- Common in Mexico and most of Latin America

**3. *almorzar* — stem-changing (o→ue)**
*Almorzar* (to have lunch) follows the same o→ue pattern as *dormir* and *poder* in the present:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **almuerzo** | nosotros: almorzamos |
| 2nd | tú: **almuerzas** | vosotros: almorzáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **almuerza** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **almuerzan** |

- *Almorzamos a las dos.* — We have lunch at two o'clock.
- *¿Vas a almorzar con nosotros?* — Are you going to have lunch with us?

**4. *cuando deje de llover* / *cuando salga* — present subjunctive for future reference**
When *cuando* (when) introduces a clause referring to the future, Spanish uses the present subjunctive (not indicative):

| Subjunctive form | Meaning | Infinitive |
|---|---|---|
| *deje de llover* | when it stops raining | *dejar de llover* |
| *salga de la habitación* | when you leave the room | *salir* |

- *Puedo lavárselas cuando deje de llover.* — I can wash them for you when it stops raining.
- *No olvides apagar la luz cuando salga de la habitación.* — Don't forget to turn off the light when you leave the room.
- Compare: *cuando salgo* (indicative) = "when I leave" (habitual/known fact)

**5. *dejar de* + infinitive — "to stop doing"**
*Dejar de* + infinitive = to stop (an action):

- *Cuando deje de llover.* — When it stops raining.
- *Dejé de fumar.* — I stopped smoking.
- *No dejes de llamarme.* — Don't forget to call me. / Don't stop calling me.

**6. *le pedí que me llamara* — *pedir que* + imperfect subjunctive (reported speech)**
Reviewed from Lesson 12. After *pedir que* in the past, the subordinate clause takes the imperfect subjunctive:

- *Le pedí que me llamara.* — I asked her to call me. (lit. I asked that she call me)
- *llamara* = imperfect subjunctive of *llamar* (regular -ar: *-ara* ending)
- Compare: *le pedí que viniera* — I asked him to come (*venir* → *viniera*)

**7. *¿puedo ayudarle a lavar los platos?* — *ayudar a* + infinitive**
*Ayudar a* + infinitive = to help to do something:

- *¿Puedo ayudarle a lavar los platos?* — Can I help you wash the dishes?
- *Me ayudó a cargar las maletas.* — He helped me carry the bags.
- *Ayudar* takes *a* before an infinitive (unlike English where it's optional).

## Verb Conjugations

### almorzar — to have lunch
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue); spelling change z→c before e (preterite 1st sg.)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **almuerzo** | nosotros: almorzamos |
| 2nd | tú: **almuerzas** | vosotros: almorzáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **almuerza** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **almuerzan** |

Preterite: ***almorcé**, almorzaste, almorzó, …*
Lesson drills: *almorzamos a las dos*, *¿vas a almorzar con nosotros?*

## Summary

- **almorzar** — stem-changing (o→ue); *almorzamos a las dos* = we have lunch at two
- **dejar** — regular -ar; *dejar de* + inf. = to stop doing; *le dejé un mensaje* = I left a message
- **ayudar** — regular -ar; *ayudar a* + inf. = to help to do; *¿puedo ayudarle a lavar los platos?*
- ***¿cómo amaneció?*** = how are you this morning? (morning greeting, *amanecer* = to dawn)
- ***ya mero*** = any minute now / just about to; *ya mero viene* = he's coming any minute
- ***cuando* + present subjunctive** — future reference: *cuando deje de llover*, *cuando salga*
- ***dejar de*** + infinitive = to stop doing: *cuando deje de llover* = when it stops raining
- ***le pedí que me llamara*** — *pedir que* + imperfect subj.: I asked her to call me
