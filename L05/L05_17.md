## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Pluperfect — "I had done" (*había* + past participle)**
The pluperfect expresses an action that had occurred before another past action:

| Person | *Haber* (imperfect) | + Past Participle | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| yo | *había* | *olvidado* | *Se me había olvidado.* |
| tú | *habías* | *dicho* | *Ya te había dicho.* |
| él/ella | *había* | *jubilado* | *Ya se había jubilado.* |
| nosotros | *habíamos* | *hablado* | *Ya habíamos hablado.* |

- *Se me había olvidado que ya eres abuela.* — I had forgotten that you're already a grandmother.
- *Le había dicho.* — I had told him.
- *¿Ya te había dicho?* — Had I already told you?

**2. Negative informal imperative: *no olvides***
Informal negative commands use *no* + present subjunctive (same form as subjunctive):

| Infinitive | Positive imp. (informal) | Negative imp. (informal) |
|---|---|---|
| *olvidar* | *olvida* | *no olvides* |
| *hablar* | *habla* | *no hables* |
| *comer* | *come* | *no comas* |
| *salir* | *sal* | *no salgas* |

- *No olvides tu paraguas.* — Don't forget your umbrella.
- Formal negative is the same as formal imperative with *no*: *no olvide*, *no salga*.

**3. *ya quiero que sea* — subjunctive of *ser* for anticipated events**
When you're eager for an event to arrive, *ya quiero que sea* + the event:

- *Ya quiero que sea la reunión.* — I can't wait for the meeting. (lit. "I already want it to be the meeting")
- *Ya quiero que sea mañana.* — I can't wait for tomorrow.
- *sea* = present subjunctive of *ser* (3rd sg.)

**4. *harto/a de* — "fed up with"**
*Estar harto/a de* + noun or infinitive:
- *Estoy harto del invierno.* — I'm fed up with winter. (*del* = *de* + *el*)
- *Estoy harta de esperar.* — I'm fed up with waiting.
- Always with *estar* (temporary state).

**5. *la fila* — the line/queue**
- *¿Esta es la fila?* — Is this the line?
- *Hacer fila* = to stand in line
- In Spain: *la cola* (also "to queue" = *hacer cola*)

**6. Pluperfect with involuntary *se*: *se me había olvidado***
Combines the involuntary *se* construction with the pluperfect *había*:
- *Se me había olvidado* = I had forgotten (lit. "it had slipped from me")
- Compare present perfect: *se me ha olvidado* = I've forgotten
- Compare preterite: *se me olvidó* = I forgot

## Verb Conjugations

### jubilarse — to retire
**Type:** regular -ar reflexive

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me jubilo | nosotros: nos jubilamos |
| 2nd | tú: te jubilas | vosotros: os jubiláis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: se jubila | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se jubilan |

Lesson drills: *mi esposo va a jubilarse el año entrante*, *Louis va a jubilarse*.

---

### cometer — to commit / make
**Type:** regular -er

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: cometo | nosotros: cometemos |
| 2nd | tú: cometes | vosotros: cometéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: comete | ellos/ellas/Uds.: cometen |

Preterite: ***cometí**, cometiste, cometió, cometimos…*
Lesson drills: *cometí un error*, *perdón, cometí un error*, *lo siento, cometí un error*.

---

### ser — to be (identity/classification)
**Type:** highly irregular; present subjunctive drilled in this lesson

Present subjunctive (drilled): *sea, seas, **sea**, seamos, seáis, sean*
Lesson drills: *ya quiero que sea la reunión*, *ya quiero que sea mañana*.

---

### haber — to have (auxiliary)
**Type:** highly irregular; imperfect = pluperfect auxiliary

Imperfect (used for pluperfect):

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **había** | nosotros: habíamos |
| 2nd | tú: **habías** | vosotros: habíais |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **había** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **habían** |

Lesson drills: *se me había olvidado*, *le había dicho*, *ya te había dicho*.

---

## Summary

- **jubilarse** — regular -ar reflexive; *va a jubilarse* = is going to retire
- **cometer** — regular -er; *cometí un error* = I made a mistake
- **ser** — irregular; present subjunctive *sea*; *ya quiero que sea* = I can't wait for it to be
- **haber** (imperfect) = *había/habías/había…* — used to form the pluperfect
- **Pluperfect** = *había* + past participle: *se me había olvidado* = I had forgotten
- **No olvides** — negative informal imperative; *no* + present subjunctive
- ***harto/a de*** = fed up with; always with *estar*
- ***junto a*** = by / next to; *mesa junto a la ventana* = table by the window
