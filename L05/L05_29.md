## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *aunque esté lloviendo* — *aunque* + present subjunctive for "even if"**
When *aunque* (although / even if) introduces an uncertain or hypothetical situation, it takes the subjunctive:

- *Tengo que ir de compras aunque esté lloviendo.* — I have to go shopping even if it's raining.
- *Voy a ir aunque llueva.* — I'm going even if it rains.
- Compare: *aunque llueve mucho* (indicative) = even though it rains a lot (acknowledged fact)
- The choice of subjunctive vs. indicative after *aunque* signals uncertainty vs. fact.

**2. *dijo que necesitaba* — reported speech with imperfect**
After a preterite reporting verb (*dijo*, *me dijo*, *me llamó y dijo*), the reported content shifts to the imperfect:

| Direct speech | Reported speech |
|---|---|
| *"Necesito leche."* | *Dijo que **necesitaba** leche.* |
| *"Hay un problema."* | *Me dijo que **había** un problema.* |
| *"Quiero más dinero."* | *Dijo que **quería** más dinero.* |

- *Dijo que necesitaba leche, pan y huevos.* — She said she needed milk, bread, and eggs.
- *Me dijo que había una buena peluquería.* — She told me there was a good barbershop.

**3. *cortarme / cortarse el pelo* — reflexive body part construction**
To express getting (or giving oneself) a haircut, use *cortar* + reflexive pronoun + *el pelo*:

- *Quiero ir a cortarme el pelo.* — I want to go get my hair cut.
- *Mi esposo se corta el pelo ahí.* — My husband gets his haircut there.
- *Me corté el pelo ayer.* — I got my haircut yesterday.
- The article (*el*) is used with body parts in Spanish (not the possessive *mi*).

**4. *había* — imperfect of *haber* (impersonal "there was/were")**
*Había* is the imperfect form of impersonal *haber* (*hay* = there is/are):

| Tense | Form | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Present | *hay* | there is / there are |
| Preterite | *hubo* | there was (a specific event) |
| Imperfect | ***había*** | there was/were (ongoing, background) |

- *Me dijo que había una buena peluquería.* — She told me there was a good barbershop.
- *Había mucha gente en el parque.* — There were a lot of people in the park.

**5. *trabajaba como mesero* — imperfect for past habitual/ongoing**
The imperfect expresses past habits or ongoing states:

- *Trabajaba como mesero cuando era estudiante.* — I used to work as a waiter when I was a student.
- *trabajaba* = I used to work / I was working (imperfect of *trabajar*)
- Contrast with preterite: *trabajé como mesero* = I worked as a waiter (completed event)

**6. *¿cuánto dejo de propina?* — asking about tip customs**
Standard phrase for asking how much to tip:

- *¿Cuánto dejo de propina?* — How much do I leave for a tip?
- *El 10%.* — Ten percent.
- *Quiero dejar una buena propina.* — I want to leave a good tip.

## Verb Conjugations

### cortar — to cut
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: corto | nosotros: cortamos |
| 2nd | tú: cortas | vosotros: cortáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: corta | ellos/ellas/Uds.: cortan |

Reflexive use: *cortarme el pelo* (me), *cortarse el pelo* (se)
Lesson drills: *quiero ir a cortarme el pelo*, *mi esposo se corta el pelo ahí*.

---

### necesitar — to need
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: necesito | nosotros: necesitamos |
| 2nd | tú: necesitas | vosotros: necesitáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: necesita | ellos/ellas/Uds.: necesitan |

Imperfect: *necesitaba, necesitabas, **necesitaba**, …*
Lesson drills: *dijo que necesitaba leche, pan y huevos* (reported speech, imperfect).

---

### ver — to see
**Type:** irregular yo (*veo*); irregular imperfect (*veía*); otherwise regular -er in present

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **veo** | nosotros: vemos |
| 2nd | tú: **ves** | vosotros: veis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: ve | ellos/ellas/Uds.: ven |

Lesson drills: *¿ves a nuestro mesero?* (informal 2nd sg. present).

## Summary

- **cortar** — regular -ar; reflexive *cortarme/cortarse el pelo* = to get one's hair cut
- **necesitar** — regular -ar; imperfect *necesitaba* = needed/used to need (in reported speech)
- **ver** — irregular present (*veo*, *ves*); *¿ves a nuestro mesero?* = do you see our waiter?
- ***aunque* + subj.** — "even if" (uncertain): *aunque esté lloviendo* = even if it's raining
- ***dijo que* + imperfect** — reported speech: *dijo que necesitaba* = she said she needed
- ***había*** = there was/were (imperfect *haber*); *me dijo que había* = she told me there was
- ***cortarme/cortarse el pelo*** = to get a haircut; reflexive body-part construction
- ***¿cuánto dejo de propina?*** = how much do I leave for a tip?; *el mesero* = the waiter (LatAm)
