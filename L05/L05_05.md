## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *valer la pena* + infinitive (worthwhile)**
*Valer la pena* is a fixed phrase meaning "to be worth it." Add an infinitive to say it is worth doing something. The clitic pronoun can be attached to the infinitive.

- *Vale la pena visitar la iglesia.* — It's worthwhile to visit the church.
- *Definitivamente vale la pena visitarla.* — It's definitely worthwhile to visit it.
- *¿Vale la pena visitarla?* — Is it worthwhile to visit it?

**2. Passive voice with *ser* + past participle**
Spanish uses *ser* + past participle for completed passive actions (who built something, when it was done). The participle agrees in gender and number with the subject.

| Subject gender | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Feminine (*la iglesia*) | *fue construida* | *La iglesia fue construida en el siglo XVI.* |
| Masculine (*el edificio*) | *fue construido* | *El edificio fue construido en el siglo XVII.* |

**3. Imperfect tense for ongoing past situations**
The imperfect (*imperfecto*) describes ongoing, habitual, or continuous past states — contrasted with the preterite (completed events).

| Use preterite for | Use imperfect for |
|---|---|
| Completed event: *Llamé al restaurante.* | Ongoing state: *No encontraba mi billetera.* |
| Completed event: *Ganó.* | Ongoing state: *Vivía en Boston.* |
| Completed event: *Dormí mal.* | Ongoing state: *Estábamos decepcionados.* |

Key imperfect forms drilled:
- *encontraba* — was finding / used to find
- *vivía* — was living / used to live
- *estábamos* — we were
- *tenía que* — had to (imperfect of *tener que*)
- *era* — was (imperfect of *ser*): *cuando era estudiante*

**4. Pluperfect (*había* + past participle)**
*Había* + past participle expresses an action completed before another past moment ("had done").

- *Había dejado mi billetera en la mesa.* — I had left my wallet on the table.

**5. Superlative with *más*: *el/la más + adjective + de***
"The most [adjective] of [group]" = *el/la más [adjective] de [group]*.

- *La casa más antigua de la ciudad.* — The oldest house in the city.
- *El edificio más antiguo.* — The oldest building.

**6. Equality comparison: *tan + adjective + como***
"Not as [adjective] as" = *no tan + adjective + como*.

- *Boston no es tan antigua como Lima.* — Boston is not as old as Lima.

**7. *quizás* + subjunctive for uncertain possibility**
When *quizás* expresses genuine uncertainty, it triggers the subjunctive (*pueda* instead of *puede*).

- *Quizás el sábado entrante pueda ir con usted.* — Maybe next Saturday I can go with you. (*pueda* = subjunctive of *poder*)

**8. *tener ganas de* + infinitive (eagerness)**
Fixed phrase expressing desire or eagerness.

- *Tengo muchas ganas de conocerlo.* — I'm very eager to meet him.

## Summary

- **dormir** — stem-changing (o→ue present; o→u preterite 3rd); preterite *dormí* = key drill
- **encontrar** — stem-changing (o→ue present); imperfect *encontraba* used for ongoing past search
- **vivir** — regular -ir; imperfect *vivía* = was living / used to live (key imperfect pattern)
- **ganar** — regular -ar; preterite *ganó* = he won
- **reunirse** — reflexive -ir; *reunirnos* used with *ir a* for planned group meetings
- **valer** — irregular (*valgo*); *valer la pena* + infinitive = fixed phrase for "worthwhile"
- **Key grammar:** imperfect for ongoing past states (*encontraba, vivía, estábamos, era*); pluperfect *había dejado*; passive *fue construida/construido*; superlative *el más antiguo de*; equality comparison *no tan antigua como*; *quizás* + subjunctive (*pueda*)
