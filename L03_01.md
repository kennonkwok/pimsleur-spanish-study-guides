## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Introducing yourself and others: *presentar***

Three key forms drilled in this lesson:

| Form | Spanish | English | Register |
|---|---|---|---|
| I introduce myself | *Me presento.* | I introduce myself (lit. "myself I present") | any |
| Permit me to introduce myself | *Permítame presentarme.* | Formal variant | formal |
| I introduce you (formal) | *Le presento.* | (to a 3rd party) | formal |
| I introduce you (informal) | *Te presento.* | (to a 3rd party) | informal |

Full formal introduction: *Le presento al señor Gómez.* (I introduce you to Mr. Gómez.)
Full informal introduction: *Te presento a mi colega / a mi esposa.*

Note: *al* = *a + el* (contraction required before masculine nouns).

---

**2. Pleasure to meet you: *mucho gusto en + infinitive with clitic***

The lesson introduces this more complete formula alongside bare *encantado/a*:

| Situation | Spanish |
|---|---|
| Meeting a woman (formal) | *Mucho gusto en conocerla.* |
| Meeting a man (formal) | *Mucho gusto en conocerlo.* |
| Meeting someone (informal) | *Mucho gusto en conocerte.* |

The clitic (*la* / *lo* / *te*) refers to the person being met and must agree in gender and formality.

---

**3. ¿A qué te/se dedicas/dedica? — Asking what someone does for a living**

A natural, idiomatic way to ask about occupation. More specific than *¿Cuál es tu trabajo?*

| Register | Question | Answer pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Informal | *¿A qué te dedicas?* | *Trabajo en una empresa internacional.* / *Soy médico.* |
| Formal | *¿A qué se dedica (usted)?* | *Soy profesor.* |
| 3rd person | *¿A qué se dedica tu esposo?* | *Es médico.* |

---

**4. *estar* + gerund — Actions in progress**

*Estoy tratando de encontrar el lugar.* — I'm trying to find the place.

Formation: *estar* (conjugated) + verb stem + *-ando* (-ar verbs) / *-iendo* (-er/-ir verbs).

- *estoy tratando* — I am trying
- *estás buscando* — you are looking for

---

**5. *nunca he estado* — Present perfect with *nunca***

*Nunca he estado allí.* — I've never been there.

Present perfect = *haber* (present) + past participle. *Nunca* (never) precedes the auxiliary.

| Pronoun | haber | Example |
|---|---|---|
| yo | he | *Nunca he estado allí.* |
| tú | has | *¿Has estado en Costa Rica?* |
| él/ella/Ud. | ha | *Él ha viajado mucho.* |
| nosotros | hemos | *Nunca hemos estado allí.* |

---

**6. *ya regreso* — Idiomatic "I'll be right back"**

*Ya* here means "right away / immediately," not "already." This is a fixed conversational formula:
- *Perdón, ya regreso.* — Sorry, I'll be right back.

## Verb Conjugations

### mostrar — to show
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue) — stem changes in all forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **muestro** | nosotros: mostramos |
| 2nd | tú: **muestras** | vosotros: mostráis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **muestra** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **muestran** |

Lesson drills: *puedes mostrarme la calle*, *mostrarte* (infinitive + clitic).

---

## Summary

- **Core theme:** Navigating being lost + meeting people formally and informally at a business lunch in Mexico.
- **mostrar** — stem-changing (o→ue); used with object clitics attached to infinitive (*mostrarme*, *mostrarte*)
- **encontrar** — stem-changing (o→ue); paired with *tratar de* + infinitive
- **presentar** — regular -ar; three forms: *me presento* (self), *le presento* (formal 3rd), *te presento* (informal 3rd)
- **conocer** — irregular yo (*conozco*); lesson focuses on *mucho gusto en conocer-* + clitic (la / lo / te) for "pleased to meet you"
- **dedicarse** — regular -ar reflexive; *¿a qué te/se dedica?* is the key occupation question; always requires preposition *a* before *qué*
- **visitar** — regular -ar; heavily drilled with indirect object pronoun *nos* (*nos visitan*)
- **regresar** — regular -ar; *ya regreso* is a fixed idiomatic phrase
- **New grammar highlights:** *estar* + gerund for actions in progress; present perfect (*nunca he estado*) for first appearance of "have been"; formal vs. informal pronoun switch (*le* vs. *te*) in introductions
