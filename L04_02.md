## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Present perfect: *haber* + past participle**
Formed with the present tense of *haber* + past participle. Used for recent or life-experience actions, similar to English "have [done]."

- *He leído su novela nueva.* — I have read her new novel.
- *¿La ha leído?* — Have you read it?

Key irregular past participles at this level:
- *leer* → *leído* (note accent mark)
- *escribir* → *escrito*
- *ver* → *visto*

**2. Preterite of *leer***
*Leer* is a spelling-change verb in the preterite. The *i* in the 3rd person singular/plural becomes *y* between two vowels:

| yo | tú | él/ella | nosotros | vosotros | ellos |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **leí** | leíste | **leyó** | leímos | leísteis | **leyeron** |

- *La leí hace poco.* — I read it a little while ago.
- *Yo la leí.* — I read it.

**3. *acabar de* + infinitive — to have just done**
Expresses a very recent action:

- *Acaba de terminar sus estudios.* — She has just finished her studies.
- *Acabo de leer la novela.* — I have just read the novel.

**4. *hacer* in time expressions — [X time] ago**
*Hace* + time period placed after a preterite verb:

- *La leí hace poco.* — I read it a little while ago.
- *Lo compré hace una hora.* — I bought it an hour ago.

**5. Direct object pronouns with books/novels**
Spanish must use a pronoun to replace a previously mentioned object. For feminine nouns like *la novela*: use *la*; for masculine *el libro*: use *lo*.

- *¿La ha leído?* — Have you read it? (referring to *la novela*)
- *Yo los leo traducidos.* — I read them translated. (referring to *los libros*)

## Verb Conjugations

### leer — to read
**Type:** regular -er (spelling-change in preterite: i→y in 3rd person)

Present tense:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **leo** | nosotros: leemos |
| 2nd | tú: lees | vosotros: leéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **lee** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **leen** |

Preterite:

| yo | tú | él/ella | nosotros | vosotros | ellos |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **leí** | leíste | **leyó** | leímos | leísteis | leyeron |

Lesson drills: *me gusta leer*, *leo traducidos*, *la leí hace poco*, *yo la leí*, *he leído su novela nueva*, *¿la ha leído?*

---

### haber — to have (auxiliary)
**Type:** irregular (auxiliary only at this level)

Present tense (used to form present perfect):

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **he** | nosotros: hemos |
| 2nd | tú: has | vosotros: habéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **ha** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **han** |

Lesson drills: *he leído*, *¿la ha leído?*, *sí, ha leído su novela nueva*.

---

### conocer — to know (a person/place)
**Type:** irregular yo form (*conozco*); otherwise regular -er

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **conozco** | nosotros: conocemos |
| 2nd | tú: conoces | vosotros: conocéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **conoce** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **conocen** |

Lesson drills: *¿conoce a la escritora Nancy Nesbitt?*, *sí, la conozco*.

---

## Summary

- **leer** — regular -er; preterite has spelling change (i→y in 3rd person: *leyó*); present perfect: *he leído*
- **haber** — irregular auxiliary; used to build present perfect (*he/ha leído*)
- **conocer** — irregular yo (*conozco*); used for knowing people (*conocer a*)
