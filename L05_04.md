## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Imperfect for ongoing/habitual past: *no encontraba***
The imperfect (*imperfecto*) describes a past action that was ongoing, habitual, or in progress — not a single completed event. The lesson explicitly contrasts this with the preterite.

- *No encontraba mi billetera.* — I wasn't finding / couldn't find my wallet. (ongoing situation)
- *La comida estaba regular.* — The food was so-so. (condition at the time)
- *Todavía estaba ahí.* — It was still there. (ongoing state)

Compare to preterite (completed, single event):
- *Llamé al restaurante.* — I called the restaurant. (one completed call)
- *Fui al cine.* — I went to the movies. (completed trip)

**2. Past perfect (*pluscuamperfecto*): *había + participio***
The past perfect expresses an action completed before another past moment. Formed with imperfect of *haber* + past participle.

| *haber* (imperfect) | Past participle |
|---|---|
| había | visto, dejado, hablado |

- *Yo ya la había visto.* — I had already seen it.
- *Había dejado mi billetera en la mesa.* — I had left my wallet on the table.
- Note: *ya* reinforces the "already" sense before a past event.

**3. *ahí* vs. *allí* (location adverbs)**
The lesson explicitly distinguishes these:

| Term | Meaning | Distance |
|---|---|---|
| *aquí* | here | close to speaker |
| *ahí* | there | relatively close; the item is in sight/near |
| *allí* | there / over there | more distant |

- *Todavía estaba ahí.* — It was still (right) there.
- The lesson notes *ahí* and *allí* are often interchangeable but defaults to *ahí* for this lesson.

**4. *tener suerte* — to be lucky**
Spanish uses *tener* + noun where English uses "to be" + adjective for many states:

- *Tuvo suerte.* — You were lucky. (lit. "You had luck.")
- *Tengo hambre.* — I'm hungry. (lit. "I have hunger.")
- *Tengo ganas de ir.* — I'm eager to go. (lit. "I have desire to go.")

**5. Reflexive infinitive clitics: *reunirme / reunirnos***
When a reflexive verb follows another verb in the infinitive, the reflexive clitic attaches to the end of the infinitive and agrees with the subject:

- *Voy a reunirme.* — I'm going to meet (get together).
- *Vamos a reunirnos.* — We're going to meet.
- *Tengo que preguntarle.* — I have to ask her/him. (*le* = indirect object, not reflexive here)

**6. *ojalá que* + subjunctive**
*Ojalá* (from Arabic *inshallah*) expresses hope or wish and always triggers the subjunctive:

- *Ojalá que no llueva.* — Hopefully it doesn't rain. (*llueva* = present subjunctive of *llover*)
- The *que* is optional in speech: *Ojalá no llueva.*

**7. *hacer* for weather**
*Hacer* is the standard verb for weather descriptions (not *ser/estar*):

- *Hace fresco.* — It's cool.
- *Hace calor/frío.* — It's hot/cold.

## Summary

- **Core tense contrast:** preterite (completed actions) vs. imperfect (ongoing/habitual past states) — central to the lesson. *Llamé* vs. *no encontraba*.
- **Past perfect:** *había + participio* for actions completed before another past moment — *ya la había visto*, *había dejado mi billetera*.
- **ir** — irregular; preterite (*fui/fuimos*) shared with *ser*; *ir a* + infinitive for near future.
- **ver** — irregular preterite (*vi/vimos*); past participle *visto*.
- **encontrar** — stem-changing (o→ue) present; lesson focuses on imperfect *encontraba*.
- **dormir** — stem-changing (o→ue present; o→u preterite); *dormí*.
- **almorzar** — stem-changing + z→c spelling shift in preterite yo (*almorcé*).
- **ganar** — regular -ar; lesson drills inverted word order *ganaron los colombianos* for emphasis.
- **reunirse** — regular reflexive; clitic attaches to infinitive (*reunirme/reunirnos*).
- **Key phrases:** *tener suerte* (to be lucky), *tener ganas de* + inf. (to be eager to), *ojalá que* + subjunctive (hopefully), *así que* (so/therefore), *a solo tres cuadras de aquí* (only three blocks from here).
- **Location adverbs:** *aquí* (here) → *ahí* (right there) → *allí* (over there).
