## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *gustar* with plural subjects and *les* (indirect object)**
When what is liked is plural, *gustar* becomes *gustan*. When referring to a third party (they/them), use *les*:

| Pronoun | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| me | 1st sg | *Me gusta la música.* / *Me gustan los juegos.* |
| le | 3rd sg | *Le gusta mirar cosas bonitas.* |
| les | 3rd pl / you-all | *Les gustó el concierto.* / *Les gustan los juegos.* |
| nos | 1st pl | *Nos gustó mucho.* |

**2. Preterite of *ir/ser* — *fui, fue, fuimos***
*Ir* (to go) and *ser* (to be) share identical preterite forms:

- *Mi amiga y yo fuimos a un concierto.* — My friend and I went to a concert.
- *Fue de música clásica.* — It was classical music.
- *Fui al cine.* — I went to the movies.

Context determines whether *fue/fuimos* means "went" or "was/were."

**3. Preterite of regular -ar verbs: *terminó*, *comenzó***
Regular -ar verbs in preterite 3rd person singular = stem + *-ó*:

- *Mi trabajo terminó.* — My job ended.
- *El concierto comenzó tarde.* — The concert started late.

**4. *podría* — conditional of *poder* (could)**
*Podría* = "could" (conditional); used to make polite requests or hypotheticals:

- *¿Podrías enviarme una tarjeta postal?* — Could you send me a postcard?
- *Podríamos ir juntos a un concierto.* — We could go to a concert together.

**5. *para mí* — "for me" / emphasis with *a mí***
*Para* + prepositional pronoun expresses purpose or benefit. Also used for emphasis in *gustar* constructions:

- *El taxi cuesta demasiado para mí.* — The taxi costs too much for me.
- *Para mí fue un día importante.* — For me it was an important day.

**6. Weather expressions with *hacer***
*Hacer* is used idiomatically for weather conditions:

- *Hace buen clima.* — The weather is good. (Lit. "It makes good climate.")
- *Hace mal clima.* — The weather is bad.
- *¿Cómo está el clima?* — How's the weather?

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Say something happened last [day] | *Fuimos a un concierto el jueves pasado.* |
| Ask if they (3rd pl) liked it | *¿Les gustó el concierto?* |

## Verb Conjugations

### terminar — to end / to finish
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: termino | nosotros: terminamos |
| 2nd | tú: terminas | vosotros: termináis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **termina** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: terminan |

Preterite drilled: *terminó* (*Mi trabajo terminó. / Terminó muy tarde.*)

---

## Summary

- **terminar** — regular -ar; preterite *terminó* drilled for completed events
- **comenzar** — stem-changing (e→ie) in present; preterite *comenzó* drilled
- **costar** — stem-changing (o→ue); *cuesta* used for price comparisons
- **enviar** — regular -ar (with accent pattern); *enviarme* drilled for postcard request
- **escribir** — regular -ir; infinitive drilled with *puedes*
- **ir** — irregular; preterite *fui / fuimos / fue* introduced for past events
