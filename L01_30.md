## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Formal register with *usted* — verbs and possessives**
Lesson 30 explicitly introduces formal register drills alongside informal. Key differences:
- Informal: *¿Puedes ayudarme?* → Formal: *¿Puede ayudarme?*
- Informal: *Puedo ayudarte.* → Formal: *Puedo ayudarle.*
- Informal: *con tu inglés* → Formal: *con su inglés*

The possessive *su* is the formal equivalent of *tu*.

**2. *Tal vez* — a second word for "maybe"**
Previously drilled: *quizá*. Lesson 30 introduces *tal vez* as an equally common synonym:
- *Tal vez un café con leche.* — Perhaps a coffee with milk.
- *Tal vez mañana.* — Perhaps tomorrow.

**3. *Entonces* — discourse connector**
Used to express "in that case" or "so then":
- *Entonces, mañana.* — In that case, tomorrow.
- *Entonces vamos a ir al teatro.* — In that case, we're going to go to the theater.

**4. *La gente* — singular collective noun**
*La gente* (people) is feminine and takes singular verbs, unlike English:
- *La gente va al cine.* — People go to the movies.
- *Hay demasiada gente.* — There are too many people. (lit. "there is too much people")

**5. *De acuerdo* — agreement expression**
- *De acuerdo.* — Agreed. / Okay.
Introduced as a standard way to agree to a plan.

**6. Time telling — *un cuarto para las…***
Latin American variant for "quarter to the hour":
- *Son las cinco menos cuarto.* — It's quarter to five. (Standard)
- *Es un cuarto para las cinco.* — It's a quarter to five. (Latin American)
Both are correct; this lesson uses the *para* construction throughout.

**7. *¿Cómo no?* — emphatic agreement**
- *¿Cómo no?* — Of course! / Certainly!
Lit. "how not?" — used as enthusiastic affirmation, especially in Latin America.

**8. *Prefiero* — stem-changing verb**
- *Prefiero el cine.* — I prefer the movies.
- *Prefiero ir al teatro con alguien.* — I prefer to go to the theater with someone.

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 30 explicitly contrasts both registers. The first half uses informal *tú*; the second half introduces a formal (*usted*) conversation with an elderly family friend. Key formal signals: *¿Puede ayudarme?*, *Puedo ayudarle*, *con su inglés*, *usted conoce*, *ha visto*.

### Pronunciation Notes
- *tal vez*: stress on second word — tal VEZ; the *z* in Latin America = /s/.
- *entonces*: four syllables — en-TON-ces; stress on second syllable.
- *alguien*: three syllables — AL-guien; *ie* is a diphthong.
- *prefiero*: four syllables — pre-FIER-o; the stem vowel *ie* is the diphthong from *e→ie*.
- *gente*: two syllables — HEN-te (Latin America) or HEN-te (Spain); *g* before *e/i* = /x/ (like an English "h").

## Verb Conjugations

### preferir — to prefer
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie) in all forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **prefiero** | nosotros: preferimos |
| 2nd | tú: prefieres | vosotros: preferís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: prefiere | ellos/ellas/Uds.: prefieren |

Lesson 30 drills: *prefiero* (I prefer) — *Prefiero el cine. / Prefiero ir al teatro.*

---

### comprar — to buy
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **compro** | nosotros: compramos |
| 2nd | tú: compras | vosotros: compráis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: compra | ellos/ellas/Uds.: compran |

Lesson 30 drills: *quiero comprar algo* (I want to buy something), *no sé qué comprar* (I don't know what to buy).

---

### ayudar — formal register object pronoun (*le*)
**Type:** regular -ar (see full table in Lesson 29)

Lesson 30 introduces the formal variant where *le* replaces *te*:
- Informal: *Puedo ayudarte.* (I can help you)
- Formal: *Puedo ayudarle.* (I can help you — Ud.)

---

### saber — to know (facts/how to)
**Type:** irregular 1st person singular (*sé*); otherwise regular

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **sé** | nosotros: sabemos |
| 2nd | tú: sabes | vosotros: sabéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: sabe | ellos/ellas/Uds.: saben |

Lesson 30 drills: *no sé qué comprar* (I don't know what to buy). Note: *saber* = knowing facts/how; *conocer* = knowing people/places.

---

### ir — present tense (vamos al teatro)
**Type:** highly irregular

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: voy | nosotros: **vamos** |
| 2nd | tú: vas | vosotros: vais |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: va | ellos/ellas/Uds.: van |

Lesson 30 drills: *vamos juntos al teatro* (we're going together to the theater), *la gente va al cine* (people go to the movies).

## Summary
- **preferir** — stem-changing (e→ie); drills *prefiero* (I prefer)
- **comprar** — regular -ar; drills *quiero comprar*, *no sé qué comprar*
- **ayudar** — regular -ar; formal register drill *puedo ayudarle* (with *le* for Ud.)
- **saber** — irregular 1st sg. (*sé*); drills *no sé qué comprar*
- **ir** — highly irregular; drills *vamos juntos*, *la gente va*
