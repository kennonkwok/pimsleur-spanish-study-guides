## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *sugerir que* + subjunctive — expressing suggestions**
*Sugerir* (to suggest) triggers the subjunctive in the clause that follows. This is one of the core subjunctive triggers (verbs of influence/desire/suggestion).

- *Le sugiero que lleve su paraguas.* — I suggest that you take your umbrella.
- *lleve* is the subjunctive form of *llevar* (3rd sg present subjunctive: *lleve*)

The present subjunctive of *llevar*: lleve, lleves, lleve, llevemos, llevéis, lleven.

**2. Preterite of *ser*: *fue* — "it was"**
*Ser* and *ir* share identical preterite forms. Context determines meaning.

| Person | ser / ir (preterite) |
|---|---|
| yo | fui |
| tú | fuiste |
| él/ella/Ud. | **fue** |
| nosotros | fuimos |
| vosotros | fuisteis |
| ellos/Uds. | fueron |

- *Fue bastante caro.* — It was quite expensive. (*ser*)
- *Fue a la tienda.* — He went to the store. (*ir*)

**3. *a menudo* — "often"**
A common frequency adverb. Typically placed at the beginning of the sentence or before the verb.

- *A menudo envío libros.* — I often send books.
- *A menudo le envío mensajes electrónicos a mi esposa.* — I often send email messages to my wife.

**4. Preterite of *enviar*: *envié* — "I sent"**
The preterite 1st singular adds accent *envié* to keep the *i* stressed (otherwise the diphthong *ie* would shift stress). 3rd singular: *envió*.

| Person | Preterite of *enviar* |
|---|---|
| yo | **envié** |
| tú | enviaste |
| él/ella/Ud. | **envió** |
| nosotros | enviamos |
| vosotros | enviasteis |
| ellos/Uds. | enviaron |

- *Le envié unos libros de historia a mi hijo.* — I sent my son some history books.
- *Ayer le envié un mensaje electrónico.* — Yesterday I sent her an email message.

**5. *estar resfriado/a* — "to have a cold"**
Uses *estar* (not *ser*) because it describes a temporary physical state. Agrees with the subject's gender.

- *Estoy resfriada.* — (woman) I have a cold.
- *Creo que estoy resfriado.* — (man) I think I have a cold.
- *Quizás está resfriada.* — Maybe you have a cold.

**6. Double object pronoun with *enviar*: *le envié unos libros a mi hijo***
When IO (*le*) and DO appear together with a preterite verb, the IO clitic comes before the verb. Adding *a mi hijo* clarifies the referent.

- *Le envié unos libros a mi hijo.* — I sent him some books (to my son).
- *Ayer le envié unas fotos.* — Yesterday I sent her some photos.

## Verb Conjugations

### sugerir — to suggest
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie/i) — e→ie in present indicative (except nosotros/vosotros); e→i in gerund/preterite

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **sugiero** | nosotros: sugerimos |
| 2nd | tú: **sugieres** | vosotros: sugerís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **sugiere** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **sugieren** |

Lesson drills: *Le sugiero que lleve su paraguas.* — 1st sg only.

## Summary

- **sugerir** — stem-changing (e→ie/i); triggers subjunctive: *Le sugiero que lleve...*
- **llamar** — regular -ar; *llamarla* / *llamarle* with clitic attached to infinitive
- **ser** (preterite) — irregular; *fue* = it was / he/she went (identical to *ir* preterite — context disambiguates)
- **enviar** (preterite) — regular -ar with accent: *envié* (I sent), *envió* (he/she sent); heavy IO + DO pronoun drilling
