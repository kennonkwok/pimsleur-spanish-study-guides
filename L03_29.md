## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *ojalá* + present subjunctive**
*Ojalá* (from Arabic *inshallah*) expresses hope. It always takes the present subjunctive:

- *Ojalá tengas algo para leer.* — Hopefully you'll have something to read. (*tengas* = subj. of *tener*)
- *Ojalá que sí.* — Let's hope so.
- *Ojalá pueda regresar el jueves.* — Hopefully I can return on Thursday.

**2. *que tiene(n) que ver con* — "that has/have to do with"**
A key new phrase for explaining relevance:

- *Cosas que tienen que ver con el viaje.* — Things that have to do with the trip.
- *Un problema que tiene que ver con el viaje.* — A problem that has to do with the trip.
- *Algo que tenga que ver con México.* — Something that has to do with Mexico. (subjunctive after indefinite *algo que*)

The subjunctive (*tenga*) appears when the antecedent is indefinite (not yet identified).

**3. *algo que* + subjunctive vs. indicative**
When describing an unidentified or hypothetical thing/person, Spanish uses the subjunctive:

- *¿Algo que tenga que ver con México?* — Something that has to do with Mexico? (hypothetical — we don't know what it is yet)
- Compare: *Esto es algo que tiene que ver con México.* — This is something that has to do with Mexico. (identified — indicative)

**4. *tener* + number + *años* — expressing age**
- *Elena tiene 14 años.* — Elena is 14 years old.
- *¿Cuántos años tiene?* — How old is she?
- *Son demasiado grandes.* — They're too old/big.

**5. *nadie* — "nobody"**
*Nadie* is a negative indefinite pronoun:
- *Nadie quiere CDs ahora.* — Nobody wants CDs now. (*nadie* before verb → no double negative needed)
- *No quiere nadie.* / *No lo sabe nadie.* — When *nadie* follows the verb, *no* is also required (double negative).

## Verb Conjugations

### leer — to read
**Type:** irregular in preterite (y inserted: *leyó*, *leyeron*); regular in present

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **leo** | nosotros: leemos |
| 2nd | tú: lees | vosotros: leéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: lee | ellos/ellas/Uds.: leen |

Preterite: *leí, leíste, **leyó**, leímos, leísteis, **leyeron***
Lesson drills: *ojalá tengas algo para leer*, *acabo de leer* (review).

---

### conocer — to know (people/places)
**Type:** *go*-verb irregularity in yo (*conozco*); regular otherwise

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|

| 2nd | tú: conoces | vosotros: conocéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: conoce | ellos/ellas/Uds.: conocen |

Lesson drills: *no los conozco* — I don't know them.

## Summary

- **leer** — irregular preterite (*leyó/leyeron*); *algo para leer* = something to read; *ojalá tengas algo para leer*
- **conocer** — *go*-verb (*conozco*); *no los conozco* = I don't know them (people)
- **durar** — regular -ar; *¿cuánto dura?* = how long does it last?; *duró dos días* = lasted two days
- **llevar** — regular -ar; *llevarles* = to take to them; *no sé qué llevarles*
