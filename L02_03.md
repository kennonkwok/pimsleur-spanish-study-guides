## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Present perfect: *haber* + past participle**
The present perfect expresses actions that have recently happened or have relevance to now. It is formed with the present tense of *haber* + past participle.

| Person | *haber* | Example |
|---|---|---|
| yo | he | *He tenido que venir sola.* — I've had to come alone. |
| tú | has | *Has tenido que venir solo.* — You've had to come alone. |
| él/ella | ha | *Ha tenido que trabajar mucho.* — He's had to work a lot. |
| nosotros | hemos | *Hemos llegado.* — We have arrived. |
| ellos/Uds. | han | *Han llegado.* — They have arrived. |

Key drills: *he tenido que venir sola*, *ha tenido que trabajar mucho*, *me ha dicho que ha tenido que trabajar mucho*.

**2. *saber* + infinitive = to know how to**
*Saber* followed by an infinitive means "to know how to" do something.
- *Sé manejar.* — I know how to drive.
- Contrast: *conocer* = to know a person or place; *saber* = to know a fact or how to do something.

**3. *gracias por* + infinitive**
"Thank you for [doing something]" uses *por* + infinitive.
- *Gracias por decirme.* — Thanks for telling me.
- *Gracias por ayudarme.* — Thanks for helping me.
- *Gracias por llevarme.* — Thanks for taking me.

**4. Reflexive/clitic object pronouns with infinitives (review)**
When the infinitive is the main verb, pronouns attach to its end:
- *Puedo llevarte.* — I can take you.
- *Puedo llevarlo.* — I can take him/it.
- *Prefiero ir más tarde.* → becomes *quiero llevarte* (take you)

**5. *no hay problema* — there's no problem**
*Hay* (from *haber*) = "there is / there are." *No hay problema* is a fixed expression meaning "no problem."

## Summary
- **alquilar** — regular -ar; key phrase: *necesito alquilar un carro*
- **llevar** — regular -ar; means to take someone somewhere or carry something; pronouns attach to infinitive (*llevarte*, *llevarlo*)
- **manejar** — regular -ar; Mexican Spanish for "to drive"; *sé manejar* = I know how to drive
- **saber** — irregular (*sé*); *saber* + inf. = to know how to; contrasts with *conocer*
- **venir** — irregular (*vengo*, e→ie); present perfect drilled: *he tenido que venir*
- **comprar** — regular -ar; *tengo que comprar muchas cosas*
