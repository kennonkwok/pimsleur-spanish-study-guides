## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *durar* — to last (duration)**
New verb introduced to ask and state how long something lasts. Works with *¿cuánto?* (how long / how much).

- *¿Cuánto va a durar la reunión?* — How long is the meeting going to last?
- *Va a durar mucho tiempo.* — It's going to last a long time.
- *La reunión de ayer duró cuatro horas.* — Yesterday's meeting lasted four hours.
- *La película de anoche duró tres horas.* — Last night's film lasted three hours.

Note: *¿cuánto tiempo?* is the full phrase; in this context *¿cuánto?* alone is sufficient and "tiempo" is understood.

---

**2. Conditional forms for polite requests: *quisiera*, *podría*, *me gustaría***
The lesson introduces the conditional mood as a politeness strategy — softer than the present indicative.

| Form | From | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| *quisiera* | *querer* | I would like / I wish |
| *podría* | *poder* | he/she/it could |
| *me gustaría* | *gustar* | I would like (reverse construction) |

- *Quisiera pedirte un favor.* — I wish to ask you a favor.
- *Así que podría durar más.* — So it could last longer.
- *Me gustaría un vaso de agua.* — I'd like a glass of water.
- *No me gustaría tomar apuntes.* — I wouldn't like to take notes.

---

**3. *pedir* vs. *preguntar* (to ask)**
Both mean "to ask" but are not interchangeable:

| Verb | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *pedir* | to ask for something (a request/favor) | *Quisiera pedirte un favor.* |
| *preguntar* | to ask a question | *Ella te pregunta.* |

---

**4. Indirect object clitic with *llevar*, *comprar*, *traer***
The lesson drills attaching indirect object clitics (*le*, *me*, *te*) to infinitives:

- *comprarle* — to buy for her/him: *Quiero comprarle un regalo.*
- *llevarle* — to take to her/him: *Quiero llevarle un regalo.*
- *traerme* — to bring me: *¿Puedes traerme agua?*

---

**5. *en lugar de* / *en mi lugar* (in place of / in my place)**
Two related uses of *lugar* drilled in this lesson:

- *en lugar de dos horas* — instead of two hours
- *en mi lugar* — in my place (on my behalf)
- *en tu lugar* — in your place

---

**6. Possessive construction with *de* for time references**
Spanish expresses "tomorrow's meeting" and "last night's film" as a *de* + time phrase:

- *la reunión de mañana* — tomorrow's meeting (lit. "the meeting of tomorrow")
- *la reunión de ayer* — yesterday's meeting
- *la película de anoche* — last night's film

---

**7. Preterite review: *escuché*, *quise*, *tuve*, *duró*, *se fueron*, *hablaron***
The lesson continues drilling the preterite for past-completed actions:

- *Escuché que la empresa tiene problemas.* — I heard that the company has problems.
- *No escuché lo que dijiste.* — I didn't hear what you said.
- *No tuve tiempo.* — I didn't have time.
- *Quise visitarla.* — I meant (intended) to visit her.
- *La reunión duró cuatro horas.* — The meeting lasted four hours.
- *No se quedaron.* — They didn't stay.
- *Se fueron.* — They left.
- *Hablaron entre ellos.* — They spoke among themselves.

---

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Say you need to buy a gift | *Necesito comprar un regalo.* |
| Say you want to take her a gift | *Quiero llevarle un regalo.* |
| Ask for a recommendation | *¿Qué regalo me recomiendas para ella?* |
| Describe a sick friend | *Una amiga que está enferma.* |
| Ask how long something will last | *¿Cuánto va a durar la reunión?* |
| State duration (present/future) | *Va a durar mucho tiempo. / Va a durar dos horas.* |
| State duration (past) | *Duró cuatro horas. / La película de anoche duró tres horas.* |
| Ask someone to do something in your place | *¿Puedes tomar apuntes en mi lugar?* |
| Say "instead of" | *En lugar de dos horas.* |
| Make a polite request with *quisiera* | *Quisiera pedirte un favor.* |
| Ask if someone can bring you something | *¿Puedes traerme agua, por favor?* |
| Say you would like something | *Me gustaría un vaso de agua.* |
| Say it could last longer | *Así que podría durar más.* |
| Reference yesterday's / tomorrow's event | *La reunión de ayer. / La reunión de mañana.* |
| Say you didn't hear what someone said | *No escuché lo que dijiste.* |
| Say the films are too noisy | *Las películas son demasiado ruidosas.* |
| Say you've seen too many films | *Hemos visto demasiadas películas.* |
| Confirm with "surely not" | *Seguro que no.* |
| Say frequently / they're interesting | *Con frecuencia son muy interesantes.* |

## Summary

- **durar** — regular -ar; key new verb for asking/stating duration; drilled in present, future (*ir a* + inf.), preterite, and conditional
- **regresar** — regular -ar; return / go back
- **llevar** — regular -ar; "to take [something] to [someone]"; drilled with indirect object clitic *le*
- **descansar** — regular -ar; to rest; drilled in infinitive and *nosotros* form
- **pedir** — stem-changing (e→i); to ask for/request (not to ask a question — use *preguntar* for that); drilled in *quisiera pedirte* construction
- **recomendar** — stem-changing (e→ie); to recommend
- **traer** — irregular (*go*-verb: *traigo*); to bring; drilled with indirect object clitic *me*
- **querer** — stem-changing (e→ie); to want; conditional *quisiera* introduced as polite softener
- **poder** — stem-changing (o→ue); can/able to; conditional *podría* introduced for "could"
