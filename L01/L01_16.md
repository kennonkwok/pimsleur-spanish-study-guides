## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *Nosotros* vs. *nosotras***
Spanish distinguishes gender in the first-person plural:
- *nosotros* — we/us (masculine or mixed group)
- *nosotras* — we/us (all-female group)

This is the first lesson to explicitly drill *nosotras*:
- *Nosotras queremos pagarlas.* — We (women) want to pay for them.

**2. *Queremos* — we want (1st person plural of *querer*)**
The nosotros form of *querer* is regular (no stem change):
- *Queremos comer juntos.* — We want to eat together.
- *Queremos tomar una cerveza.* — We want to have a beer.

**3. *Ella* — she (optional subject pronoun)**
Spanish omits subject pronouns unless needed for clarity or emphasis. This lesson introduces *ella* (she) as an optional emphatic subject:
- *Ella está aquí.* — She is here.
- *Ella también quiere una cerveza.* — She also wants a beer.

**4. Numbers 60–79**
- *sesenta* (60), *sesenta y uno* (61) … *sesenta y nueve* (69)
- *setenta* (70), *setenta y cinco* (75), *setenta y ocho* (78), *setenta y siete* (77)
- Pattern: *[tens] y [units]* — same as 30s and 40s

**5. *Solo / sola* — alone (gender agreement)**
The adjective agrees with the gender of the speaker/person it describes:
- *Voy solo.* — (a man speaking) I'm going alone.
- *Voy sola.* — (a woman speaking) I'm going alone.
- *Yo solo.* / *Yo sola.* — I alone. (emphatic)

**6. *Tenemos* — we have**
First-person plural of *tener*:
- *Tenemos una hora.* — We have one hour.
- *Tenemos muchos amigos.* — We have many friends.

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 16 uses **both** registers:
- Opening conversation: **formal** (*señor González, Buenos días, No tengo tiempo ahora*)
- Main practice: **informal** (*tú* forms: *tienes, quieres, vas, tu esposa*)
- The lesson explicitly models *tú y tu esposa* (informal you + your wife).

### Pronunciation Notes
- *Nosotros / nosotras*: no-SO-tros / no-SO-tras — stress on second syllable.
- *Juntos*: HOON-tos — *j* = /h/ sound.
- *Esposa / esposo*: es-PO-sa / es-PO-so — stress on second syllable.
- *Sesenta / setenta*: se-SEN-ta / se-TEN-ta — note the difference in the middle consonant.
- *Queremos*: ke-RE-mos — stress on second syllable; no stem change in nosotros form.

## Verb Conjugations

**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie); nosotros/vosotros keep *e*

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **quiero** | nosotros: **queremos** |
| 2nd | tú: **quieres** | vosotros: queréis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **quiere** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **quieren** |

Lesson 16 drills: *quiero*, *quieres*, *quiere*, **queremos** (new this lesson), *quieren*.
