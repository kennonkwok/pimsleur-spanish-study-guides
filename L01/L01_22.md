## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Demonstratives: *ese/esa/esos/esas* (that/those)**
Pimsleur distinguishes *este/esta/estos/estas* (this/these — near speaker) from *ese/esa/esos/esas* (that/those — near listener or in view).

- *Esa mujer* — that woman
- *Ese hombre* — that man
- *Esas personas* — those people
- *Esas tazas son grandes.* — Those cups are big.

**2. Tag questions with *verdad***
Add *¿verdad?* to the end of a statement to turn it into a confirmation question ("right?"):

- *Son norteamericanas, ¿verdad?* — They're American, right?

**3. Relative clause: *que* + verb**
*La mujer que ya se va* — The woman who is already leaving. *Que* connects the noun to a verb phrase describing it.

**4. Reflexive departure: *irse* / *se va***
*Se va este mes.* — He/She is leaving this month. The reflexive *se* signals a departure from a place.

**5. Telling time (supplement)**
- *¿Qué hora es?* — What time is it?
- *Son las + number* for most hours: *Son las tres.*
- *Es la una* for 1:00 (singular).
- Quarter/half: *y cuarto* (+ 15 min), *y media* (+ 30 min).
- Before the hour: *veinte para las ocho* (twenty to eight) or *un cuarto para las cuatro* (a quarter to four).

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Comment on a group's ability | *Las mujeres hablan muy bien.* |
| Tag question | *Son norteamericanas, ¿verdad?* |
| Express opinion / I think so | *Creo que sí.* / *Creo que no.* |
| Ask where someone is | *¿Dónde están ahora?* |
| Ask who someone is | *¿Quién es ese hombre/esa mujer?* |
| Describe someone leaving | *Se va este mes / esta semana.* |
| Say which one | *¿Cuál? La pequeña o la grande.* |
| Express need (yo / tú / nosotros) | *Necesito / Necesitas / Necesitamos un médico.* |
| Describe illness | *Está un poco enferma.* |

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 22 uses **both** registers. Formal (*usted*) appears in references to addressing strangers; informal (*tú*) dominates in drills between friends (*necesitas*, *crees*).

### Pronunciation Notes
- *Norteamericanas*: five syllables, stress on the *a* before *nas* — nor-te-a-me-ri-CA-nas.
- *Vacaciones*: four syllables, stress on third — va-ca-CIO-nes.
- *Quizá*: stress on final syllable — ki-ZÁ.

## Verb Conjugations

### hablar — to speak
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: hablo | nosotros: hablamos |
| 2nd | tú: hablas | vosotros: habláis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: habla | ellos/ellas/Uds.: hablan |

Lesson 22 drills: *hablan* (they speak), *hablo* (I speak — prior lessons).
