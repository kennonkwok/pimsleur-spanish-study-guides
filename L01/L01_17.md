## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *Ustedes* — plural you**
In Latin American Spanish, *ustedes* (abbreviated *Uds.*) is the only plural "you." It takes 3rd-person plural verb forms — the same as *ellos/ellas*.

- *¿Son ustedes de aquí?* — Are you (all) from here?
- *¿Quieren ustedes café?* — Do you (all) want coffee?

The subject *ustedes* is often dropped once established: *¿Quieren café?*

**2. *Nosotros* — we**
First-person plural subject pronoun. Verb endings for *nosotros* in -ar verbs end in *-amos*, in -er verbs in *-emos*, in -ir verbs in *-imos*.

- *Hablamos español.* — We speak Spanish.
- *Comemos juntos.* — We eat together.
- *Somos norteamericanos.* — We are North Americans.

**3. *Nuestro/a* — our**
Possessive adjective agreeing in gender and number with the noun it modifies (not with the owner).

- *nuestros amigos* — our friends (masculine/mixed plural)
- *nuestras amigas* — our friends (feminine plural)

**4. *Poder* + infinitive — to be able to / can**
*Poder* (o→ue stem-changing) followed by an infinitive expresses ability or possibility.

- *¿Puedes comer con nosotros?* — Can you eat with us?
- *Sí, podemos.* — Yes, we can.

**5. *Para* + infinitive — in order to**
*Para* followed by an infinitive expresses purpose.

- *Tenemos tiempo para comer.* — We have time (in order) to eat.
- *Para comprar algo.* — In order to buy something.

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Invite someone to eat with you | *¿Puedes comer con nosotros?* |
| State we eat together | *Comemos juntos.* |
| State we speak Spanish | *Hablamos español.* |
| State we are American | *Somos norteamericanos.* |
| Ask if they are from here (plural) | *¿Son ustedes de aquí?* |
| Answer where we are from | *Somos de Ecuador.* |
| Express maybe + noun | *Quizá café.* |
| Ask if we can buy something | *¿Podemos comprar algo?* |
| Ask about purpose with *para* | *¿Tenemos tiempo para comer?* |

### Formal vs. Informal
This lesson introduces both registers side by side:
- **Informal**: *¿Puedes comer con nosotros?* (tú form — with a friend)
- **Formal**: *¿Son ustedes de aquí?* (Uds. form — to a group)

### Pronunciation Notes
- *Nosotros*: stress on second syllable — no-SO-tros.
- *Nuestros*: the *ue* is a diphthong — one syllable, like "NWES-tros."
- *Cien*: a single syllable; do not add an extra vowel sound.
- *Juntos*: the *j* is a Spanish /x/ — a back-of-throat sound, not like English "j."
- *Quizá*: stress on final syllable — ki-ZA.

## Verb Conjugations

### comer — to eat
**Type:** regular -er

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: como | nosotros: **comemos** |
| 2nd | tú: comes | vosotros: coméis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: come | ellos/ellas/Uds.: comen |

Lesson 17 drills: *como* (I eat), *come* (he/she eats), *comemos* (we eat), *comen* (they eat).

---

### poder — to be able to / can
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue); irregular in no forms beyond stem change

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: puedo | nosotros: **podemos** |
| 2nd | tú: **puedes** | vosotros: podéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: puede | ellos/ellas/Uds.: pueden |

The stem *o* changes to *ue* in all forms except *nosotros* and *vosotros*.
Lesson 17 drills: *puedes* (can you), *podemos* (we can).

---

### saber — to know (a fact or how to do something)
**Type:** irregular (*yo* form); otherwise regular -er

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **sé** | nosotros: **sabemos** |
| 2nd | tú: sabes | vosotros: sabéis |
| 3rd | él/sella/Ud.: sabe | ellos/ellas/Uds.: saben |

The accent on *sé* distinguishes it from *se* (reflexive pronoun).
Lesson 17 drills: *sabes* (you know), *sabemos* (we know).

## Summary
- **comer** — regular -er; *comemos* = we eat
- **poder** — stem-changing (o→ue); *podemos/puedes*
- **saber** — irregular *yo* (*sé*); otherwise regular -er
- **hablar** — regular -ar; *hablamos* = we speak
- **comprar** — regular -ar; used in *para comprar algo*
- **ser** — irregular; *somos* = we are, *son* = they are / you (pl.) are
