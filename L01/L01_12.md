## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Near future: *ir a + infinitive***
This is the most common way to express future plans in spoken Spanish.

- *Yo voy a pagar.* — I'm going to pay.
- *Vamos a comer.* — We're going to eat. / Let's eat.
- *No vamos a comer hoy.* — We're not going to eat today.

The formula: conjugate *ir* in present tense + *a* + infinitive (unchanged).

**2. Telling time: *a las…* and *son las…***
- To say "at what time": *¿A qué hora?*
- To say "at [time]": *a las cinco* (at five o'clock); *a la una* (at one o'clock — singular)
- Half hour: *a las seis y media* (at six thirty)
- Quarter past: *a las ocho y cuarto* (at eight fifteen)
- Quarter to: *a las cinco menos cuarto* (at quarter to five)
- "Today": *hoy*; "tomorrow": *mañana*

**3. Impersonal *hay* (there is / there are)**
*Hay* is invariable — one form covers both singular and plural:
- *No hay tiempo.* — There's no time.
- *No hay refrescos.* — There are no sodas.

**4. *Gustar* construction**
*Gustar* works "backwards" from English — the thing liked is the grammatical subject:
- *Me gusta comprar cosas.* — I like to buy things. (Lit.: buying things pleases me)
- *Me gusta tomar agua.* — I like to drink water.

**5. Emphatic negation: *hoy no / mañana no***
Spanish can place *no* after a time word for emphasis:
- *Hoy no.* — Not today.
- *Mañana no puedo.* — Tomorrow I can't.

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Want/like + infinitive | *Quiero pagar.* / *Me gusta comprar.* |
| Going to + infinitive | *Voy a pagar.* / *Vamos a comer.* |
| Can + infinitive | *Puedes pagar.* / *No puedo comer.* |
| At what time? | *¿A qué hora puedes comer?* |
| Time answer | *A las cinco.* / *A las ocho y media.* |
| There is/are | *Hay tiempo.* / *No hay refresco.* |
| "Not today/tomorrow" | *Hoy no.* / *Mañana no puedo.* |
| Buy/eat something | *Voy a comprar un refresco.* |
| Numbers 11–15 | *once, doce, trece, catorce, quince* |

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 12 uses **informal** register throughout (*tú* forms: *quieres, puedes, compras, vas*). Formal *usted* forms are reviewed at the end when practiced alongside *tú*.

### Pronunciation Notes
- *Panadería*: stress on the *í* — pa-na-de-RÍ-a. The accent mark is required.
- *Mañana*: the *ñ* is a distinct letter (ny sound). Contrast with *manana* (no such word).
- *A las cinco*: the words run together in speech — sounds like *a-LAS-cin-co*.
- *Y media*: *media* ends with the same vowel as *la* — they link smoothly.

## Verb Conjugations

### tomar — to take / to drink / to have
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: tomo | nosotros: tomamos |
| 2nd | tú: tomas | vosotros: tomáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: toma | ellos/ellas/Uds.: toman |

Lesson 12 drills: *tomo* (I drink/have), *tomas* (you drink), *toma* (he/she has).

### gustar — to please / to like
**Type:** regular -ar (but used with indirect object pronouns)

## Summary
- **tomar** — regular -ar (to take/drink/have)
- **comprar** — regular -ar (to buy)
- **pagar** — regular -ar (to pay)
- **querer** — stem-changing (e→ie); nosotros/vosotros keep *e*
- **poder** — stem-changing (o→ue); nosotros/vosotros keep *o*
- **comer** — regular -er (to eat)
- **gustar** — regular -ar; used with indirect object pronouns (*me, te, le…*)
- **ir** — irregular; memorize all 6 forms; *ir a + inf.* = near future
