## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Prepositional pronouns: *contigo, conmigo***
After prepositions, Spanish uses special pronoun forms:
- *conmigo* — with me
- *contigo* — with you (informal)
- *con él / con ella / con usted* — with him / her / you (formal)
- *con nosotros* — with us

*Quiero hablar contigo.* — I want to talk with you.

**2. *Nosotros* as subject and object**
- *Nosotros* = we/us (masc. or mixed group)
- *Nosotras* = we/us (all-female group)
- *Vamos juntos, nosotros.* — We're going together.
- *Vamos a tomar juntos.* — We're going to drink together.

**3. *¿Qué vamos a hacer?* — What are we going to do?**
*Hacer* (to do/make) introduced in the near-future construction:
- *¿Qué vamos a hacer?* — What are we going to do?
- *No vamos a hacer nada.* — We're not going to do anything.
- Double negation (*no … nada*) is grammatically required in Spanish.

**4. *Demasiado* — too much**
- *Es demasiado.* — It's too much.
- *Cuesta demasiado dinero.* — It costs too much money.
- *Carlos toma demasiado café.* — Carlos drinks too much coffee.

**5. Numbers: 20–59**
- 20s: *veinte* → *veintiuno, veintidós …veintinueve*
- 31+: *treinta y uno, treinta y dos …*
- 50: *cincuenta*; 55: *cincuenta y cinco*
- Before a masculine noun, *uno* shortens to *un*: *veintiún minutos*

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 15 uses **informal** register throughout (*tú* forms: *tienes, puedes, hablas*). The character's dialogue explicitly teaches *tu y yo* and *tú* forms as the default register for peer conversation.

### Pronunciation Notes
- *Contigo*: con-TI-go — stress on second syllable. The *g* is soft (not like English hard "g").
- *Demasiado*: de-ma-SIA-do — four syllables.
- *Juntos*: HOON-tos — the *j* sounds like English *h*.
- *Nosotros*: no-SO-tros — stress on second syllable.
- *¿Qué?* vs. *que*: *¿Qué?* (what?) has an accent; *que* (that/which) does not.

## Verb Conjugations

### tener — to have
**Type:** irregular (*go* verb in yo; e→ie stem-changing)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **tengo** | nosotros: tenemos |
| 2nd | tú: **tienes** | vosotros: tenéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **tiene** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **tienen** |

Lesson 15 drills: *tengo* (I have), *tienes* (you have), *tiene* (he has).

## Summary
- **tener** — irregular (*tengo*, e→ie stem-change); to have
- **hacer** — irregular yo (*hago*); to do/make
- **saber** — irregular yo (*sé*); to know a fact
- **hablar** — regular -ar; to speak/talk
- **querer** — stem-changing (e→ie); to want
- **poder** — stem-changing (o→ue); to be able to / can
- **ir** — irregular; memorize all 6 forms
- **tomar** — regular -ar; to take/drink/have
