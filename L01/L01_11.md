## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *poder* — to be able to / can**
*Poder* is a stem-changing verb (o→ue) used before an infinitive to express ability:
- *Puedo pagar.* — I can pay.
- *Puedes comer algo frío.* — You can eat something cold.
- *Usted puede.* — You can. (formal)
- Negation: *No puedo pagar.* — I can't pay.

**2. *hay* — there is / there are**
*Hay* is a special form of *haber* used to express existence. It is invariable — it does not change whether the subject is singular or plural:
- *Hay trece.* — There are thirteen.
- *¿Hay tiempo?* — Is there time?
- *No hay nada.* — There's nothing.

**3. *estar* + adjective — temporary states**
*Frío/fría* with *estar* describes a temporary temperature state:
- *No está frío.* — It's not cold. (the soda isn't cold right now)
- Compare with *ser* which would imply a permanent quality.
- *El agua está muy fría.* — The water is very cold. (*estar* because it is a temporary state)

**4. *si* (if) clauses — conditional**
The word *si* (no accent) introduces a condition:
- *Si puedo, voy a pagar.* — If I can, I'm going to pay.
- *Puedes pagar si quieres.* — You can pay if you want.
- Do NOT confuse with *sí* (yes, with accent).

**5. Adjective position — noun + adjective vs. adjective + noun**
In Spanish, adjectives usually follow nouns, but some common adjectives (*bueno/a*, *mucho/a*, *poco/a*) can precede:
- *Una concha buena.* / *Una buena concha.* — both acceptable
- *Cosas buenas.* — good things (adjective after noun, most neutral)
- *Muchas cosas.* — many things (quantity adjective precedes)

**6. *comprar* — to buy; *compro/compras***
A new regular -ar verb introduced this lesson with full informal conjugation practiced:
- *Yo compro.* — I buy.
- *Tú compras muchas cosas.* — You buy many things.

**7. *le gusta* — formal/3rd person "likes"**
Contrast with *me gusta* (I like) and *te gusta* (you like, informal):
- *Le gusta la panadería.* — He/she/you (formal) likes the bakery.

**8. Time-of-day greetings — full set**
All four greetings consolidated this lesson:
- *Buenos días* — Good morning (until ~noon)
- *Buenas tardes* — Good afternoon (~noon to ~8pm)
- *Buenas noches* — Good evening/night (~8pm onward)
- *Buenas* — informal shortening (any time of day)

### Formal vs. Informal
Both registers appear. Informal (*tú*): *¿Tienes algo frío? / Puedes comer.* Formal (*usted*): *Usted puede. / Le gusta.* The lesson also introduces the formal 3rd-person *le gusta* as a reading item.

### Pronunciation Notes
- *Frío*: two syllables — *FRÍ-o*; the accent mark indicates the *í* is stressed and forms its own syllable.
- *Puedes*: *PWE-des*; the *ue* diphthong collapses into one syllable.
- *Panadería*: stress on *rí* — *pa-na-de-RÍ-a*; note the accent.
- *Hay*: pronounced like English "eye" — *ay*; rhymes with *¡ay!*
- *Concha*: *CON-cha*; the *ch* is like English "ch" in "church."
- *Buenas* (shortening): informal; the dropped word (días/tardes/noches) is understood from context/time.

## Verb Conjugations

### poder — to be able to / can
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue)
The stem vowel *o* changes to *ue* in all forms except *nosotros* and *vosotros*.

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **puedo** | nosotros: podemos |
| 2nd | tú: **puedes** | vosotros: podéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **puede** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: pueden |

Lesson 11 drills: *Puedo pagar.* / *No puedo pagar.* / *Puedes comer algo frío.* / *Usted puede.*

## Summary
- **poder** — stem-changing (o→ue); yo: *puedo*; used before infinitive to express ability
- **comprar** — regular -ar; yo: *compro*
- **ir** — irregular; yo: *voy*, tú: *vas*, nosotros: *vamos*; *ir a* + infinitive = near future
- **estar** — irregular; used for temporary states (*no está frío*) and location
- **tener** — irregular (go-verb); yo: *tengo*
- **querer** — stem-changing (e→ie); yo: *quiero*
- **tomar** — regular -ar; to drink (beverages)
- **pagar** — regular -ar; to pay
- **gustar** — regular -ar but used with indirect object pronouns; *me/te/le gusta*
