## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *Tengo que irme* — reflexive infinitive with obligation**
*Irse* (to leave/go away) is a reflexive verb. The pronoun attaches to the infinitive:
- *Tengo que irme.* — I have to leave.
- *Tenemos que irnos.* — We have to leave.

**2. Direct object pronouns attached to infinitive — extended practice**
The pronoun can attach to the infinitive (after *poder*, *querer*, *tener que*, *ir a*):
- *Puedes ayudarme.* — Can you help me?
- *Sí, puedo ayudarte.* — Yes, I can help you.
- *Voy a ayudarla.* — I'm going to help her.
- *Voy a ayudarlo.* — I'm going to help him.

Gender agreement matters: *la* (feminine object), *lo* (masculine object).

**3. *¿Qué tal?* — casual greeting**
Interchangeable with *¿Cómo estás?* in informal contexts. The response is *bien* or *tal* (= about the same, so-so). Introduced as a high-frequency informal phrase.

**4. *Con mucho gusto* — accepting an invitation**
The standard polite response to an invitation or request for help. Lit. "with much pleasure." Equivalent to English "I'd love to" or "Gladly."

**5. *Hasta pronto* — leave-taking**
- *Hasta pronto.* — See you soon. (lit. "until soon")
- Contrasts with *hasta luego* (see you later) and *hasta mañana* (see you tomorrow).

**6. *No puedo decirte* — negated modal + infinitive-pronoun**
- *No puedo decirte.* — I can't tell you.
- *Puedo decirte más tarde.* — I can tell you later.
The pronoun glues onto the infinitive; it does NOT go between *no* and *puedo*.

**7. *En un momentito* — diminutive for softening**
The *-ito* suffix added to *momento* conveys "just a brief moment." Common in conversational Spanish.

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 29 uses the **informal** *tú* register throughout. One note: the phone call scene demonstrates that *-mos* endings (*tenemos*, *podemos*) are first person plural (nosotros), not a formality marker.

### Pronunciation Notes
- *ayudar*: four syllables — a-yu-DAR; stress on final syllable.
- *momentito*: five syllables — mo-men-TI-to; the *-ito* suffix carries secondary stress.
- *nuestra*: two syllables — NUES-tra; the *ue* is a diphthong.
- *hasta pronto*: two words; spoken fluidly — AS-ta-PRON-to.

## Verb Conjugations

### ayudar — to help
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **ayudo** | nosotros: ayudamos |
| 2nd | tú: **ayudas** | vosotros: ayudáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: ayuda | ellos/ellas/Uds.: ayudan |

Lesson 29 drills: *puedes ayudarme* (can you help me), *puedo ayudarte* (I can help you), *voy a ayudarla / ayudarlo* (I'm going to help her/him).

## Summary
- **ayudar** — regular -ar; drills with clitic pronouns attached: *ayudarme, ayudarte, ayudarla, ayudarlo*
- **escribir** — regular -ir; drills *tengo que escribir*
- **decir** — irregular; irregular past participle *dicho*; drills *no puedo decirte, ella ha dicho*
- **irse** — reflexive of irregular *ir*; drills *tengo que irme, ya me voy*
- **estar** — irregular; present perfect drills *han estado, ha estado*
