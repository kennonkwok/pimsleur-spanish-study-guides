## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Emphatic subject pronouns: *yo* and *usted***
In Spanish, subject pronouns are normally dropped because the verb ending signals the subject. However, they are retained (or added) for **emphasis or contrast**:

- *Yo soy de Estados Unidos.* — *I* am from the United States. (emphasizing "I, not someone else")
- *Usted no es de aquí.* — *You* are not from here. (emphasizing "you specifically")

The lesson explicitly drills this with the instruction to "pay attention to intonation."

**2. *Querer* — "to want"**
A new e→ie stem-changing verb:
- *quiero* — I want
- *quieres* — you want (informal)
- *quiere* — you want (formal) / he, she wants

Pattern: *Quiero doscientos pesos.* — I want two hundred pesos.

**3. Numbers: dos, tres, cien/ciento, doscientos, trescientos**
Lesson 6 introduces the first cardinal numbers:
- *dos* — 2
- *tres* — 3
- *cien* — 100 (used before a noun: *cien pesos*)
- *doscientos* — 200 (lit. "two hundreds")
- *trescientos* — 300 (lit. "three hundreds")

**4. *Mucho / muchos / muchas* — "much / many"**
This adjective must agree in gender and number with the noun it modifies:
- *No mucho.* — Not much. (invariable adverb form)
- *No tengo muchos pesos.* — I don't have many pesos. (masc. pl.)
- *Muchas gracias.* — Thank you very much. (fem. pl.)

**5. *Cuántos* — "how many"**
Interrogative adjective that agrees with the noun: *¿Cuántos pesos?* — How many pesos?

**6. *En* + place — location**
The preposition *en* means "in" or "at" for location:
- *Tengo dólares en el hotel.* — I have dollars in the hotel.
- *Nos vemos en el restaurante.* — See you in the restaurant.

**7. Informal verb forms now active**
Lesson 6 is the first lesson to actively drill informal *tú* verb forms in conversation:
- *¿Entiendes?* — Do you understand? (informal)
- *¿Cuántos quieres?* — How many do you want? (informal)

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Emphatic "I" | *Yo soy de Estados Unidos.* |
| Say I want | *Quiero doscientos pesos.* |
| Ask how many | *¿Cuántos pesos tienes?* |
| Say not much / very little | *No mucho.* / *Muy poco.* |
| Say it's not much | *No es mucho.* |
| Say many thanks | *Muchas gracias.* |
| State location | *Tengo dólares en el hotel.* |
| Confirm understanding (informal) | *¿Entiendes?* |
| Casual farewell with location | *Nos vemos en el restaurante.* |

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 6 actively uses **both registers** in the same scenarios:
- Formal: *¿Tiene usted pesos?* / *¿Sabe usted?*
- Informal: *¿Tienes pesos?* / *¿Sabes?* / *¿Entiendes?* / *¿Quieres?*

### Pronunciation Notes
- *Cuántos*: stress on first syllable: *CUÁN-tos*. Accent mark required.
- *Doscientos*: four syllables: *dos-CIEN-tos*; the *ci* before *e* sounds like "s" (in Latin American Spanish) or "th" (in Castilian).
- *Muchas gracias*: run smoothly — *MU-chas GRA-cias*.
- *¿Cómo?*: when used as "pardon/huh?", it is a rising-intonation one-word question, not the same as *¿Cómo está?*
