## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *Voy a* + infinitive with attached pronoun**
When a d.o. pronoun is used with a two-verb construction (*ir a* + infinitive), the pronoun can attach to the infinitive:

- *Voy a verlo.* — I'm going to see him. (*lo* attached to *ver*)
- *Quiero verlo también.* — I want to see him too.

**2. *Contigo* and *conmigo* — special prepositional forms**
The preposition *con* (with) has irregular forms when combined with personal pronouns:
- *conmigo* — with me (not *con mí*)
- *contigo* — with you (informal, not *con ti*)
- *consigo* — with himself/herself/themselves (reflexive)
All other: *con él, con ella, con nosotros, con ustedes*

**3. Directions: right, left, straight ahead**
- *a la derecha* — to the right
- *a la izquierda* — to the left
- *derecho* — straight ahead
- *sigue/siga* — continue (tú/Ud. command of *seguir*)
- *siempre derecho* — always straight ahead

**4. *Poder* + infinitive — expressing ability**
- *Puedo ir contigo.* — I can go with you.
- *Puede venir conmigo.* — You can come with me. (formal)
- Construction: *poder* (conjugated) + bare infinitive.

**5. Formal invitation: *Puede venir conmigo***
The formal "you can come with me" uses *puede* (Ud. form of *poder*). The lesson drills switching between formal (*puede venir*) and requesting permission formally (*¿Puedo ir con usted?*).

**6. *¿Cómo se llama tu amigo?* — asking someone else's name**
*Llamarse* in 3rd person: *Se llama Sebastián.* — His name is Sebastián.

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 25 mixes registers. Informal (*tú*): *conoces*, *tienes*, *dime*, *sigue*, *contigo*. Formal (*usted*): *puede venir conmigo*, *¿Puedo ir con usted?* The lesson explicitly notes to use formal when first introduced to someone.

### Pronunciation Notes
- *Conmigo*: three syllables — con-MI-go.
- *Contigo*: three syllables — con-TI-go.
- *Izquierda*: iz-QUIER-da; the *qu* is /k/.
- *Siempre*: SIEM-pre; *ie* is a diphthong.
- *Seguimos*: se-GUI-mos; *gui* = /gi/.

## Summary
- **conocer** — irregular yo *conozco*; otherwise regular -er
- **ver** — irregular; yo *veo*
- **ir** — irregular; yo *voy*; used in *ir a* + infinitive construction
- **poder** — stem-changing (o→ue)
- **seguir** — stem-changing (e→i); yo *sigo*
- **vivir** — regular -ir
- **venir** — irregular; yo *vengo*; tú *vienes* (e→ie)
- **querer** — stem-changing (e→ie); irregular yo *quiero*
