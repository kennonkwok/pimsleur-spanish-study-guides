## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *Verdad* — true / right (tag question and affirmation)**
*¿Verdad?* appended to a statement functions like English "right?" or "isn't it?"
- *Habla más rápido, ¿verdad?* — He speaks faster, right?
- *Sí, es verdad.* — Yes, that's right/true.
- *Es verdad también.* — That's also true.

**2. Proximal demonstratives: *este/esta* — this**
*Este* modifies masculine nouns; *esta* modifies feminine nouns. Both refer to something near the speaker.
- *este hombre* — this man
- *esta persona* — this person (always feminine noun)
- *esta semana* — this week
- *este mes* — this month

*Nota:* The word *persona* is always grammatically feminine even when referring to a man.

**3. Comparatives: *más…que* — more…than**
Place *más* before the adjective/adverb and *que* before the compared element:
- *Más rápido que yo.* — Faster than I (am).
- *Más rápido que tú.* — Faster than you.
- *Más rápido que nosotros.* — Faster than we (are).
- *Más despacio.* — More slowly. (comparative without *que* when nothing is compared)

**4. *Demasiado* — too / too much**
As an adverb (modifying a verb or adjective), *demasiado* is invariable:
- *Habla demasiado rápido.* — He speaks too fast.
- *Compro demasiado.* — I buy too much.
- *Habla demasiado.* — He talks too much.

**5. *Dime* — tell me**
*Dime* is the *tú* affirmative command of *decir* (*di*) + the indirect object pronoun *me* (me). It is a fixed informal phrase meaning "tell me" or "go ahead."
- *Dime, quiero saber.* — Tell me, I want to know.
- *Dime, ¿quién es este hombre?* — Tell me, who is this man?

**6. *Despacio* vs. *lentamente***
The lesson uses *despacio* (a common colloquial adverb for "slowly") rather than *lentamente* (the adverb derived from *lento*). Both are correct; *despacio* is more common in everyday speech throughout Latin America.

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Tag question | *¿Verdad?* / *Es verdad.* |
| Say someone speaks too fast | *Hablas demasiado rápido.* |
| Compare speed | *Más rápido que yo / tú / nosotros.* |
| Use proximal demonstratives | *Este hombre.* / *Esta persona.* |
| Use distal demonstratives | *Ese hombre.* / *Esa persona.* |
| Give a gentle command | *Dime.* / *Dime, quiero saber.* |
| State going tomorrow | *Me voy mañana.* |
| State going to Argentina | *Voy a Argentina.* |
| Say I buy too much | *Compro demasiado.* |

### Formal vs. Informal
- *Dime* is informal (tú command)
- Formal equivalent: *Dígame* (Ud. command of *decir*)
- *¿Habla despacio?* (formal) vs. *¿Hablas despacio?* (informal)

### Pronunciation Notes
- *Verdad*: the final *d* is soft/silent in casual speech — "ver-DA(th)."
- *Despacio*: four syllables — des-PA-cio; the *cio* is a diphthong (one syllable).
- *Rápido*: stress on first syllable — RÁ-pi-do; three syllables.
- *Demasiado*: five syllables — de-ma-SIA-do; *ia* is a diphthong.
- *Dime*: stress on first syllable — DI-me.

## Verb Conjugations

### decir — to say, to tell
**Type:** irregular (go-verb; e→i stem change)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **digo** | nosotros: decimos |
| 2nd | tú: dices | vosotros: decís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: dice | ellos/ellas/Uds.: dicen |

*Dime* (tell me) = *di* (tú command) + *me*.
Lesson 20 drills: *dime* (tell me), *dice* (he/she says).

---

### hablar — to speak
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: hablo | nosotros: hablamos |
| 2nd | tú: **hablas** | vosotros: habláis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **habla** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: hablan |

Lesson 20 drills: *hablas rápido* (you speak fast), *habla despacio* (he/she speaks slowly), *habla demasiado* (he/she talks too much).

### irse — to leave, to go away
**Type:** irregular (reflexive)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **me voy** | nosotros: nos vamos |
| 2nd | tú: **te vas** | vosotros: os vais |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **se va** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **se van** |

Lesson 20 drills: *me voy mañana* (I'm leaving tomorrow), *te vas solo* (you're leaving alone), *se va de vacaciones* (he/she is going on vacation), *van todos juntos* (they're all going together).

---

### gustar — to please / to like
**Type:** regular -ar, but used with indirect object pronouns (reverse construction)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | (a mí) me gusta / gustan | (a nosotros) nos gusta / gustan |
| 2nd | (a ti) te gusta / gustan | (a vosotros) os gusta / gustan |
| 3rd | (a él/ella/Ud.) le gusta / gustan | (a ellos/Uds.) les gusta / gustan |

*Gusta* when the thing liked is singular; *gustan* when plural.
Lesson 20 drills: *me gusta Buenos Aires* (I like Buenos Aires), *me gusta más* (I like it more), *no me gusta* (I don't like it).

---

## Summary
- **decir** — highly irregular (*digo/dices/dice/decimos/decís/dicen*); *dime* = tell me (tú command + *me*)
- **hablar** — regular -ar; *hablas rápido*, *habla demasiado*
- **comprar** — regular -ar; *compro demasiado*
- **ir** — irregular; *voy a Argentina*
- **irse** — reflexive departure; *me voy mañana*, *te vas solo*, *se va / se van*
- **gustar** — reverse construction; *me gusta* = I like; subject is the thing liked
