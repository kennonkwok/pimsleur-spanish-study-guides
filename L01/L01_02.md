## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *Un poco de* + noun**
When *poco* is followed by a specific noun, the preposition *de* is required.

- *Entiendo un poco de español.* — I understand a little Spanish. (lit. a little *of* Spanish)
- *Hablo un poco de español.* — I speak a little Spanish.

Without a noun, *de* is dropped: *Entiendo un poco.* — I understand a little.

**2. Gender agreement: *norteamericano* vs. *norteamericana***
Spanish adjectives agree in gender with the noun or pronoun they describe. The *-o* ending is masculine; *-a* is feminine.

- *Usted es norteamericano.* — (speaking to a man)
- *Usted es norteamericana.* — (speaking to a woman)

**3. *Señora* — addressing married/older women**
Lesson 2 introduces *señora* alongside the already-known *señorita* and *señor*. These titles are used without first names when addressing strangers formally.

**4. *Ser* vs. *estar*: permanent vs. temporary**
The lesson explicitly notes that Spanish has two verbs for "to be":
- *Ser* (*es*) → permanent or inherent traits: nationality, identity.
- *Estar* (*está*) → temporary states or conditions: health, location.

- *Usted es norteamericano.* — You are North American. (*ser* — identity)
- *¿Cómo está usted?* — How are you? (*estar* — current condition)

**5. *Buenos días* — gender of *día***
*Día* is masculine despite ending in *-a*, so the adjective takes *buenos* (masc. pl.) not *buenas*.

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Polite attention-getter | *Disculpe, señora.* |
| Introduce new vocabulary: *un poco de* | *Hablo un poco de español.* / *Entiendo un poco de español.* |
| Ask / answer: how are you | *¿Cómo está?* / *Bien, gracias.* / *Muy bien, gracias.* |
| Say / ask: speak Spanish? | *Habla usted español.* / *No hablo español.* |
| Say good morning | *Buenos días, señora.* |
| Identify nationality (m./f.) | *Es usted norteamericano.* / *Es usted norteamericana.* |
| Say goodbye | *Adiós, señorita.* |

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 2 uses only **formal** register throughout — *usted*, *señor*, *señora*, *señorita*. Informal *tú* is not introduced yet.

### Pronunciation Notes
- *Buenos días*: *Buenos* ends with a linking *-s* that blends into *días* in natural speech.
- *Muy bien*: the diphthong *uy* in *muy* is a single syllable; do not separate into two.
- *Señora* vs. *señorita*: the *ñ* is a distinct sound ("ny"); pay attention to the suffix difference between the two forms.
- *Norteamericana*: five syllables; stress on the fourth: *nor-te-a-me-ri-CA-na*.

## Verb Conjugations

### hablar — to speak
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **hablo** | nosotros: hablamos |
| 2nd | tú: hablas | vosotros: habláis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **habla** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: hablan |

Lesson 2 drills: *hablo* (I speak), *habla* (you/she speaks — in questions *¿Habla usted español?*).

---

### entender — to understand
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie)
The stem vowel *e* changes to *ie* in all forms **except** *nosotros* and *vosotros*.

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **entiendo** | nosotros: entendemos |
| 2nd | tú: entiendes | vosotros: entendéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **entiende** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: entienden |

Lesson 2 drills: *entiendo* (I understand), *entiende* (you understand).

---

## Summary
- **disculpar** — regular -ar (lesson uses Ud. command *Disculpe*)
- **entender** — stem-changing (e→ie); nosotros/vosotros keep original stem
- **estar** — irregular; used for temporary states; lesson drills *está*
- **hablar** — regular -ar
- **ser** — irregular; memorize all 6 forms
