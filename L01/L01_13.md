## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Direct object pronouns attached to infinitives**
When a direct object pronoun (*lo, la, los, las*) refers to a noun, it agrees in gender and number and attaches to the end of the infinitive:

- *Comerla* — to eat it (referring to *la concha*, fem. sg.)
- *Comprarla* — to buy it (fem. sg.)
- *Comprarlas* — to buy them (referring to *las conchas*, fem. pl.)
- *Pagarlas* — to pay for them (fem. pl.)
- *Tomarlos* — to drink them (referring to *los cafés*, masc. pl.)

The key rule: pronoun gender/number matches the noun it replaces.

**2. Numbers 16–19**
Sixteen through nineteen are formed by contracting *diez y …* into a single word:
- *dieciséis* (16), *diecisiete* (17), *dieciocho* (18), *diecinueve* (19)

Note: *dieciséis* has an accent on the *é*.

**3. Quarter-hour time expressions**
- *y cuarto* = quarter past → *a las once y cuarto* (at 11:15)
- *menos cuarto* = quarter to → *a las doce menos cuarto* (at 11:45)

**4. *Costar* (to cost) — stem-changing**
- *¿Cuánto cuesta una concha?* — How much does one concha cost?
- *Cuestan mucho.* — They cost a lot.

**5. *Beber* vs. *tomar***
Both mean "to drink." *Tomar* is more colloquial in Latin America and also means "to take/have." Both are introduced this lesson.

| I'm going to buy them | *Voy a comprarlas.* |
| We're going to drink them | *Vamos a tomarlos.* |

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 13 uses **informal** register (*tú* forms: *estás, quieres, puedes*). The opening conversation between María and a friend models informal daily speech.

### Pronunciation Notes
- *Dieciséis*: accent on *é* — die-ci-SÉIS. The *s* at the end is voiced/final.
- *Comerla, comprarla*: the pronoun attaches without a space or pause — practice saying it as one word.
- *¿Cuánto cuesta?*: *cu-AN-to CUES-ta* — stress on first syllable of *cuánto* and first syllable of *cuesta*.
- *Cuarto*: sounds like KWAR-to. Distinguish from *cuatro* (KWAH-tro = four).

## Summary
- **querer** — stem-changing (e→ie); nosotros/vosotros keep *e*
- **poder** — stem-changing (o→ue); nosotros/vosotros keep *o*
- **comer** — regular -er (to eat)
- **tomar** — regular -ar (to take/drink/have)
- **beber** — regular -er (to drink); synonym of *tomar* for beverages
- **costar** — stem-changing (o→ue); typically used in 3rd person only
- **saber** — irregular yo (*sé*); to know a fact
- **pagar** — regular -ar (to pay)
- **comprar** — regular -ar (to buy)
