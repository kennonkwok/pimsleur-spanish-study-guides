## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Present perfect — 3rd person plural: *han* + past participle**
Used for "they have" or "you (all) have" (Uds.):

- *¿Dónde han vivido en México?* — Where (in Mexico) have you (all) lived?
- *Han tomado cerveza mexicana.* — They/You have drunk Mexican beer.
- *¿Han hablado español allí?* — Have they spoken Spanish there?

**2. Present perfect — 1st person plural: *hemos* + past participle**
- *Hemos vivido en Acapulco.* — We have lived in Acapulco.
- *Hemos comido mole.* — We have eaten mole.
- *Hemos hablado español en casa.* — We have spoken Spanish at home.

**3. *Ha dicho* (3rd person singular present perfect of *decir*)**
- *Me ha dicho la dirección.* — He/She has told me the address.
- *Mi profesor me ha dicho eso.* — My teacher has told me that.
- *¿Le has dicho?* — Have you told him/her?

**4. Direct object pronouns with *querer* + infinitive**
Pronoun attaches to the infinitive:
- *Quiero verla.* — I want to see her.
- *Quiero verlos.* — I want to see them (my father and mother).

**5. *Todavía no* — not yet / still not**
- *Pero todavía no entiendo.* — But I still don't understand.
- *Todavía no la he visto.* — I haven't seen her yet.

**6. *Tengo que comer algo* — obligation + indefinite object**
- *Tengo que comer algo.* — I have to eat something.
- *Tengo que comer también.* — I have to eat too.

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Want to see them (group) | *Quiero verlos.* |
| State how long someone has lived somewhere | *Ha vivido aquí mucho tiempo.* |
| Ask where they have lived | *¿Dónde han vivido en México?* |
| Say we have lived somewhere | *Hemos vivido en Acapulco.* |
| Say someone has told me | *Mi profesor me ha dicho eso.* |
| Ask if someone has eaten | *¿Has comido?* |
| Deny having eaten yet | *Todavía no he comido esta mañana.* |
| Express obligation + something | *Tengo que comer algo.* |
| Propose eating together | *Podemos comer juntos.* |
| Ask if can come tomorrow | *¿Pueden venir conmigo mañana?* |
| Say can wait | *Puedo esperar.* |
| Invite: come with us | *Ven con nosotros.* |

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 28 uses the **informal** *tú* register for most conversations. The *usted* (plural) form *ustedes* appears when addressing a group — *¿Dónde han vivido?* (formal/pl.).

### Pronunciation Notes
- *todavía*: four syllables — to-da-VÍ-a; accent on the í.
- *mole*: in Mexican Spanish, two syllables — MO-le (not like English "mole").
- *leche*: two syllables — LE-che; the *ch* = /tʃ/.
- *película*: four syllables — pe-LÍ-cu-la; accent on the second syllable.

## Verb Conjugations

### poder — to be able to / can
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue) in all forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **puedo** | nosotros: **podemos** |
| 2nd | tú: puedes | vosotros: podéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: puede | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **pueden** |

Lesson 28 drills: *puedo* (I can), *podemos* (we can), *pueden* (they/you all can).

## Summary
- **poder** — stem-changing (o→ue); drills *puedo, podemos, pueden*
- **vivir** — regular -ir; present perfect drills *ha vivido, hemos vivido, han vivido*
- **comer** — regular -er; present perfect drills *he/has/hemos comido*
- **tomar** — regular -ar; present perfect drills *han tomado*
- **decir** — highly irregular; irregular past participle *dicho*; drills *me ha dicho*
