## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Imperfect tense — habitual/continuous past (-aba / -ía)**

The lesson extensively drills the *imperfect* tense, used for:
- Actions that used to happen repeatedly (*I used to ski*)
- States or conditions that persisted (*she was a student*)
- Background context (*she used to live in Boston*)

For regular **-ar** verbs, imperfect endings are: *-aba, -abas, -aba, -ábamos, -abais, -aban*
For **-er / -ir** verbs: *-ía, -ías, -ía, -íamos, -íais, -ían*

Key imperfect forms drilled:
- *trabajaba* — she/he/I used to work
- *esquiaba* — she/he/I used to ski
- *estudiaba* — she/he was studying
- *vivía* — she/he/I used to live
- *sabía* — she/he/I knew / used to know
- *era* — she/he/I was (condition/identity, from *ser*)
- *tenía que* — she/he/I had to (obligation, imperfect of *tener*)

**2. *ser* + passive construction: *fue construida***

The lesson introduces the passive voice using *ser* in the preterite + past participle:

- *Fue construida en el siglo XVII.* — It was built in the 17th century.
- *Fue construida en el siglo XVI.* — It was built in the 16th century.

The participle (*construida*) agrees in gender and number with the subject (*la iglesia*, *la casa* — both feminine singular).

**3. Present perfect: *he visto* (haber + past participle)**

Used for actions completed but relevant to the present:

- *No he visto mucho.* — I haven't seen much.
- *¿Qué ha visto en Lima?* — What have you seen in Lima?
- *Ha sido presidente solo desde enero.* — He has been president only since January.

Irregular past participles drilled: *visto* (ver), *sido* (ser).

**4. Subjunctive with *esperar que*: *espero que no haya sido***

When expressing hopes, *esperar que* triggers the subjunctive. The lesson drills the present perfect subjunctive:

- *Espero que no haya sido un error.* — I hope that it may not have been a mistake.

*Haya* is the present subjunctive of *haber* (3rd sg.). *Sido* is the past participle of *ser*.

**5. Conditional perfect: *habría votado***

"Would have [done]" = conditional of *haber* (*habría*) + past participle:

- *Habría votado por él.* — I/she would have voted for him.
- *También habría votado por él.* — I too would have voted for him.

The lesson notes that *habría* (not the alternative *hubiera*) is the preferred form for now.

**6. Past subjunctive: *si fuera* (if I were)**

Hypothetical conditions ("if I were...") use *si* + imperfect subjunctive:

- *Si fuera norteamericano...* — If I were American (m)...
- *Si fuera norteamericana...* — If she were American (f)...

*Fuera* is the imperfect subjunctive of *ser* (or *ir*). Used with conditional perfect in the main clause: *Si fuera…, habría votado…*

**7. *se me olvidó* — accidental/unintentional construction**

Spanish encodes accidental forgetting with an indirect object + *se*:

- *Se me olvidó.* — I forgot (it). Lit. "It forgot itself to me."

Compare: *No me acuerdo de dónde dejé mis lentes.* — I don't remember where I left my glasses. (*acordarse de* = to remember something specific)

**8. Adjective position: *nuevo* before vs. after noun**

- *Su nuevo presidente* — replacement/successor (pre-nominal)
- *Un libro nuevo* — brand new (post-nominal)

The lesson explicitly explains this positional distinction.

## Summary

- **Imperfect tense** is the lesson's central focus: *trabajaba, esquiaba, vivía, sabía, era, tenía que* — all drilled to express habitual or continuous past states and actions.
- **Passive voice** (*fue construida*) is introduced for the churches and the Aliaga House — past participle agrees with subject gender.
- **Present perfect** (*he visto, ha sido*) contrasted with preterite (*fui, voté*) — completed past with present relevance vs. one-time completed action.
- **Conditional perfect** (*habría votado*) + **past subjunctive** (*si fuera*) form the lesson's hypothetical structure: *Si fuera norteamericano, habría votado por él.*
- **Subjunctive with *esperar que*** (*espero que no haya sido*) — present perfect subjunctive used for a hope about the past.
- **se me olvidó** — accidental construction; contrasted with *no me acuerdo de* (I don't remember).
- **Adjective position**: *nuevo* before noun = replacement; after noun = brand new.
- **Key vocabulary**: *los lentes* (glasses, Latin America), *vale la pena* (it's worthwhile), *ya veremos* (we'll see), *definitivamente*, *antigua*, *la estación de trenes*.
