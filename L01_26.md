## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *Saber* vs. *conocer* — two ways to "know"**
- *Saber* = to know a fact, know how to do something, know information
  - *Ya sé un poco de inglés.* — I already know a little English.
  - *¿Sabe dónde está el bar?* — Do you know where the bar is?
- *Conocer* = to be acquainted with a person or place
  - *Conozco a Sebastián muy bien.* — I know Sebastián very well.
Both have irregular yo forms: *sé* (saber), *conozco* (conocer).

**2. *Ven* — irregular tú command of *venir***
The tú affirmative command of *venir* is irregular: *ven* (not *viene*).
- *Ven.* — Come.
- *Ven conmigo.* — Come with me.
Contrast with formal (*Ud.*): *Venga* / *Puede venir conmigo.*

**3. *Vamos a* + infinitive — "we're going to"**
Used in first-person plural to express future plans:
- *Vamos a estudiar inglés juntos.* — We're going to study English together.
- *¿Cómo vamos a ir?* — How are we going to go?

**4. *Ya* — "already" or emphatic "now"**
- *Ya sé un poco de inglés.* — I already know a little English.
- *Quiero ir ahora.* — I want to go now. (plain *ahora*)
- *Ya se va.* — She's already leaving. (emphatic immediacy)

**5. Direct object pronoun replacing language name**
- *Pero no lo hablo bien.* — But I don't speak it well. (*lo* = *el inglés*)
- *La tengo en mi teléfono.* — I have it in my phone. (*la* = *la dirección*)

**6. Asking what a word means**
- *¿Qué significa "siempre"?* — What does "always" mean?
- *Esa palabra significa "always."* — That word means "always."

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 26 explicitly contrasts registers. Formal: *Puede venir conmigo*, *¿Puedo ir con usted?*, *Venga*, *Escríbalo*. Informal: *Ven conmigo*, *¿Puedes hacer las reservas?*, commands with *-tú* forms. The lesson also notes that *ven* is the informal command and *venga* is the formal.

### Pronunciation Notes
- *Encantado*: four syllables — en-can-TA-do.
- *Allí*: stress on final syllable — a-LLÍ; the *ll* = /j/ in Latin American Spanish.
- *Juntos*: HUN-tos; *j* is pronounced like English *h*.
- *Siempre*: SIEM-pre; the *ie* is a diphthong.
- *Izquierda*: iz-QUIER-da.

## Summary
- **saber** — irregular; yo *sé*; knows facts/skills (vs. *conocer* for persons/places)
- **aprender** — regular -er
- **estudiar** — regular -ar
- **venir** — irregular; yo *vengo*; tú command *ven* (irregular)
- **recordar** — stem-changing (o→ue)
- **ir** — irregular; yo *voy*, nosotros *vamos*
- **poder** — stem-changing (o→ue)
- **seguir** — stem-changing (e→i); yo *sigo*
- **conocer** — irregular yo *conozco*; otherwise regular -er
- **querer** — stem-changing (e→ie); irregular yo *quiero*
