## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Present perfect: *he/has/ha + participio* (I have done)**
The present perfect describes actions completed with relevance to the present:

| Subject | Auxiliary | Example |
|---|---|---|
| yo | he | *No he podido viajar.* — I haven't been able to travel. |
| yo | he | *No he tenido el dinero.* — I haven't had the money. |
| ella | ha | *Ha tenido demasiado trabajo.* — She has had too much work. |
| nosotros | hemos | *No hemos podido.* — We haven't been able to. |

Regular past participles: -ar → *-ado*; -er/-ir → *-ido*
Irregular: *poder → podido*, *tener → tenido*, *decir → dicho*

**2. *salir* — irregular yo (*salgo*); meanings: to leave, go out, depart**
*Salir* is used for leaving a place, going outside, or departing:

- *Sale de la oficina.* — He leaves the office.
- *¿Sales tú?* — Are you going out?
- *Tenemos que salir del edificio.* — We have to leave the building.
- *Pero sin salir, no.* — But not without going out.

**3. *quedarse* — reflexive "to stay/remain"**
*Quedar* alone means "to be located." *Quedarse* (reflexive) means "to stay":

- *Prefiero quedarme aquí.* — I prefer to stay here.
- *Puedes quedarte aquí.* — You can stay here.

**4. Conditional *podría* — "I/he/she could"**
The conditional of *poder* is used for hypotheticals and polite suggestions:

- *Podría comer.* — I could eat.
- *Podría ir al concierto.* — I could go to the concert.
- *¿Podría esperar?* — Could you wait?

**5. *nadie* and *nadie más* — nobody / no one else**
*Nadie* always triggers a negative verb (double negative when the verb comes after):

- *Nadie sabe.* — No one knows. (*nadie* before verb = no *no* needed)
- *No sabe nadie.* — No one knows. (*nadie* after verb = *no* required)
- *Nadie más de la oficina.* — No one else from the office.
- *Ni yo ni nadie más.* — Neither I nor anyone else.

## Verb Conjugations

### salir — to leave / to go out
**Type:** irregular yo (*salgo*); otherwise regular -ir

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **salgo** | nosotros: salimos |
| 2nd | tú: **sales** | vosotros: salís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **sale** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: salen |

Lesson drills: *sale*, *¿sales?*, *sino sales*, *salir del edificio*, *yo voy a salir solo*, *sin salir, no*.

---

### viajar — to travel
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: viajo | nosotros: viajamos |
| 2nd | tú: viajas | vosotros: viajáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: viaja | ellos/ellas/Uds.: viajan |

Lesson drills: *no he podido viajar*, *tampoco he podido viajar*, *me gusta viajar*.

---

### necesitar — to need
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: necesito | nosotros: necesitamos |
| 2nd | tú: **necesitas** | vosotros: necesitáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: necesita | ellos/ellas/Uds.: necesitan |

Lesson drills: *necesitas vacaciones*.

---

### haber — to have (auxiliary for present perfect)
**Type:** highly irregular (auxiliary only)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **he** | nosotros: **hemos** |
| 2nd | tú: **has** | vosotros: habéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **ha** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **han** |

Lesson drills: *no he podido*, *no he tenido*, *ha tenido demasiado trabajo*, *tampoco he podido viajar*.

---

## Summary

- **salir** — irregular yo (*salgo*); key verb for leaving/going out; *sin salir* drilled
- **viajar** — regular -ar; used with present perfect: *no he podido viajar*
- **necesitar** — regular -ar; *necesitas vacaciones* drilled
- **haber** — auxiliary for present perfect (*he, has, ha, hemos, han*); introduced with *podido* and *tenido*
