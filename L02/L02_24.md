## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Present perfect: *ha cambiado* ("has changed")**
The present perfect in Spanish is formed with *haber* (present) + past participle. It describes an action that occurred in the past with present relevance.

Structure: *haber* + [-ado / -ido]

| Subject | *haber* | Example |
|---|---|---|
| yo | he | *He comido.* |
| tú | has | *Has cambiado.* |
| él/ella/Ud. | **ha** | *Ha cambiado de idea.* |
| nosotros | hemos | *Hemos terminado.* |
| ellos/Uds. | han | *Han terminado.* |

Lesson drills: *ha cambiado de idea*, *ha cambiado*, *dije que ha cambiado de idea*.

Past participle formation:
- *-ar* verbs: stem + *-ado* → *cambiar* → *cambiado*
- *-er* verbs: stem + *-ido* → *comer* → *comido*
- *-ir* verbs: stem + *-ido* → *vivir* → *vivido*

**2. Preterite of *decir*: *dije* and *dijiste***
This lesson drills both the yo and tú forms of the preterite of *decir*:

| Form | Spanish | Example |
|---|---|---|
| I said | *dije* | *Le dije que no debería comenzar otra.* |
| you told me | *me dijiste* | *Me dijiste que quiere ser médico.* |
| he/she said | *dijo* | *(review from L22)* |

**3. Conditional *debería* — "should / ought to"**
*Debería* is the conditional of *deber*. It expresses moral obligation or advice ("should").

- *Debería terminar una cosa.* — He should finish one thing.
- *No debería cambiar de idea otra vez.* — He shouldn't change his mind again.
- *No debería comenzar otra cosa.* — He shouldn't start something else.

Note: *Debería* = I/he/she should (same form for yo and él/ella in conditional).

**4. Stressed possessives: *mío/a, tuyo/a* ("of mine, of yours")**
Unlike regular possessives (*mi, tu, su*), stressed possessives follow the noun and agree in gender and number. They emphasize ownership.

- *Es amiga mía.* — She's a friend of mine. (not just any friend — *my* friend)
- *Pablo García es amigo tuyo.* — Pablo García is a friend of yours.
- *Es un buen amigo mío.* — He's a good friend of mine.

**5. Comparative: *mejor que yo* — "better than I"**
Spanish comparisons: *más/menos + adjective + que* or adjective *mejor/peor + que*.

- *Ella juega mejor que yo.* — She plays better than I.
- *Soy mejor jugando voleibol.* — I'm better at playing volleyball.

**6. *regresar* — present tense for scheduled future**
Like English, Spanish present tense can express near-future scheduled events:
- *Regresan pasado mañana.* — They return/are returning the day after tomorrow.
- *Regresa este fin de semana.* — He returns this weekend.

## Verb Conjugations

### regresar — to return / to come back
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **regreso** | nosotros: regresamos |
| 2nd | tú: **regresas** | vosotros: regresáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **regresa** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **regresan** |

Lesson drills: *¿cuándo regresan?*, *regresan este fin de semana*, *regresan pasado mañana*, *regresa este fin de semana*, *toda mi familia regresa*.

Lesson drills: *antes de comenzar otra*, *no debería comenzar otra cosa*. Infinitive form drilled; no conjugated present forms explicit in lesson.

## Summary

- **regresar** — regular -ar; "to return" (Latin Am.); present tense used for scheduled returns
- **cambiar** — regular -ar; used in *cambiar de idea* (change mind) and *cambiar dinero* (exchange money); past participle *cambiado* drilled in present perfect
- **comenzar** — stem-changing (e→ie); *comenzar otra cosa* — infinitive construction
- **haber** — irregular auxiliary; present perfect *ha cambiado* — key new grammar introduced
- **deber** (conditional) — *debería/no debería* = "should/shouldn't" — advice and obligation
- **decir** (preterite) — *dije* (I said), *me dijiste* (you told me), *le dije* (I told him/her)
