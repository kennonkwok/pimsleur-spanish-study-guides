## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *gustar* with plural indirect objects: *les gusta / nos gusta***
The *gustar* construction expands to plural indirect objects.

| Indirect object | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| me (to me) | me gusta | *Me gusta el vino.* |
| te (to you, informal) | te gusta | *¿Te gusta?* |
| le (to him/her/you formal) | le gusta | *Le gusta.* |
| nos (to us) | nos gusta | *Nos gusta mucho.* |
| les (to you all/them) | les gusta | *¿Les gusta el refresco?* |

- *¿Les gusta el refresco?* — Do you (all) like soda?
- *No nos gusta el refresco.* — We don't like soda.
- *Nos gustaría el vino más tarde.* — We'd like the wine later.

**2. *me gustaría / nos gustaría* — conditional of *gustar***
The conditional form *gustaría* = "would like." It is more polished than *quiero* and softer than *quisiera*.
- *Me gustaría conocerlo.* — I would like to meet him.
- *Nos gustaría ir.* — We would like to go.
- *Les gustaría venir mañana.* — Would you (all) like to come tomorrow?

**3. Present perfect: *hemos* + past participle**
Building on Lesson 3, the nosotros form is drilled:
- *Hemos estado aquí desde ayer.* — We have been here since yesterday.
- *No hemos visto mucho todavía.* — We haven't seen much yet.
- *Hemos llegado.* — We have arrived.

Note: *visto* is the irregular past participle of *ver*.

**4. *quedarse* — reflexive verb for "to stay"**
The reflexive verb *quedarse* = to stay. The reflexive pronoun matches the subject:
- *Me quedo* (I stay) → *voy a quedarme* (I'm going to stay)
- *¿Van a quedarse?* — Are you going to stay?
- *No podemos quedarnos.* — We can't stay.

**5. *desde* + time — since / from**
*Desde* expresses the starting point of an action that is still ongoing.
- *Desde ayer.* — Since yesterday.
- *¿Desde cuándo están ustedes aquí?* — Since when have you been here?
- *Solo hemos estado aquí desde ayer.* — We've only been here since yesterday.

**6. Indirect object pronoun *le* with infinitive**
When taking or buying something *to/for* someone, use the indirect object pronoun *le* (or *les* for plural):
- *Quiero llevarle algo.* — I want to take to her something. (= I want to take her something)
- *Quiero comprarle algo.* — I want to buy for him something.
Contrast with direct object: *verlo* (to see him) vs. *llevarle algo* (to take him something).

## Verb Conjugations

### quedarse — to stay
**Type:** regular -ar, reflexive

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me quedo | nosotros: nos quedamos |
| 2nd | tú: te quedas | vosotros: os quedáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: se queda | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se quedan |

With *ir a* + infinitive: *van a quedarse*, *voy a quedarme*, *vas a quedarte*.

Lesson drills: *¿cuánto tiempo van a quedarse?*, *solo hasta mañana*, *vamos a quedarnos*.

---

### gustar — to please / to like (reverse construction)
**Type:** regular -ar; always used with indirect object pronoun

| Indirect pronoun | Form | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| me | me gusta / me gustan | I like |
| te | te gusta / te gustan | you like |
| le | le gusta / le gustan | he/she/you (formal) likes |
| nos | nos gusta / nos gustan | we like |
| les | les gusta / les gustan | you all/they like |

Conditional: *me gustaría, nos gustaría, les gustaría* (I/we/you all would like)

Lesson drills: *¿les gusta el refresco?*, *no nos gusta el refresco*, *nos gustaría el vino más tarde*, *me gustaría conocerlo*, *nos gustaría ver más*.

---

### ver — to see (present perfect: *visto*)
**Type:** irregular; past participle: *visto* (irregular)

Present perfect forms drilled:
- *no hemos visto mucho todavía* — we haven't seen much yet

(Full present tense conjugation in Lesson 4.)

### haber — to have (auxiliary for present perfect)
**Type:** irregular auxiliary verb

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: he | nosotros: **hemos** |
| 2nd | tú: has | vosotros: habéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: ha | ellos/ellas/Uds.: han |

Lesson drills (nosotros focus): *hemos estado*, *no hemos visto*, *hemos llegado*.

---

## Summary
- **quedarse** — regular -ar reflexive; "to stay"; *van a quedarse*, *voy a quedarme*
- **gustar** — regular -ar; reverse construction; expanded to *nos gusta / les gusta*; conditional: *gustaría*
- **ver** — irregular; past participle *visto* (irregular); *no hemos visto mucho todavía*
- **haber** — irregular auxiliary; nosotros form *hemos* drilled for present perfect
