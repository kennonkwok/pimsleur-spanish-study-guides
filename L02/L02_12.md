## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Preterite of *gustar*: *me gustó / me gustaron***
The preterite of *gustar* works the same as the present — the verb agrees with the thing liked (grammatical subject), not the person:

- *Me gustó la tienda.* — I liked the store. (*la tienda* = 3rd sg. → *gustó*)
- *Me gustó mucho.* — I really liked it.
- *Me gustaron las cosas.* — I liked the things. (*las cosas* = 3rd pl. → *gustaron*)

**2. Indirect object pronoun *les* appended to infinitives**
*Les* means "to/for them" (or "to/for you, plural"). It attaches to infinitives:

- *Comprarles juegos.* — To buy them games. (*les* = for Paula's children)
- *Podríamos comprarles juegos.* — We could buy them games.
- *Tenemos que comprarles juegos.* — We have to buy them games.

Contrast with direct object *los/las*:
- *Tenemos que comprarlos.* — We have to buy them. (*los regalos* — what is bought)
- *Tenemos que comprarles juegos.* — We have to buy them games. (*les* = for the children)

**3. Conditional *podríamos* — we could**
Building on L2.11's introduction of *podría/podrían*, this lesson adds the *nosotros* form:

- *Podríamos comprarles juegos.* — We could buy them games.
- *Podríamos ir después de comprar los juegos.* — We could go after buying the games.

**4. Preterite of *ir*, 2nd person: *fuiste***
- *¿Ya fuiste a la tienda?* — Did you already go to the store?
- *Fui la semana pasada.* — I went last week. (review)
- *Fui otra vez el lunes.* — I went again on Monday.

**5. Demonstrative adjectives: *ese / esa***
*Ese/esa* refers to something near the listener (or at a middle distance), while *este/esta* refers to something near the speaker:

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|---|---|
| Singular | *ese* | *esa* |
| Plural | *esos* | *esas* |

- *En esa tienda pequeña.* — In that small store.

**6. *mirar* vs. *buscar***
Both relate to "looking" but are distinct:

| Verb | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *mirar* | to look at, to watch | *Me gusta mirar cosas bonitas.* |
| *buscar* | to look for, to search | *Tenemos que buscar regalos.* |

## Verb Conjugations

### mirar — to look at / to watch
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: miro | nosotros: miramos |
| 2nd | tú: miras | vosotros: miráis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: mira | ellos/ellas/Uds.: miran |

Lesson drills: *me gusta mirar cosas bonitas*, *podríamos mirar cosas allí*.

---

### gustar — to please / to like (preterite)
**Type:** regular -ar (drilled in preterite 3rd person forms)

Present tense (review):

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 3rd (sg. subject) | gusta | — |
| 3rd (pl. subject) | — | gustan |

Preterite (new in this lesson):

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 3rd (sg. subject) | **gustó** | — |
| 3rd (pl. subject) | — | **gustaron** |

Lesson drills: *me gustó esa tienda*, *me gustó mucho la tienda*.

---

### ir — to go (preterite, extended)

Lesson drills: *fui la semana pasada*, *fui otra vez el lunes*, *¿ya fuiste a la tienda?*, *yo fui el sábado*, *fui el jueves*.

### poder — could (conditional, extended)
**Type:** irregular conditional

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|

Lesson drills: *podríamos comprarles juegos*, *podríamos ir después de comprar los juegos*, *ustedes podrían comprarles*, *nosotros podríamos*.

## Summary
- **mirar** — regular -ar; "to look at/watch"; contrasts with *buscar* (look for)
- **gustar** (preterite) — *gustó / gustaron*; same reverse construction as present
- **ir** (preterite, *fuiste*) — new 2nd person sg. form; *fuiste/fui/fue/fuimos* all drilled
- **poder** (conditional, *podríamos*) — "we could"; adds to *podría/podrían* from L2.11
