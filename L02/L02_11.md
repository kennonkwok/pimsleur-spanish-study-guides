## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Preterite tense of *ir* / *ser* (they share the same forms)**
The preterite (simple past) of *ir* (to go) and *ser* (to be) are identical — context clarifies meaning:

| Pronoun | Form | Example (ir) |
|---|---|---|
| yo | **fui** | *Fui a España.* — I went to Spain. |
| tú | fuiste | *¿Fuiste a España?* — Did you go to Spain? |
| él/ella | **fue** | *Fue a comer.* — He went to eat. |
| nosotros | fuimos | *Fuimos juntos.* — We went together. |
| ellos | fueron | *Fueron allí.* — They went there. |

*Se fue* = he/she left (reflexive *irse*, preterite), or formal "you went" (*usted se fue*).

**2. Conditional of *poder*: *podría, podrías, podrían***
The conditional expresses "could" (polite possibility). Key forms drilled:

- *Podría venir.* — She could come.
- *¿Podrían venir el sábado?* — Could you (pl.) come on Saturday?
- *Podría estar allí todavía.* — He could still be there.

Full conditional of *poder*:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | podría | podríamos |
| 2nd | podrías | podríais |
| 3rd | podría | podrían |

**3. *conocer* vs. *saber***
Both mean "to know" but in different senses:

| Verb | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *saber* | Know a fact, information | *No sé si puedo.* / *¿Sabes dónde queda?* |
| *conocer* | Know / be acquainted with a person/place | *No lo conozco.* / *Deberías conocerla.* |

*Conocer* is irregular only in *yo*: *conozco*. All other forms are regular -er.

**4. *gracias por* + infinitive**
*Gracias por* + infinitive = "thanks for [doing something]":

- *Gracias por invitarnos.* — Thanks for inviting us.
- *Gracias por invitarme.* — Thanks for inviting me.
- *Gracias por esperar.* — Thanks for waiting. (L2.08 review)

**5. Preterite of *llegar*: *llegué***
*Llegar* is regular -ar in the preterite, but the spelling changes to preserve the /g/ sound before *-é*: *llegué* (not *llegé*).

- *Llegué esta mañana.* — I arrived this morning.

## Summary
- **invitar** — regular -ar; "to invite"; *gracias por invitarnos/invitarme*
- **visitar** — regular -ar; "to visit someone"
- **conocer** — irregular (*yo conozco*); "to know/meet a person or place"
- **ir** (preterite) — highly irregular; *fui/fue/fuimos*; shared with *ser* preterite
- **poder** (conditional) — *podría/podrían*; "could" (polite possibility)
- **llegar** (preterite) — *llegué*; spelling-change -ar verb
