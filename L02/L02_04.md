## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *quisiera* — polite "I would want/like"**
*Quisiera* is the imperfect subjunctive of *querer*, but it functions as a polished, softened way to make requests. It is the standard way to order in a restaurant or make a polite request.
- *Quisiera un vino tinto.* — I would like a red wine.
- *Quisiera una cerveza.* — I would like a beer.

**2. *nunca* — word order**
When *nunca* (never) comes before the verb, no additional negative word is needed.
- *Nunca tomo vino blanco.* — I never drink white wine.
But when *nunca* follows the verb, *no* must precede the verb:
- *No tomo vino blanco nunca.* — I never drink white wine. (less common)

**3. *sin* + infinitive — without doing**
"Without" + verb: Spanish uses *sin* + infinitive (not a gerund as in English).
- *Sin comer.* — without eating
- *Nunca tomo sin comer algo.* — I never drink without eating something.
- *Tengo que irme sin comer.* — I have to leave without eating.

**4. Adjectives used as nouns**
When context is clear, an adjective can replace a noun by simply using the article + adjective.
- *No, el grande no. El pequeño.* — Not the big one. The small one. (referring to *el carro*)
- *La Casa Blanca.* — The White House.

**5. Possessive adjective after noun: *mío / tuyo***
When emphasizing ownership (or using it after the noun), stressed possessives are used:
| Unstressed (before noun) | Stressed (after noun) |
|---|---|
| mi amigo | un amigo mío |
| tu amigo | un amigo tuyo |

- *Un amigo mío* — a friend of mine
- *Este amigo mío* — this friend of mine

**6. Time: *de la tarde / de la mañana***
When specifying a.m. or p.m. with clock times:
- *A las cinco de la tarde.* — At 5 p.m.
- *A las cinco de la mañana.* — At 5 a.m.
- *Después de las cuatro de la tarde.* — After 4 p.m.

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Make a polite request (order) | *Quisiera un vino tinto.* |
| Say you always / never drink | *Siempre tomo vino blanco. / Nunca tomo vino blanco.* |
| Say without eating/drinking | *Sin comer. / Sin tomar.* |
| Ask if someone wants something more | *¿Quieres algo más?* |
| Answer nothing more | *No, nada más.* |
| Say there's never enough time | *Nunca hay suficiente tiempo.* |
| Say it's already late | *Ya es tarde.* |
| Refer to big/small one | *El grande / el pequeño.* |
| Say a friend of mine (stressed possessive) | *Un amigo mío.* |
| Specify time of day (a.m./p.m.) | *A las cinco de la tarde. / Después de las cuatro de la tarde.* |
| Do you see her/him/it? | *¿La ves? / ¿Lo ves? / ¿La ve usted?* |
| I see her/him/it | *La veo. / Lo veo.* |

## Verb Conjugations

### ver — to see
**Type:** irregular (yo: *veo*; also irregular preterite and past participle)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **veo** | nosotros: vemos |
| 2nd | tú: **ves** | vosotros: veis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **ve** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **ven** |

Lesson drills: *la veo*, *¿la ves?*, *la ve usted*, *lo ves*, *no lo veo*.

---

### tomar — to drink / to take
**Type:** regular -ar (in Latin America; used for drinking beverages)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **tomo** | nosotros: tomamos |
| 2nd | tú: **tomas** | vosotros: tomáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: toma | ellos/ellas/Uds.: toman |

Lesson drills: *siempre tomo vino blanco*, *nunca tomo vino blanco*, *nunca tomo sin comer algo*.

---

### querer — to want / to love
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie) — changes in all present forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **quiero** | nosotros: queremos |
| 2nd | tú: **quieres** | vosotros: queréis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **quiere** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **quieren** |

*Quisiera* (I would like) = imperfect subjunctive, used for polite requests. Lesson drills: *quisiera un vino tinto*, *quisiera una cerveza*, *no quieres algo más*.

---

## Summary
- **ver** — irregular (*veo*, *ves*, *ve*); drilled with direct object pronouns: *la veo*, *lo ves*
- **tomar** — regular -ar; in Latin America = to drink (beverages); *nunca tomo sin comer algo*
- **querer** — stem-changing (e→ie); *quisiera* = polite "I would like" for ordering/requests
