## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Preterite tense: *llegar* (to arrive)**
The lesson introduces the preterite (past tense) for the first time. *Llegar* is a regular -ar verb in the preterite, but note the spelling change in yo: *llegué* (not *llegé*) to preserve the hard /g/ sound.

| Person | Form |
|---|---|
| yo | llegué |
| tú | llegaste |
| él/ella/Ud. | llegó |
| nosotros | llegamos |
| ellos/Uds. | llegaron |

- *¿Cuándo llegaste a la ciudad?* — When did you arrive in the city?
- *Llegué hoy en la tarde.* — I arrived today in the afternoon.

**2. *de visita* — on a visit**
A fixed prepositional phrase; no article is used.
- *Estoy aquí de visita.* — I'm here on a visit.

**3. *después de* + infinitive**
"After" + verb uses an infinitive in Spanish (not a gerund as in English).
- *Después de trabajar* — after working / after work
- *Después de comer* — after eating

**4. Personal *a***
When the direct object of a verb is a specific person (or personified entity), Spanish requires *a* before it.
- *Tengo que visitar a alguien.* — I have to visit someone.
- *Quiero ver a mi amigo.* — I want to see my friend.

**5. Object pronouns attached to infinitives**
Direct object pronouns can attach to the end of an infinitive:
- *verlo* — to see him/it
- *visitarlo* — to visit him
- *ayudarme* — to help me

**6. Time expressions: *en la mañana / tarde / noche***
| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| en la mañana | in the morning |
| en la tarde | in the afternoon |
| en la noche | in the evening/at night |
| mañana en la mañana | tomorrow morning |

| Pattern | Example |

## Verb Conjugations

### llegar — to arrive
**Type:** regular -ar (spelling change in preterite yo: -gué)

**Present tense:**

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: llego | nosotros: llegamos |
| 2nd | tú: llegas | vosotros: llegáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: llega | ellos/ellas/Uds.: llegan |

**Preterite tense (drilled in lesson):**

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **llegué** | nosotros: llegamos |
| 2nd | tú: **llegaste** | vosotros: llegasteis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **llegó** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: llegaron |

Lesson drills: *llegué hoy*, *llegaste a la ciudad*, *llegó* (he arrived).

## Summary
- **llegar** — regular -ar; preterite introduced: *llegué / llegaste / llegó*
- **conocer** — irregular (*conozco*); means to know a person/place, or to meet for the first time
- **vivir** — regular -ir; *vive en la ciudad de México*
- **girar** — regular -ar; used in directions (*gire / gira* = turn)
- **visitar** — regular -ar; always takes personal *a* before a person (*visitar a alguien*)
