## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. The imperfect tense: *tenía* (I had / he had)**
The imperfect tense describes ongoing states or habitual actions in the past — something that *used to be* or *was* true. *Tenía* is the imperfect of *tener*.

- *Antes tenía más tiempo.* — Before I had more time.
- *Hace dos años tenía más tiempo.* — Two years ago I had more time.
- *Tenía un periódico.* — I had a newspaper (but don't anymore).

The imperfect signals that the situation existed for a while in the past. Compare with the preterite (*tuve*) which marks a completed action.

**2. Preterite of *tener*: *tuve* ("I had" — completed action)**
*Tuve* is the irregular preterite of *tener*. Used for a specific past event.

- *Tuve que salir de mi casa muy temprano.* — I had to leave my house very early.
- *Tuve que comprar medicina.* — I had to buy medicine.

**3. Preterite of *ir*: *fui* ("I went")**
*Fui* is the preterite of *ir*. Irregular — same forms as preterite of *ser* (context distinguishes them).

- *Ayer fui a la farmacia.* — Yesterday I went to the pharmacy.
- *Por eso fui a la farmacia.* — That's why I went to the pharmacy.

**4. *hace* + time = "ago"**
The same verb *hacer* used for weather is also used to express time elapsed. Structure: *hace* + time expression.

- *Hace dos años* — two years ago
- *Hace unos años* — a few years ago
- *Hace dos años tenía más tiempo.* — Two years ago I had more time.

**5. *toda mi familia* / *toda tu familia* — "all of my/your family"**
*Todo/toda* precedes the possessive and noun. It agrees with the noun in gender and number.

- *Toda mi familia vive en Nueva York.* — All of my family lives in New York.
- *Toda tu familia vive en la Ciudad de México.* — All of your family lives in Mexico City.

**6. *ya no* vs. *todavía no***
| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *ya no* | no longer / not anymore | *Ya no trabajo allí.* |
| *todavía no* | not yet | *Todavía no está en la universidad.* |

## Verb Conjugations

### vivir — to live (reside)
**Type:** regular -ir

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|

Lesson drills: *¿Dónde vive tu familia?*, *toda tu familia vive en la Ciudad de México*, *vivo aquí*.

## Summary

- **vivir** — regular -ir; *¿dónde vive?* / *vive en…* — location of family
- **terminar** — regular -ar; preterite *terminó* (work ended); present forms introduced minimally
- **tener** (preterite) — irregular *tuve*; *tuve que* + inf. = "I had to" (completed past obligation)
- **ir** (preterite) — irregular *fui*; "I went" — drilled for pharmacy/bank trips
- **tenía** (imperfect of *tener*) — key new concept: expresses past states (*antes tenía más tiempo*)
