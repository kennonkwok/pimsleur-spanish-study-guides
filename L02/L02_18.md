## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *llevar* vs. *traer* — taking away vs. bringing to speaker**
These two verbs both translate as "bring" or "take" in English but differ directionally:

| Verb | Direction | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *traer* | toward the speaker's location | *Gracias por traer el postre.* — Thanks for bringing the dessert (here). |
| *llevar* | away from speaker / with you | *Me gustaría llevar algo conmigo.* — I'd like to take something with me. |

**2. *hay que* + infinitive — "one must / you have to"**
*Hay que* is an impersonal expression meaning "one must" or "it is necessary to." It never changes form:

- *Hay que estudiar todos los días.* — One must study every day.
- *Hay que encontrar algo bueno.* — One must find something good.
- *Hay que pensarlo.* — One must think about it.

Compare with *tener que* (personal obligation):
- *Tengo que estudiar.* — I have to study.
- *Hay que estudiar.* — One has to study (in general).

**3. *la próxima semana* — new standard for "next week"**
The lesson explicitly replaces *la semana entrante* with *la próxima semana*:

- *Me voy la próxima semana.* — I'm leaving next week.
- *Antes de la próxima semana.* — Before next week.

Both are valid; the course now uses *próxima* as the default.

**4. *poder* used impersonally: *puede* = "one can"**
*Puede* in the third person without a stated subject can mean "one can":

- *No puede aprender sin estudiar.* — No one can learn without studying.
- *Nadie puede aprender sin estudiar todos los días.* — No one can learn without studying every day.

**5. *pensar* — stem-changing (e→ie) + infinitive or object**
*Pensar* can take a direct object or *en* + noun:

- *Hay que pensarlo.* — One must think about it.
- *Tengo que pensar en eso.* — I have to think about that.

## Verb Conjugations

### llevar — to take / to carry / to bring (away)
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: llevo | nosotros: llevamos |
| 2nd | tú: llevas | vosotros: lleváis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: lleva | ellos/ellas/Uds.: llevan |

Lesson drills: *me gustaría llevar algo conmigo*, *podría llevar dulces*.
