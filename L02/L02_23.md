## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *conocer* — "to know" people and places (irregular *yo*)**
*Conocer* means to know/be acquainted with a person or place. It takes *a* before a person (personal *a*).

- *¿Conoces a María Pérez?* — Do you know María Pérez?
- *No, pero conozco a su familia.* — No, but I know her family.

Full conjugation:
| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| yo | **conozco** | nosotros: conocemos |
| tú | conoces | vosotros: conocéis |
| él/Ud. | conoce | ellos/Uds.: conocen |

**2. Gerund as description: *jugando* ("at playing")**
The gerund (*-ando/-iendo*) can be used after *ser bueno/a*, *ser mejor* to express "good/better at doing something."

- *Soy bueno jugando.* — I'm good at playing.
- *No soy buena jugando tenis.* — I'm not good at playing tennis. (*buena* because speaker is female)
- *Soy mejor jugando voleibol.* — I'm better at playing volleyball.

**3. *ustedes juegan* — plural "you all play" / "they play"**
*Juegan* serves both as 3rd person plural (they play) and as the form used with *ustedes* (you all play). Context distinguishes.

- *Ustedes tres juegan tenis.* — You three play tennis.
- *Ellos juegan muy bien.* — They play very well.
- *No juegan bien.* — They don't play well.

**4. Days of the week with *el* for scheduling**
When referring to a specific scheduled day, use *el* + day:
- *El miércoles tengo una reunión.* — On Wednesday I have a meeting.
- *El jueves es posible.* — Thursday is possible.
- *El viernes no puedo.* — On Friday I can't.

Days of the week do not take capital letters in Spanish.

**5. Conditional *podríamos* — "we could"**
*Podríamos* is the conditional of *poder* (nosotros form). It expresses hypothetical possibility.

- *Pensé que podríamos jugar.* — I thought that we could play.
- *Pensé que podríamos jugar a las cinco.* — I thought we could play at five.

**6. *pasado mañana* — "the day after tomorrow"**
A fixed adverbial expression: *pasado* (past) + *mañana* (tomorrow).

- *¿Qué tal pasado mañana?* — How about the day after tomorrow?
- *El jueves — pasado mañana.* — Thursday — the day after tomorrow.

## Verb Conjugations

### conocer — to know (people/places)
**Type:** irregular yo (*conozco*); otherwise regular -er

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **conozco** | nosotros: conocemos |
| 2nd | tú: conoces | vosotros: conocéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: conoce | ellos/ellas/Uds.: conocen |

Lesson drills: *¿conoces a María Pérez?*, *conozco a su familia*.

---

### llevar — to take (someone) / to carry
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|

| 2nd | tú: **llevas** | vosotros: lleváis |

Lesson drills: *¿puedes llevarme al centro?*, *buena suerte con tu carro*.

## Summary

- **conocer** — irregular yo (*conozco*); to know people/places; requires personal *a* before persons
- **llevar** — regular -ar; *llevarme* = to take me; common in "can you take me to…" constructions
- **empezar** — stem-changing (e→ie); *empieza* = it starts; used for scheduling meetings/events
- **jugar** — stem-changing (u→ue); fully drilled: all persons including nosotros *jugamos*; *jugando* used as gerund for "at playing"
- **poder** (conditional) — *podríamos* = "we could"; introduced for hypothetical scheduling
