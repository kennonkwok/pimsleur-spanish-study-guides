## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *tuve que* + infinitive — preterite of obligation**
*Tuve* is the preterite (simple past) of *tener*. *Tuve que* + infinitive = "I had to [do something]":
- *Tuve que alquilar un carro.* — I had to rent a car.

**2. Formal imperative: *déjame / déjeme***
*Dejar* in imperative + object pronoun:
- *Déjame ver.* — Let me see. (informal)
- *Déjeme ver.* — Let me see. (formal)

**3. *te lo dije* — double object pronoun (review + extension)**
Indirect object before direct object, both before conjugated verb:
- *Te lo dije.* — I told you (it). / I said it to you.
- *¿No te acuerdas? Te lo dije antes.* — Don't you remember? I told you before.

**4. Giving directions: *girar a la derecha / a la izquierda***
- *Gira a la izquierda.* — Turn to the left.
- *Gira a la derecha.* — Turn to the right.
- *Toma la calle Bolívar.* — Take Bolívar Street.

**5. *buscar* — to look for (no preposition needed)**
Unlike English, *buscar* takes a direct object with no preposition:
- *Busco la aplicación en mi celular.* — I'm looking for the app on my phone.
- *Déjame buscarlo.* — Let me look for it.

**6. *antes de* + infinitive — before doing something**
- *Tengo mucho que hacer antes de irme.* — I have a lot to do before leaving.
- *Antes de regresar.* — Before returning.

**7. Comparative: *mejor* (better)**
- *Es mejor hablarlo que leer.* — It's better to speak it than to read.
- *La próxima vez tienes que jugar mejor.* — Next time you have to play better.

## Verb Conjugations

### almorzar — to have lunch
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue); spelling change z→c before e (in preterite)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **almuerzo** | nosotros: almorzamos |
| 2nd | tú: **almuerzas** | vosotros: almorzáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **almuerza** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **almuerzan** |

Lesson drills: *¿te gustaría almorzar conmigo?* (infinitive context).

---

### jugar — to play (sport/game)
**Type:** stem-changing (u→ue) — unique pattern; changes in all forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **juego** | nosotros: jugamos |
| 2nd | tú: **juegas** | vosotros: jugáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **juega** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **juegan** |

Lesson drills: *no juego tenis bien*, *podemos jugar tenis el viernes*, *tienes que jugar mal / mejor*.

## Summary
- **almorzar** — stem-changing (o→ue); "to have lunch"
- **jugar** — stem-changing (u→ue); "to play (a game/sport)"; unique vowel change
- **buscar** — regular -ar; "to look for" (no preposition needed)
- **traer** — irregular (*traigo*); "to bring"
- **girar** — regular -ar; "to turn" (directions)
- **tener** (preterite *tuve*) — "I had to" (*tuve que* + inf.)
