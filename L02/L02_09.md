## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Present Perfect tense with *haber***
The present perfect is formed with *haber* (auxiliary) + past participle. It expresses actions that have occurred with relevance to the present.

| Pronoun | Haber | Past Participle | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| yo | he | estudiado | *He estudiado mucho.* — I have studied a lot. |
| nosotros | hemos | tenido | *Hemos tenido que alquilar un carro.* — We've had to rent a car. |
| tú | has | hablado | *¿Con quién has hablado?* — With whom have you spoken? |

Regular past participles:
- *-ar* verbs: stem + *-ado* (*hablar → hablado*, *estudiar → estudiado*)
- *-er* / *-ir* verbs: stem + *-ido* (*comer → comido*, *salir → salido*)

**2. Reflexive verb: *quedarse* (to stay)**
*Quedar* alone means to be located (lesson 1); *quedarse* (reflexive) means to stay/remain somewhere.

- *Me gustaría quedarme tres semanas.* — I'd like to stay three weeks.
- *Va a quedarse un año.* — She's going to stay a year.
- *Quiero quedarme.* — I want to stay.

Reflexive pronouns: *me, te, se, nos, os, se* — match the subject.

**3. *salir* — irregular *go*-verb**
*Salir* (to leave/depart) is irregular only in the *yo* present form: *salgo*. All other forms are regular -ir.

- *El avión sale a las siete.* — The plane departs at seven.
- *Sale temprano.* — It departs early.

**4. Expressing ongoing duration with present perfect: *desde hace***
Building on lesson 8, the present perfect can also be combined with *desde hace*:

- *He estudiado desde hace tres años.* — I've been studying for three years.

**5. *buscarlos / buscarlas* — direct object pronouns on infinitives**
Pronouns attach to the end of an infinitive. Gender of pronoun matches the noun:

- *Tengo que buscarlos.* — I have to look for them. (*los regalos* — masc. pl.)
- *Puedes buscarlas en muchas tiendas.* — You can look for them in many stores. (*las playeras* — fem. pl.)
- *Tengo que escribirla.* — I have to write it. (*la tarjeta postal* — fem. sg.)
- *Tengo que enviarla.* — I have to send it. (*la tarjeta postal*)

## Verb Conjugations

### estudiar — to study
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: estudio | nosotros: estudiamos |
| 2nd | tú: estudias | vosotros: estudiáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: estudia | ellos/ellas/Uds.: estudian |

Lesson drills: *he estudiado mucho*, *estudio desde hace tres años*.

## Summary
- **estudiar** — regular -ar; drilled in present perfect (*he estudiado mucho*)
- **salir** — irregular (*yo salgo*); key verb for plane/departure context
- **buscar** — regular -ar; central verb for shopping scenario; takes direct obj. pronouns
- **quedarse** — regular -ar reflexive; "to stay" (*me gustaría quedarme*)
- **enviar** — regular -ar (accent shift in singular/3rd-pl.); "to send" a postcard
- **haber** — highly irregular auxiliary; forms present perfect with past participle
