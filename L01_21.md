## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Distal demonstratives *ese/esa* — that**
*Ese* (masculine) and *esa* (feminine) point to things away from the speaker. These were previewed in Lesson 20 and extended here to people:
- *Ese hombre está enfermo.* — That man is sick.
- *Esa mujer está enferma también.* — That woman is sick too.

Contrast with proximal (*este/esta* = this, near speaker):
- *este vaso* vs. *ese vaso*
- *esta taza* vs. *esa taza*

**2. Adjectives as pronouns with the definite article**
When the noun is omitted but understood, the definite article + adjective stands in as a pronoun, agreeing in gender with the omitted noun:
- *La grande es para el té.* — The big one is for the tea. (*la* refers to *taza*)
- *La pequeña es para el café.* — The small one is for the coffee.
- *Este es grande.* — This one is big. (*este* refers to *vaso*)

**3. *Grande* — invariable for gender**
*Grande* does not change form between masculine and feminine:
- *un vaso grande* (m.) — a big glass
- *una taza grande* (f.) — a big cup

But *pequeño/pequeña* does change:
- *un vaso pequeño* (m.) — a small glass
- *una taza pequeña* (f.) — a small cup

**4. *El agua* — feminine noun with masculine article**
*Agua* is a feminine noun but takes *el/un* in the singular to avoid the double-*a* sound at a stressed syllable:
- *el agua* (not *la agua*) — the water
- *un vaso de agua* — a glass of water
- But: *Las aguas* (plural returns to feminine article)

**5. *Dame* — give me**
*Dame* = *da* (tú command of *dar*) + *me* (indirect object pronoun). This is the informal imperative:
- *Dame un té.* — Give me a tea.
- *Dame una taza de té, por favor.* — Give me a cup of tea, please.
- *Dame un vaso de agua.* — Give me a glass of water.

Formal equivalent: *Deme un té, por favor.* (Ud. command)

**6. Plural demonstratives: *estas/esas/estos/esos***
This lesson introduces plural demonstratives:
- *Esas mujeres son norteamericanas.* — Those women are North Americans.
- *Son amigas.* — They are friends. (feminine plural)

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Ask if someone is sick | *¿Está enfermo/a?* |
| Request a drink | *Dame un té.* / *Dame un vaso de agua.* |
| Specify size | *Un vaso grande.* / *Un vaso pequeño.* |
| Use pronoun replacement | *La grande es para el té.* |
| Point to a person | *Ese hombre.* / *Esa mujer.* |
| Describe a group | *Esas mujeres son norteamericanas.* |
| State they drink | *Toman el té.* / *Los norteamericanos toman mucho café.* |
| State they buy | *Las mujeres compran.* |

### Formal vs. Informal
- *Dame* — informal (tú command)
- *Deme* — formal (Ud. command)
- *¿Está enfermo?* — both formal and informal (same *estar* form for Ud. and él/ella)

### Pronunciation Notes
- *Enfermo/enferma*: stress on second syllable — en-FER-mo.
- *Taza*: stress on first syllable — TA-za; the *z* sounds like English *s* in Latin America.
- *Grande*: stress on first syllable — GRAN-de; two syllables.
- *Pequeño*: stress on second syllable — pe-KE-ño; the *ñ* is like "ny."
- *Esa/ese*: stress on first syllable — E-sa / E-se.
- *Agua*: the *gu* is not pronounced as in English "guard" — it is simply *a-gua*, two syllables.

## Verb Conjugations

### dar — to give
**Type:** irregular (*yo* form); otherwise regular -ar pattern with irregularities

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **doy** | nosotros: damos |
| 2nd | tú: das | vosotros: dais |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: da | ellos/ellas/Uds.: dan |

*Dame* = *da* (tú affirmative command) + *me*.
Lesson 21 drills: *dame un té*, *dame una taza de té*, *dame un vaso de agua*.

---

### estar — to be (state, location, temporary condition)
**Type:** irregular

| | Singular | Plural |

| 2nd | tú: estás | vosotros: estáis |

## Summary
- **dar** — irregular *yo* (*doy*); *dame* = give me (tú command + *me*)
- **estar** — irregular; used for sickness (*está enfermo/a*) and location (*están en el bar*)
- **tomar** — regular -ar; *toman* = they drink; *toma el té* when she's sick
- **comprar** — regular -ar; *compran* = they buy
- **ser** — irregular; *son norteamericanas*, *son amigas*
- **querer** — stem-changing (e→ie); *quiere tomar algo*
