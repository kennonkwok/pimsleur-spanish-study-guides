## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *pudo haber sido peor* — "it could have been worse"**
This expresses that something bad happened but could have been worse — a past possibility:

| Part | Structure | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| *pudo* | preterite of *poder* (3rd sg.) | could (in the past) |
| *haber sido* | perfect infinitive of *ser* | to have been |

- *Pudo haber sido peor.* — It could have been worse.
- Compare: *podría ser peor* = it could be worse (present/future possibility — conditional)
- The pattern *pudo haber* + past participle = "could have" in the past: *pudo haber llegado antes* = he could have arrived earlier.

**2. *vine / vino* — preterite of *venir* (to come)**
Reviewed explicitly in this lesson:

| Person | Preterite |
|---|---|
| yo | ***vine*** |
| tú | *viniste* |
| él/ella/Ud. | ***vino*** |
| nosotros | *vinimos* |
| ellos/Uds. | *vinieron* |

- *Vine porque me invitaron a una graduación.* — I came because they invited me to a graduation.
- *¿Vino usted de viaje de negocios?* — Did you come on a business trip?

**3. *nos invitaron a mi esposa y a mí* — clarifying pronoun referent**
When a subject could be ambiguous, Spanish adds a clarifying phrase after the pronoun:

- *Nos invitaron a mi esposa y a mí a la graduación.* — They invited my wife and me to the graduation.
- *Nos* is the direct object; *a mi esposa y a mí* clarifies exactly who is included.
- *a mí* (with accent) = prepositional form of *yo*; used after *a* for emphasis/clarification.

**4. *graduarse* vs. *se va a graduar* — flexible reflexive infinitive placement**
With reflexive verbs in periphrastic constructions (*ir a* + inf.), the reflexive pronoun can either:
- Attach to the infinitive: *va a graduarse* ✓
- Move before the conjugated verb: *se va a graduar* ✓
Both are equally correct and very common.

**5. *¿cómo se escribe?* — "how do you spell it?"**
Standard phrase for spelling names/words:
- *¿Cómo se escribe su nombre?* — How do you spell your name?
- *Se escribe G-O-R-D-O-N.* — It's spelled G-O-R-D-O-N.
- Uses impersonal *se* + *escribir* (3rd sg.)

**6. *me siento mejor* — *sentirse* for physical/emotional state**
*Sentirse* (stem-changing e→ie) expresses how one feels:
- *Me siento mucho mejor.* — I feel much better.
- *¿Cómo se siente?* — How do you feel? (formal)
- *¿Cómo te sientes?* — How do you feel? (informal)

## Verb Conjugations

### venir — to come
**Type:** highly irregular (*go*-verb yo: *vengo*; stem-changing e→ie; irregular preterite *vin-*)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **vengo** | nosotros: venimos |
| 2nd | tú: **vienes** | vosotros: venís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **viene** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **vienen** |

Preterite: ***vine**, viniste, **vino**, vinimos, vinisteis, vinieron*
Lesson drills: *vine porque me invitaron*, *¿vino usted de viaje de negocios?*, *mi esposa viene la semana entrante*.

---

### graduarse — to graduate
**Type:** regular -ar reflexive with accent shift (*yo me gradúo*)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me **gradúo** | nosotros: nos graduamos |
| 2nd | tú: te **gradúas** | vosotros: os graduáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: se **gradúa** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se **gradúan** |

Lesson drills: *ya va a graduarse de la universidad*, *ya se va a graduar de la universidad* (both equivalent).

---

### sentirse — to feel
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie present; e→i preterite); reflexive

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me **siento** | nosotros: nos sentimos |
| 2nd | tú: te **sientes** | vosotros: os sentís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: se **siente** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se **sienten** |

Lesson drills: *ahora me siento mucho mejor*, *¿cómo se siente usted?*

## Summary

- **venir** — irregular; preterite *vine* (1st sg.), *vino* (3rd sg.); *vine porque me invitaron*
- **graduarse** — -ar reflexive with accent shift; *va a graduarse* = *se va a graduar* (both valid)
- **sentirse** — stem-changing e→ie; *me siento mejor* = I feel better
- ***pudo haber sido peor*** = it could have been worse; *pudo* (pret. *poder*) + *haber* + past participle
- ***nos invitaron a mi esposa y a mí*** — clarifying who "us" means with *a* + prepositional pronoun
- ***¿cómo se escribe?*** = how do you spell it? — impersonal *se* + *escribir*
- ***me invitaron*** = they invited me; passive/impersonal 3rd pl. for unnamed "they"
