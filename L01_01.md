## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Subject pronoun: *usted* (formal you)**
Spanish has separate formal and informal second-person pronouns. Lesson 1 uses only *usted* (formal), which takes 3rd-person singular verb forms — the same forms used for *él/ella*.

- *Usted es norteamericano.* — You are North American.
- *Entiende?* — Do you understand? (the *usted* subject is omitted — this is normal)

Spanish often drops the subject pronoun because the verb ending makes it clear who is acting.

**2. Negation: *no* before the verb**
Place *no* directly before the conjugated verb. No other word goes between *no* and the verb.

- *No entiendo.* — I don't understand.
- *No hablo inglés.* — I don't speak English.
- *No, no hablo inglés.* — No, I don't speak English. (first *no* = "no"; second = negation)

**3. Question formation by intonation**
Spanish yes/no questions use the same word order as statements — just raise your pitch at the end.

- Statement: *Entiende.* (You understand.)
- Question: *¿Entiende?* (Do you understand?)

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Polite attention-getter | *Disculpe, señorita / señor.* |
| Ask if someone understands | *¿Entiende?* / *¿Entiende inglés?* |
| Say you understand / don't understand | *Entiendo.* / *No entiendo.* |
| Say you speak / don't speak | *Hablo español.* / *No hablo inglés.* |
| Express degree | *Entiendo un poco.* / *Hablo un poco.* |
| Affirm + verb | *Sí, entiendo.* / *Sí, entiendo un poco.* |
| Identify nationality | *Usted es norteamericano.* |

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 1 uses only **formal** register throughout (*usted*, *señorita*, *señor*). Informal forms (*tú*, first-name address) are not introduced yet.

### Pronunciation Notes
- The **r** in *señorita* is a flapped /r/, softer than English. Pimsleur drills this explicitly.
- *Español*: the *ñ* is a distinct letter; its sound is like English "ny" (*can-yon*).
- *No entiendo*: the two words link together in speech — practice running them together smoothly.
- *Inglés*: stress falls on the final syllable (*in-GLÉS*), unlike English "EN-glish".

## Verb Conjugations

### entender — to understand
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie)
The stem vowel *e* changes to *ie* in all forms **except** *nosotros* and *vosotros* (those keep the original stem).

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **entiendo** | nosotros: entendemos |
| 2nd | tú: entiendes | vosotros: entendéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: entiende | ellos/ellas/Uds.: entienden |

Lesson 1 drills: *entiendo* (I understand), *entiende* (you/he/she understands).

---

### hablar — to speak
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: hablo | nosotros: hablamos |
| 2nd | tú: hablas | vosotros: habláis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: habla | ellos/ellas/Uds.: hablan |

Lesson 1 drills: *hablo* (I speak), *habla* (you speak).

---

### ser — to be
**Type:** irregular (one of the most irregular verbs in Spanish)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: soy | nosotros: somos |
| 2nd | tú: eres | vosotros: sois |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **es** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: son |

Lesson 1 drills: *es* only — *Usted es norteamericano.*

---

### disculpar — to excuse / to apologize
**Type:** regular -ar
Note: in the lesson, *Disculpe* functions as a fixed polite interjection ("Excuse me"). It is actually the *usted* command (imperative) form, not the present indicative — so you won't use the present-tense table in normal conversation, but it is provided for completeness.

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: disculpo | nosotros: disculpamos |
| 2nd | tú: **disculpas** | vosotros: disculpáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: disculpa | ellos/ellas/Uds.: disculpan |

*Disculpe* (the form drilled) = Ud. affirmative command (polite imperative), not present indicative.

## Summary
- **disculpar** — regular -ar (lesson uses the Ud. command *Disculpe* as an interjection)
- **entender** — stem-changing (e→ie); nosotros/vosotros keep the original stem
- **hablar** — regular -ar
- **ser** — irregular; memorize all 6 forms
