## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *estuvo* — preterite of *estar* for completed events**
The preterite of *estar* (*estuvo/estuve/estuvimos…*) describes a completed, bounded experience — how something *was* at a particular time or event:

- *La comida estuvo deliciosa.* — The food was delicious. (the lunch experience is complete)
- *El almuerzo estuvo bien.* — The lunch went well.
- Compare imperfect: *La comida estaba deliciosa* = the food was (ongoing, background) delicious.

| Preterite of *estar* |
|---|
| *estuve, estuviste, estuvo, estuvimos, estuvisteis, estuvieron* |

**2. *le pedí que me llamara* — past subjunctive in reported requests**
When *pedir* (or *decir*, *querer*) in the past introduces a request with a different subject, the subordinate verb goes into the **imperfect subjunctive**:

- *Le pedí que me llamara.* — I asked him to call me. (lit. "I asked him that he call me")
- *Espero que llame antes de la reunión.* — I hope he calls before the meeting. (present subj. after present *esperar*)
- Pattern: main verb (past) + *que* + imperfect subjunctive (*llamara, comiera, viniera…*)

**3. *me duele / me duelen* — *doler* like *gustar***
*Doler* (to hurt/ache) works exactly like *gustar*: the subject is the thing that hurts, and the person uses an indirect object pronoun:

| Subject | Verb | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *el pie* (sg) | *me duele* | *Me duele el pie.* — My foot hurts. |
| *los pies* (pl) | *me duelen* | *Me duelen los pies.* — My feet hurt. |
| *la rodilla* (sg) | *le duele* | *Le duele la rodilla.* — His/her knee hurts. |

**4. *le estoy devolviendo la llamada* — present progressive with IO pronoun**
*Devolver* (o→ue, like *volver*) in the present progressive:
- *Estoy devolviendo* — I'm returning (something)
- *Le estoy devolviendo la llamada.* — I'm returning your call. (*le* = to you, indirect object)
- The IO pronoun can also attach to the participle: *Estoy devolviéndole la llamada.*

**5. *vale la pena* + infinitive — "it's worth it to"**
*Valer la pena* is an impersonal expression meaning "to be worth the trouble":
- *Creo que vale la pena llamar.* — I think it's worth calling.
- *¿Vale la pena ir?* — Is it worth going?
- The infinitive follows directly after *vale la pena*.

## Verb Conjugations

### devolver — to return / give back
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue); present participle *devolviendo*; past participle *devuelto* (irregular)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **devuelvo** | nosotros: devolvemos |
| 2nd | tú: **devuelves** | vosotros: devolvéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **devuelve** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **devuelven** |

Preterite: *devolví, devolviste, devolvió, devolvimos…*
Lesson drills: *le estoy devolviendo la llamada* — I'm returning your call.

---

### recibir — to receive

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: recibo | nosotros: recibimos |
| 2nd | tú: recibes | vosotros: recibís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: recibe | ellos/ellas/Uds.: reciben |

Preterite: *recibí, recibiste, recibió, recibimos…*
Lesson drills: *recibí su mensaje* — I received your message.

---

### doler — to hurt / ache
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue); used like *gustar* — subject is what hurts

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| (thing that hurts, sg) | **duele** | **duelen** (pl) |

- *Me duele el pie.* — My foot hurts.
- *Me duelen los pies.* — My feet hurt.
- *Le duele la rodilla.* — His/her knee hurts.

Lesson drills: *me duelen los pies*, *me duele la rodilla*.

---

### reunirse — to meet / gather
**Type:** regular -ir, reflexive

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me reúno | nosotros: nos reunimos |
| 2nd | tú: te reúnes | vosotros: os reunís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: se reúne | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se reúnen |

Lesson drills: *vamos a reunirnos en Costa Rica*.

## Summary

- **devolver** — stem-changing (o→ue); *devolviendo* = returning; *le estoy devolviendo la llamada* = I'm returning your call
- **recibir** — regular -ir; preterite *recibí su mensaje* = I received your message
- **doler** — stem-changing (o→ue); used like *gustar*; *me duelen los pies* = my feet hurt
- **reunirse** — regular -ir reflexive; *vamos a reunirnos* = we're going to meet
- ***estuvo*** — preterite of *estar* for completed events: *la comida estuvo deliciosa*
- ***le pedí que me llamara*** — *pedir que* + past subjunctive for reported past requests
- ***vale la pena*** + inf. = it's worthwhile to; *vale la pena llamar*
- Apartment vocab: *habitación, baño con ducha, cocina, terraza, balcón, vista al mar, gratis*
