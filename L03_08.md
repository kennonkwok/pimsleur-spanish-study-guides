## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Preterite of -ar verbs: *pagar* as model**
Regular -ar preterite endings: -é / -aste / -ó / -amos / -asteis / -aron.
*Pagar* is a spelling-change verb (g→gu before -é) to preserve the hard /g/ sound.

| Person | Pagar |
|---|---|
| yo | pagué |
| tú | pagaste |
| él/ella/Ud. | pagó |
| nosotros | pagamos |
| vosotros | pagasteis |
| ellos/Uds. | pagaron |

Lesson drills: *pagué su boleto*, *¿no pagaste?*, *la empresa pagó el hotel*, *yo no pagué*.

**2. *hay que* + infinitive (impersonal obligation)**
*Hay que* + infinitive = "one must / it is necessary to." No subject — applies to everyone in context. Contrasts with *tener que* + inf. (personal obligation: "I/you/we have to").

- *Hay que repararlo.* — It has to be repaired. / One must repair it.
- *Hay que reparar mi carro.* — My car has to be repaired.
- *Tener que*: *Tenemos que hacer las reservaciones.* — We have to make the reservations.

**3. Direct object pronouns attached to infinitives**
When a verb phrase ends in an infinitive, the object pronoun may be appended directly to it:

- *Tenemos que hacerlas pronto.* — We have to make them soon. (*las* = las reservaciones)
- *Hay que repararlo.* — It must be repaired. (*lo* = el carro)
- *Lo estacionas* / *¿Dónde lo estacionas?* — You park it / Where do you park it?

**4. *bastante* as a degree adverb**
*Bastante* = "rather / quite / fairly." Modifies adjectives and adverbs without changing form.

- *El apartamento es bastante grande.* — The apartment is rather big.
- *También es bastante cómodo.* — It's also quite comfortable.
- *Es bastante pequeño.* — It's rather small.
- *Es bastante bonita.* — It's quite pretty. (agrees with *la plaza*)

**5. Comparisons: *tan… como* and *más/menos… que***
*Tan + adj./adv. + como* = "as … as." *Más/menos + adj. + que* = "more/less … than."

- *No tan interesante como la del año pasado.* — Not as interesting as last year's.
- *Nicaragua no es tan bonito como Costa Rica.* — Nicaragua isn't as beautiful as Costa Rica.
- *No es tan caro como el Ritz.* — It's not as expensive as the Ritz.
- *Un estacionamiento es mejor que la calle.* — A parking garage is better than the street.

**6. *atrás* — in the back / behind**
Spatial adverb used for fixed positions (behind/in back of something).

- *Atrás hay un patio.* — In the back there's a patio.
- *Y me gusta el patio atrás.* — And I like the patio in the back.
- *Hay un estacionamiento atrás.* — There's a parking garage in the back.

## Summary

- **Core theme:** travel logistics, paying, and apartment/parking descriptions
- **pagar** — regular -ar (g→gu in yo preterite: *pagué*); preterite is the main tense drilled — distinguish *pagué* (I paid) vs. *pagaste* (you paid) vs. *pagó* (he/she paid)
- **hay que** + inf. — impersonal obligation ("one must / it must be"); contrasts with personal *tener que* + inf.
- **Object pronouns on infinitives** — *hacerlas*, *repararlo*, *lo estacionas*; pronoun agrees in gender/number with the noun it replaces
- **bastante** — degree adverb meaning "rather/quite"; no agreement needed when modifying adjectives
- **tan… como** — equality comparisons; *más/menos… que* for inequality; *mejor que* (better than) is irregular
- **estacionar / el estacionamiento** — verb and derived noun; used for both parking garages and parking lots
- **conocer** — yo form is *conozco*; the tú form *conoces* is drilled directly in this lesson
- **atrás** — spatial adverb for "in the back"; common in apartment/car/restaurant contexts
