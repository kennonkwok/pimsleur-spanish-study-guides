## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *quedarse* vs. *irse* — reflexive verbs for staying and leaving**
Both are reflexive verbs derived from *quedar* and *ir*. The lesson drills them extensively in the preterite.

| Action | Verb | Preterite example |
|---|---|---|
| to leave / to go away | *irse* | *Se fueron. / Y Raúl se fue también.* |
| to stay / to remain | *quedarse* | *¿Por qué no se quedaron? / No se quedaron para el discurso.* |

- *¿Por qué se fueron?* — Why did they leave?
- *¿Por qué no se quedaron?* — Why didn't they stay?
- Note: *se fue* (he/she left, singular); *se fueron* (they left, plural)

---

**2. *me hubiera gustado* + infinitive — expressing what you would have liked**
A fixed idiomatic expression using the imperfect subjunctive of *haber* + past participle of *gustar*. Equivalent to "I would have liked to..."

- *Me hubiera gustado irme antes del almuerzo.* — I would have liked to leave before lunch.
- The infinitive that follows (*irme*, *ir*, *quedarse*, etc.) carries the action.
- This is the same reverse-construction as regular *gustar*: the thing liked is the grammatical subject.

---

**3. Double-object pronouns: *te lo recomiendo* / *me lo recomiendas***
When both an indirect object pronoun and a direct object pronoun appear, the indirect comes first:

| Order | Example |
|---|---|
| Indirect + Direct + Verb | *Te lo recomiendo.* — I recommend it to you. |
| Question form | *¿Me lo recomiendas?* — Do you recommend it to me? |

- The direct object pronoun (*lo*) replaces the noun (*el apartamento*).
- The indirect object pronoun (*te* / *me*) marks the recipient.

---

**4. Adjective agreement review: *tranquilo/a*, *ruidoso/a*, *contento/a***
The lesson drills gender agreement explicitly by using these adjectives with masculine and feminine nouns:

| Adjective | Masculine | Feminine | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| tranquilo | *un barrio tranquilo* | *una calle tranquila* | *Es una calle muy tranquila.* |
| ruidoso | *fue muy ruidoso* | *una calle ruidosa* | *No es una calle ruidosa.* |
| contento | *vas a estar contento* | — | *Creo que vas a estar contento allí.* |

---

**5. Preterite review: 3rd-person plural patterns**
The lesson drills several 3rd-pl preterite forms in context:

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Regular -ar (3rd pl): *-aron* | *Hablaron con nuestro gerente.* |
| Irregular ir/ser (3rd pl): *fueron* | *Se fueron. / Y luego se fueron.* |
| Irregular -ir reflexive (3rd pl): *se comprometieron* | *Se comprometieron.* |
| *quedarse* (3rd pl): *se quedaron* | *¿Por qué no se quedaron?* |

---

**6. *no escuché lo que dijiste* — reported speech / relative clause**
A key repair phrase drilled multiple times in the lesson:

- *No escuché lo que dijiste.* — I didn't hear what you said.
- *lo que* = "what" (that which) — introduces a noun clause as direct object
- *dijiste* = preterite 2nd sg of *decir* (irregular: *dij-* stem)

- **descansar** — regular -ar; key new verb for "to rest"; drilled repeatedly in infinitive after *quiero*, *necesito*, *es difícil*

- **quedarse** — regular -ar reflexive; "to stay"; preterite drill: *se quedaron*
- **hablar** — regular -ar; review; preterite 3rd pl *hablaron* drilled in context
- **necesitar** — regular -ar; review; *necesito descansar*
- **bailar** — regular -ar; review; *no puedo bailar muy bien*
- **decir** — irregular; review; preterite 2nd sg *dijiste* in repair phrase *no escuché lo que dijiste*
