## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *mientras* + imperfect — simultaneous background action**
*Mientras* ("while") pairs with the imperfect to describe an ongoing action interrupted by a preterite event:

- *Se cayó mientras esquiaba.* — He fell while he was skiing.
- The imperfect (*esquiaba*) = ongoing; the preterite (*se cayó*) = the interrupting event.
- Compare: *Mientras comía, sonó el teléfono.* — While I was eating, the phone rang.

**2. *si no fuera* — present counterfactual ("if it weren't so…")**
For hypotheticals about the present or future, *si* + imperfect subjunctive:

| Part | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *si* clause | *si* + imperfect subjunctive (*fuera/fuese*) | *Si no fuera tan caro viajar* |
| Result clause | conditional (*habría*) | *Habría venido.* |

- *No, habría venido, si no fuera tan caro viajar.* — No, he would have come, if it weren't so expensive to travel.
- Compare to past counterfactual (L5/8): *si no hubiera sido* (past subj.) + *habría comprado* (cond. perfect) — that's for past hypotheticals.

**3. *si no hubiera comido* — past counterfactual revisited**
The same past counterfactual structure from Lesson 8, with *comer*:

- *Me gustaría compartir uno, si no hubiera comido tanto.* — I'd like to share one, if I hadn't eaten so much.
- *hubiera comido* = past subjunctive of *haber* + past participle of *comer*

**4. *gran* vs. *grande* — position changes meaning**
*Grande* shortens to *gran* before a singular noun, with a meaning shift:

| Position | Form | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Before noun | *gran* | great / large | *Gran parte de la provincia* |
| After noun | *grande* | big (size) | *Una ciudad grande* |

- *Es un gran amigo mío.* — He's a great friend of mine.
- *Es un amigo grande.* — He's a big (tall/large) friend.

**5. *nada que tenga* — subjunctive after indefinite "nothing that"**
When the antecedent is an indefinite negative (*nada*, *nadie*, *ninguno*), the relative clause uses the subjunctive:

- *Pero nada que tenga chocolate.* — But nothing that has chocolate. (hypothetical — no specific dessert in mind)
- Compare: *Ese postre no tiene chocolate.* — That dessert doesn't have chocolate. (specific, identified → indicative)

**6. *estadounidense* — precise demonym for US citizens**
*Estadounidense* (from *los Estados Unidos*) is more precise than *norteamericano/a* (which technically includes Canadians and Mexicans):

- *Es estadounidense.* — He/She is from the United States.
- Gender-invariable adjective: *el hombre estadounidense / la mujer estadounidense*

## Verb Conjugations

### compartir — to share
**Type:** regular -ir

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: comparto | nosotros: compartimos |
| 2nd | tú: compartes | vosotros: compartís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: comparte | ellos/ellas/Uds.: comparten |

Preterite: *compartí, compartiste, compartió, compartimos, compartisteis, compartieron*
Lesson drills: *vamos a compartir media botella*, *me gustaría compartir uno*, *vamos a compartir un postre*.

---

### venir — to come
**Type:** highly irregular (*go*-verb yo: *vengo*; stem-changing e→ie; irregular preterite *vin-*)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **vengo** | nosotros: venimos |
| 2nd | tú: **vienes** | vosotros: venís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **viene** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **vienen** |

Preterite: *vine, viniste, vino, vinimos, vinisteis, vinieron*
Conditional perfect: *habría venido* — he/she would have come
Lesson drills: *habría venido si no fuera tan caro viajar*.

---

### comer — to eat
**Type:** regular -er

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: como | nosotros: comemos |
| 2nd | tú: comes | vosotros: coméis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: come | ellos/ellas/Uds.: comen |

Past subjunctive (drilled): *hubiera comido* — had eaten
Lesson drills: *si no hubiera comido tanto*, *no puede comerlo tampoco*.

---

### ser — to be (identity/inherent)
**Type:** highly irregular

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **soy** | nosotros: somos |
| 2nd | tú: **eres** | vosotros: sois |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **es** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **son** |

Imperfect subjunctive (drilled): *si no fuera* — if it weren't
Lesson drills: *soy alérgico al chocolate*, *ella también es alérgica*, *es una región vinícola muy conocida*, *si no fuera tan caro viajar*.

## Summary

- **compartir** — regular -ir; *vamos a compartir* = let's share; *habría venido* = would have come
- **venir** — highly irregular (*vengo*, preterite *vine*); conditional perfect *habría venido* = would have come
- **comer** — regular -er; past subjunctive *hubiera comido* in past counterfactual *si* clause
- **ser** — highly irregular; imperfect subjunctive *fuera* in present counterfactual *si* clause (*si no fuera tan caro*)
- **mientras** + imperfect = "while [ongoing action]" interrupted by a preterite event
- **gran** (before noun) = great/large; **grande** (after noun) = big
- **nada/nadie/ninguno que** + subjunctive = indefinite antecedent triggers subjunctive
