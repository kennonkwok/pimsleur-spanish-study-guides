## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *quizá / quizás* + Present Subjunctive**
When *quizá(s)* expresses genuine uncertainty (the speaker doesn't know if the action will happen), it triggers the present subjunctive. This is one of the first productive uses of the subjunctive in Level 3.

- *Quizá vayamos a otro parque.* — Maybe we'll go to another park.
- *Quizá vayamos a verlo el sábado.* — Maybe we'll see it on Saturday.
- *Quizá vayamos el domingo.* — Maybe we'll go on Sunday.

The key subjunctive form drilled is *vayamos* (nosotros subjunctive of *ir*). If the speaker is more confident, indicative can follow *quizá*, but the subjunctive is the dominant pattern here.

**2. *acabar de* + infinitive (immediate past)**
*Acabar de* + infinitive expresses something that just happened ("to have just..."). The conjugation of *acabar* sets the subject and tense.

- *Acaba de regresar.* — She just returned.
- *Acabas de conseguir un ascenso.* — You just got a promotion.
- *Acaba de terminar sus estudios.* — She just finished her studies.

**3. *se me olvidó* — The accidental/unplanned *se***
This construction removes personal blame and frames the forgetting as something that happened *to* the speaker. Structure: *se + indirect object pronoun + olvidar (3rd person, agreeing with the thing forgotten)*.

- *Se me olvidó.* — I forgot. (Lit. "It forgot itself to me.")
- *Se me olvidó tu refresco.* — I forgot your soda.
- *Se me olvidó el refresco otra vez.* — I forgot the soda again.
- *Se me olvidó cómo se llama.* — I forgot what it's called.

The same construction applies to other "accidental" verbs: *se me cayó* (I dropped it), *se me perdió* (I lost it).

**4. *dejar* — to leave something behind**
*Dejar* is used for intentionally or accidentally leaving an object in a place. Contrast with *salir* (to leave/go out) which is used for people leaving a location.

- *Dejé las flores arriba.* — I left the flowers upstairs.
- *Lo dejé en mi apartamento.* — I left it in my apartment.
- *Lo dejé en la mesa.* — I left it on the table.

**5. Postpositive possessives: *una amiga mía***
Spanish can place the possessive *after* the noun (with the indefinite article) to express "a friend of mine" — emphasizing the relationship.

- *Una amiga mía lo conoce.* — A friend of mine knows it.
- Pattern: indefinite article + noun + *mío/mía/tuyo/tuya...*

**6. *mientras tanto* — "in the meantime"**
A fixed adverb phrase drilled repeatedly in this lesson, always following a contrast or interruption situation.

- *Pero mientras tanto, ¿te gustaría tomar algo?* — But in the meantime, would you like something to drink?

## Verb Conjugations

### perder — to lose / to miss
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie) — stem changes in all present forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **pierdo** | nosotros: perdemos |
| 2nd | tú: **pierdes** | vosotros: perdéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **pierde** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **pierden** |

Reflexive form (*perderse*) used throughout: *No quiero perderme nada.* / *No quieres perderte nada.* / *No quiero perdermelo.*

## Summary

- **perder** — stem-changing (e→ie); reflexive *perderse* = to miss out on; *perdermelo/perdertelo* = miss it (with direct object pronoun appended)
- **terminar** — regular -ar; used with *ir a* (future) and *acabar de* (immediate past)
- **regresar** — regular -ar; synonym of *volver* for returning
- **acabar** — regular -ar; key phrase: *acabar de* + infinitive = "to have just done something"
- **conseguir** — stem-changing (e→i), irregular yo (*consigo*); "to get/obtain"
- **traer** — irregular (*traigo* in yo); "to bring"; drilled with *ir a* construction
- **olvidar** — regular -ar; lesson uses *se me olvidó* (accidental reflexive preterite) throughout
- **dejar** — regular -ar; "to leave something behind"; preterite *dejé* drilled; distinct from *salir*
- **sentarse** — reflexive, stem-changing (e→ie); *siéntate* = sit down (tú command)
- **ir** — irregular; key new form: present subjunctive *vayamos* after *quizá* to express "maybe we'll go"
