## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *se ha comido* — present perfect of reflexive *comerse***
A key new structure. *Comerse* (reflexive of *comer*) expresses mechanical consumption:

- *El cajero automático se ha comido mi tarjeta.* — The ATM has eaten my card.
- *El cajero automático no se ha comido mi tarjeta.* — The ATM hasn't eaten my card.
- Structure: *se* (reflexive) + *ha* (3rd sg. of *haber*) + *comido* (past participle)

Note the contrast between possessive: *se ha comido mi tarjeta* vs. *se ha comido tu tarjeta*.

**2. *mientras* + present tense — "while"**
*Mientras* introduces a simultaneous action. The verb that follows stays in the present tense:

- *Mientras espera, puede llenar este formulario.* — While you wait, you can fill out this form.
- *Mientras verifico la fecha...* — While I check the date...

**3. *fuera del país* — "out of the country"**
Fixed phrase for expressing absence abroad:

- *Voy a estar fuera del país.* — I'm going to be out of the country.
- *Tengo que perderme la exposición.* — I have to miss the trade show.
- *La próxima vez yo quiero estar fuera del país.* — Next time I want to be out of the country. (ironic)

**4. *estoy equivocado/a* — "I'm wrong"**
Uses *estar* (not *ser*) because it describes a temporary state:

- *Estoy equivocado.* (male speaker) — I'm wrong.
- *Estoy equivocada.* (female speaker) — I'm wrong.
- *No, no estoy equivocado.* — No, I'm not wrong.
- *Puedo estar equivocado.* — I could be wrong.

**5. *antes de* + infinitive — "before doing something"**
*Antes de* is always followed by an infinitive (never a conjugated verb in this construction):

- *Antes de irme, tengo que comprar unas cosas.* — Before I leave, I have to buy a few things.
- *Antes de salir.* — Before leaving.

**6. Calendar dates**
Dates use the format *el [number] de [month]*:

- *El dieciséis de enero.* — The 16th of January.
- *El trece de enero.* — The 13th of January.
- *Comienza el dieciséis. Termina el diecinueve.* — It starts the 16th. It ends the 19th.

## Verb Conjugations

### comer — to eat
**Type:** regular -er; *comerse* = reflexive form (to eat something up)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: como | nosotros: comemos |
| 2nd | tú: comes | vosotros: coméis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: come | ellos/ellas/Uds.: comen |

Present perfect: *he comido, has comido, ha comido, hemos comido, habéis comido, han comido*
Reflexive: *me he comido, te has comido, **se ha comido**, nos hemos comido…*
Lesson drill: *el cajero automático se ha comido mi tarjeta*.

## Summary

- **comer / comerse** — regular -er; reflexive *comerse*; present perfect *se ha comido* — key idiom for "the ATM swallowed my card"
- **verificar** — regular -ar (c→qu spelling change); *verificar la fecha / los números*
- **mirar** — regular -ar; *mirar en el calendario* = to check the calendar
- **comenzar** — stem-changing (e→ie) + z→c spelling change; *comienza el dieciséis*
- **esperar** — regular -ar; *mientras espera* = while you wait
