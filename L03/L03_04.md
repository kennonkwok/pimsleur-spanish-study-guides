## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *recomendar* with indirect object pronouns (te / les)**

*Recomendar* (e→ie) requires an indirect object pronoun to say "recommend TO someone." The lesson contrasts singular and plural recipients:

| Pronoun | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *me* | to me | *¿Qué me recomiendas?* — What do you recommend to me? |
| *te* | to you (singular) | *Te recomiendo esta botella.* — I recommend to you this bottle. |
| *les* | to you (plural) / to them | *Les recomiendo la especialidad de la casa.* — I recommend to you (all) the house specialty. |

- Word order: pronoun + verb + noun → *Te recomiendo este vino.*
- Question form: *¿Qué me recomiendas?* (informal) / *¿Qué les recomiendo?* (formal, plural)

**2. *quisiera* — polite "I would like"**

*Quisiera* is the imperfect subjunctive of *querer*, used as a softened, polite request. It does not conjugate differently per lesson drill — always *quisiera* for "I'd like/want":

- *Quisiera una cerveza.* — I'd like a beer.
- *Quisiera una botella de vino.* — I'd like a bottle of wine.
- More polite than *quiero* (I want), preferred when ordering.

**3. Adjective agreement with nationalities**

Nationality adjectives agree in gender and number with the noun they modify. Lesson contrasts masculine (-o) and feminine (-a) endings, and shows postposition (adjective after noun):

| Masculine | Feminine | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *francés* | *francesa* | *un vino francés* / *una empresa francesa* |
| *italiano* | *italiana* | *vino italiano* / *una italiana* (a female Italian) |
| *chileno* | *chilena* | *el gerente chileno* / *mi profesora chilena* |

Note: *francés* / *francesa* also means "the French language" (*el francés*).

**4. Personal *a* with *conocer***

When *conocer* refers to knowing a specific person, Spanish requires the personal *a* before the direct object:

- *Conozco a Juan.* — I know Juan. (specific person → *a* required)
- *Conozco una empresa.* — I know a company. (not a person → no *a*)

**5. Preterite review: *aprender* and *llegar***

The lesson revisits preterite forms in the opening dialogue:

| Verb | yo | tú |
|---|---|---|
| *aprender* | *aprendí* | *aprendiste* |
| *llegar* | *llegué* | *llegaste* |

- *Aprendí mucho.* — I learned a lot.
- *Lo aprendí en la universidad.* — I learned it at the university.
- *¿Dónde lo aprendiste?* — Where did you learn it?

**6. *fue* — preterite of *ser***

*Fue* is the preterite 3rd-person singular of both *ser* and *ir* (context disambiguates):

- *¿Fue difícil?* — Was it difficult? (*ser* context)
- *Fue un poco difícil.* — It was a little difficult.

## Verb Conjugations

### recomendar — to recommend
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie) — stem changes in all forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **recomiendo** | nosotros: recomendamos |
| 2nd | tú: **recomiendas** | vosotros: recomendáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **recomienda** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **recomiendan** |

Lesson drills: *¿Qué me recomiendas?*, *te recomiendo*, *les recomiendo la especialidad*.

---

### conocer — to know (a person or place)
**Type:** irregular *yo* only (*go*-type: yo → *conozco*); all other forms are regular -er

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **conozco** | nosotros: conocemos |
| 2nd | tú: conoces | vosotros: conocéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: conoce | ellos/ellas/Uds.: conocen |

Lesson drills: *Lo conozco.*, *Conozco a Juan.*

Note: *conocer* = to be acquainted with (people, places). *saber* = to know facts/skills. Not interchangeable.

## Summary

- **recomendar** (e→ie) — core verb of the lesson; paired with indirect object pronouns *me / te / les* to say "recommend to me/you/you all"; drills *recomiendo*, *recomiendas*, *les recomiendo*
- **conocer** — irregular *yo* (*conozco*); always use personal *a* with people: *Conozco a Juan*
- **vender / comprar** — regular -er/-ar; drilled in 3rd-person singular and combined: *compra y vende*
- **quisiera** — imperfect subjunctive of *querer*; fixed polite form for ordering; more polite than *quiero*
- **Nationality adjectives** — agree in gender (-o/-a); placed after noun; *francés/francesa*, *italiano/italiana*, *chileno/chilena*
- **Preterite** — *aprendí / aprendiste* (regular -er); *fue* (irregular, 3rd sg preterite of *ser*)
- **Key phrase** — *¿Desde cuándo...?* (since when); *seguro que sí/no* (strong confirmation/denial); *por eso* (that's why); *además* (besides/furthermore)
