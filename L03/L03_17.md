## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *acabar de* + infinitive (immediate past)**
*Acabar de* + infinitive expresses an action that has just been completed. The present tense of *acabar* is used regardless of when the action happened.

- *Acaba de llegar.* — She just arrived.
- *Acabo de regresar.* — I just returned.
- *Acabo de leer algo.* — I just read something.
- *Acaba de terminar sus estudios.* — She just finished her studies.
- *Acaba de llamarme con la noticia.* — She just called me with the news.

**2. *al* + infinitive (upon doing something)**
*Al* + infinitive = "upon [doing]" / "when [doing]." It describes what happens at the moment an action takes place.

- *Al regresar a Estados Unidos, ¿ya va a trabajar?* — Upon returning to the United States, is she going to work?

**3. *por* meaning "through / throughout / around"**
In this lesson, *por* expresses movement through or across a space (not *para*, which expresses destination).

- *Quiere viajar por toda América Latina.* — She wants to travel throughout all of Latin America.

**4. Absolute superlative: *-ísimo/a***
Adding *-ísimo/a* to an adjective creates an intensified form ("very/extremely/terrific"). It agrees in gender and number.

- *bueno → buenísimo/a* — very good / terrific
- *Fue un viaje buenísimo.* — It was a terrific trip.
- *Fue una estudiante buenísima.* — She was a terrific student.

**5. *tan … como* (equality comparisons)**
"As [adjective] as" = *tan* + adjective + *como*.

- *El clima es tan importante como el idioma.* — The climate is as important as the language.

**6. *ambos/ambas* (both)**
*Ambos* (m) / *ambas* (f) = "both." More formal than *los dos / las dos*.

- *Ambos son importantes.* — Both are important.
- *Ambos hermanos están casados.* — Both brothers are married.

**7. Preterite of *querer*: *quise***
*Quise* (yo preterite of *querer*) = "I wanted to" / "I tried to." Note: in preterite, *querer* often implies an attempt.

- *Quise quedarme más tiempo.* — I wanted to stay longer.

**8. Preterite of *tener*: *tuve***
*Tuve que* + infinitive = "I had to [do something]" (past obligation).

- *Tuve que regresar.* — I had to return.
- *Tuve que regresar a la oficina.* — I had to return to the office.

**9. *siéntate* — informal imperative of *sentarse***
*Siéntate* is the *tú* affirmative imperative of *sentarse*. Stem-changes: e→ie.

- *Siéntate, por favor.* — Sit down, please.
- *Siéntate con nosotros.* — Sit down with us.
- *Siéntate a tomar un café.* — Sit down and have a coffee.

**10. *estar* + gerund (present progressive)**
*Estar* + present participle (-ando/-iendo) expresses an ongoing action.

- *Estamos hablando.* — We are talking.
- *Estamos hablando de algo interesante.* — We're talking about something interesting.
- *Estamos hablando de las diferencias entre los países.* — We're talking about the differences between the countries.
