## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *quiero que* + present subjunctive — "I want it to"**
When *querer* has a different subject in the dependent clause, Spanish uses *que* + subjunctive:

- *Y quiero que tenga un gimnasio.* — And I want it to have a gym. (*tenga* = present subj. of *tener*)
- Compare: *Quiero tener un gimnasio.* — I want to have a gym. (same subject → infinitive)

**2. *¿qué tan grande?* — Mexican Spanish "how big?"**
A colloquial Mexican Spanish question structure:
- *¿Qué tan grande es?* — How big is it?
- Pattern: *¿qué tan* + adjective?

**3. *así es* — "that's right"**
A versatile confirmation expression:
- *Así es.* — That's right. / Exactly.
- Used to confirm what the other person just said.

**4. *por lo menos* — "at least"**
Expresses a minimum:
- *Necesito por lo menos dos dormitorios.* — I need at least two bedrooms.
- *Por lo menos seis o siete personas.* — At least six or seven people.

**5. *hace* + time + *imperfect* — recent past state**
- *Estaba por aquí hace unos minutos.* — It was around here a few minutes ago.
- *Hace poco estaban por aquí.* — They were around here a little while ago.

**6. *de* + day-of-week as genitive**
*De* + day creates a possessive/temporal relationship:
- *La presentación de lunes.* — Monday's presentation. (the presentation of Monday)
- *La reunión de mañana.* — Tomorrow's meeting. (from prior lessons)

## Verb Conjugations

|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: alquilo | nosotros: alquilamos |

| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: alquila | ellos/ellas/Uds.: alquilan |

## Summary

- **alquilar** — regular -ar; *el alquiler* = the rent
- **visitar** — regular -ar; *voy a visitarlo* = I'm going to visit him
- **tener** — irregular; present subjunctive *tenga* used in *quiero que tenga* construction
