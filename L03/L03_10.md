## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Preterite of *ir* and *ser* (identical forms)**
*Ir* and *ser* share the same preterite paradigm. Context determines meaning.

| Person | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| yo | fui | *Fui a la fiesta.* — I went to the party. |
| tú | fuiste | *¿Cuándo fuiste a Puerto Rico?* |
| él/ella/Ud. | fue | *La fiesta fue muy divertida.* — The party was very fun. |
| nosotros | fuimos | *Fuimos a bailar.* — We went dancing. |
| ellos/Uds. | fueron | *Pablo y Sofía fueron también.* — They went too. |

Note: *fue* = "it was" (ser) or "she/he went" (ir) — context resolves the ambiguity.

---

**2. *Había* — imperfect of *haber* (there was / there were)**
Used to describe past states or what existed, not what happened. Invariable (always singular form regardless of noun count).

- *Había comida deliciosa.* — There was delicious food.
- *Había mucha comida picante.* — There was a lot of spicy food.
- *Había música muy buena.* — There was very good music.
- *Había un restaurante muy bueno.* — There was a very good restaurant.

Contrast with *hubo* (preterite = there was a specific event) — Pimsleur uses *había* throughout this lesson for descriptions.

---

**3. *Fue* vs. *estaba* for past descriptions**
The lesson explicitly flags this contrast when describing food:

| Form | Type | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| *fue* | preterite of *ser* | Completed, defining characteristic: *La comida fue deliciosa.* |
| *estaba* | imperfect of *estar* | Ongoing condition during a period: *Estaba deliciosa.* |

Both are correct for describing food quality. Pimsleur recommends using *fue* when in doubt at this stage.

---

**4. *Me hubiera gustado* — past conditional of *gustar* ("I would have liked")**
This is the key new structure of the lesson. Pattern: *me hubiera gustado* + infinitive.

- *Me hubiera gustado ir a la fiesta.* — I would have liked to go to the party.
- *No me hubiera gustado ir a bailar.* — I would not have liked to go dancing.
- *Me hubiera gustado quedarme más tiempo.* — I would have liked to stay longer.

The formula is fixed: **me/te/le + hubiera gustado + infinitive**. The verb *hubiera* is the imperfect subjunctive of *haber*; *gustado* is the past participle of *gustar*.

---

**5. Preterite of *pedir* (e→i stem-changer)**
*Pedir* changes the stem vowel e→i in the preterite 3rd person (él, ellos) only; 1st person yo uses the changed stem as well.

- *Pedí un taxi.* — I ordered a taxi. (yo: e→i stem)
- *Pedí la lista ayer.* — I requested the list yesterday.
- *Tengo que pedirla otra vez.* — I have to request it again. (infinitive + clitic)

---

**6. *Hace* + time period = "ago"**
To say how long ago something happened: **preterite + *hace* + time period**.

- *Fui hace tres meses.* — I went three months ago.

---

**7. Direct object clitics attached to infinitives**
The lesson drills clitics appended to infinitives. The clitic refers back to a previously mentioned noun.

- *Tengo que pedirla otra vez.* — I have to request it again. (*la lista*)
- *Voy a pedirla otra vez.* — I'm going to request it again.
- *Allí está mi taxi.* → *la lista de los invitados que vienen* referred back to as *-la*

## Verb Conjugations

### ir — to go / ser — to be (shared preterite)
**Type:** highly irregular (preterite identical for both verbs)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **fui** | nosotros: **fuimos** |
| 2nd | tú: **fuiste** | vosotros: **fuisteis** |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **fue** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **fueron** |

Lesson drills: *fui a la fiesta*, *fuimos a bailar*, *fueron también*, *fue muy divertida*, *fue una fiesta de compromiso*, *¿cuándo fuiste a Puerto Rico?*

## Summary

- **Key new structure:** *me hubiera gustado* + infinitive = "I would have liked to [do]" — fixed formula, used in positive and negative forms throughout the lesson.
- ***ir/ser* preterite:** identical forms (*fui, fuiste, fue, fuimos, fueron*) — context resolves ambiguity; heavily drilled for travel and past event narration.
- ***había*:** imperfect of *haber* for past descriptions ("there was/were"); invariable; contrasts with *hubo* (specific occurrence).
- ***fue* vs. *estaba*:** both acceptable for describing past qualities (food, events); *fue* recommended at this stage.
- ***pedir* preterite:** e→i stem change in yo (*pedí*) and 3rd persons (*pidió, pidieron*); object clitics attach to infinitives (*pedirla*).
- ***hace* + time:** fixed pattern for "ago" — *fui hace tres meses*.
- **Engagement vocabulary:** *comprometerse* / *se comprometieron*, *la fiesta de compromiso* — reflexive preterite construction.
- **Setting:** dual context — office meeting logistics (guest list, hotel reservations, engineers from Peru) and social narration (engagement party, dancing, food, travel to Puerto Rico/Costa Rica).
