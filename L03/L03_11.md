## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Preterite of *ir* / *ser* — they share the same forms**
Both *ir* (to go) and *ser* (to be) have identical preterite conjugations. Context disambiguates meaning.

| Form | *ir* meaning | *ser* meaning |
|---|---|---|
| fui | I went | I was |
| fuiste | you went | you were |
| fue | he/she/it went | he/she/it was |
| fuimos | we went | we were |
| fueron | they went | they were |

- *Anoche fui a la fiesta.* — Last night I went to the party.
- *Fue divertida.* — It was fun. (ser)
- *Fuimos a bailar.* — We went dancing.
- *Roberto y Sonia también fueron.* — Roberto and Sonia also went.

**2. *irse* — reflexive *ir* = "to leave / to go away"**
Adding the reflexive pronoun (*se*) to *ir* shifts the meaning from "to go (somewhere)" to "to leave / to go away":

- *Fueron.* — They went. (destination implied)
- *Se fueron.* — They left. / They went away. (departure emphasized)
- *Se fue.* — He/she left.
- *Se fueron después del discurso.* — They left after the speech.
- *Quiénes más se fueron.* — Who else left?

**3. *quedarse* — reflexive *quedar* = "to stay / to remain"**
The reflexive form *quedarse* means "to stay behind / to remain":

- *Tuve que quedarme hasta tarde.* — I had to stay until late.
- *Los ingenieros también se quedaron.* — The engineers also stayed.
- *No se quedaron para el almuerzo.* — They didn't stay for lunch.
- *No sé por qué no se quedaron.* — I don't know why they didn't stay.

**4. *me hubiera gustado* + infinitive — "I would have liked to…"**
This is the past conditional of *gustar*. It expresses regret about something that didn't happen. The structure is fixed: *me hubiera gustado* + infinitive.

- *Me hubiera gustado bailar.* — I would have liked to dance.
- *Me hubiera gustado ir.* — I would have liked to go.
- *Me hubiera gustado bailar contigo.* — I would have liked to dance with you.
- *Me hubiera gustado llegar más temprano.* — I would have liked to arrive earlier.

**5. Preterite of -ar verbs (first-person singular) — spelling change**
Regular -ar verbs ending in *-car*, *-gar*, *-zar* undergo spelling changes in the preterite *yo* form to preserve pronunciation. *Escuchar* is a plain -ar verb; the preterite *yo* = *escuché* (accent marks required).

- *No escuché el discurso.* — I didn't hear the speech.
- *El taxi llegó tarde.* — The taxi arrived late.

**6. *entre* — "between" (two) and "among" (multiple)**
*Entre* works for both "between" (two referents) and "among" (a group):

- *Entre ellos.* — Among themselves.
- *Entre nosotros.* — Between us.
- *Entre Filadelfia y Washington.* — Between Philadelphia and Washington.

**7. Reported speech with *dijo que* — "he said that…"**
*Dijo* (preterite of *decir*, 3rd sg) introduces reported speech. The clause that follows uses its own tense:

- *Dijo que la empresa tiene problemas.* — He said that the company has problems.

## Summary

- **ir** — highly irregular; preterite (*fui/fuiste/fue/fuimos/fueron*) is identical to preterite of *ser*; reflexive *irse* = to leave/go away
- **comer** — regular -er; preterite *comimos* / *comieron* drilled extensively
- **quedarse** — regular -ar reflexive; preterite *quedé/quedaron* = stayed; key contrast with *irse*
- **tener** — irregular; preterite stem *tuv-*; *tuve que* + inf. = I had to (preterite obligation)
- **escuchar** — regular -ar; preterite *escuché* (yo) / *escuchaste* (tú) drilled in both persons
- **hablar** — regular -ar; preterite *hablaron* (they talked) drilled in context of meeting aftermath
- **decir** — irregular (*digo*, e→i, preterite stem *dij-*); only *dijo* (3rd sg preterite) drilled
- **comprometerse** — regular -er reflexive; preterite *se comprometieron* = they got engaged
- **divertirse** — reflexive stem-changing (e→ie); preterite *nos divertimos* = we had fun
- **creer** — regular -er; only present (*creo*) drilled
- **bailar** — regular -ar; used only as infinitive complement in *fuimos a bailar* / *me hubiera gustado bailar*
