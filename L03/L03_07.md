## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *Ojalá* + que + indicative/subjunctive**
*Ojalá* expresses hope or a wish. It derives from Arabic (*inshallah*). When followed by *que*, it typically takes the subjunctive for a future wish, but the fixed expression *ojalá que sí* ("I hope so") uses *sí* directly.

- *Ojalá que sí.* — I hope so. (Lit. "God willing, yes.")
- *Ojalá que podamos ir.* — I hope we can go. (subjunctive)

**2. *Quizá(s)* — perhaps**
Can precede indicative (more certain) or subjunctive (less certain). Lesson uses indicative:

- *Quizá el mes entrante.* — Perhaps next month.
- *Quizá está en una de estas carpetas.* — Maybe it's in one of these folders.

**3. *Tan … como* — as … as (equality comparisons)**
Structure: *tan* + adjective/adverb + *como* + reference point. Used for both positive equality and negated inequality:

- *No es tan interesante como crees.* — It's not as interesting as you think.
- *No tan caro como pensé.* — Not as expensive as I thought.

**4. *Estar* in the imperfect — *estaba* (was located)**
The imperfect of *estar* is used to say where something *was* (ongoing past location):

- *¿Dónde estaba el archivo?* — Where was the file?
- *El archivo estaba en esa carpeta.* — The file was in that folder.
- *Estaba debajo de estos papeles.* — It was under these papers.

**5. Present perfect — *haber* + past participle**
First appearance of the present perfect in this lesson. Formed with *he/has/ha/hemos/habéis/han* + past participle (-ado/-ido):

- *Ha viajado mucho.* — He has traveled a lot. (*viajar* → *viajado*)
- *Pero no he podido.* — But I haven't been able to. (*poder* → *podido*)

Note: *poder* has a regular past participle (*podido*) even though it is a stem-changer.

**6. Preterite of *pagar* — spelling change**
*Pagar* is regular in the preterite except in the *yo* form, where *g* → *gu* before *e* to preserve the [g] sound:

- *yo pagué* (I paid) — NOT *pagé*
- *él/la empresa pagó* (he/it paid)

**7. *Con tarjeta de crédito* — payment with credit card**
Spanish uses *con* (with) for payment method, not a preposition like "by":

- *Siempre pago con tarjeta de crédito.* — I always pay by credit card.
- *¿Puedo pagar con tarjeta?* — Can I pay by card?

**8. Demonstrative adjectives — *este/ese/aquel* review**
Lesson drills all three distances with masculine and feminine, singular and plural:

| | Masc. sg | Fem. sg | Masc. pl | Fem. pl |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| This (near) | este | esta | estos | estas |
| That (mid) | ese | esa | esos | esas |
| That (far) | aquel | aquella | aquellos | aquellas |

Examples: *esa carpeta*, *esos papeles*, *estas carpetas*, *ese archivo*.

## Summary

- **Core theme:** trip planning — changing hotel reservations, making reservations, discussing what has and hasn't been done.
- **cambiar / hacer / llamar** — regular verbs used in *hay que* and *tener que* + infinitive structures.
- **saber vs. conocer** — both mean "to know" but are not interchangeable: *saber* = facts/how; *conocer* = people/places/things.
- **Present perfect introduced** — *haber* + past participle: *ha viajado*, *no he podido*, *no he visto*. Key past participles: *viajado*, *podido*, *visto* (irregular).
- **pagar** — regular -ar in present; preterite *yo* = *pagué* (spelling change to preserve [g] sound). Third-person preterite: *pagó*.
- **pensar** — e→ie stem-changer in present; preterite is fully regular (*pensé*, *pensó*).
- **Tan … como** — equality comparison: *no tan caro como pensé*, *no es tan interesante como crees*.
- **Imperfect of estar** — *estaba/estaban* for past location.
- **Payment phrasing** — *con tarjeta de crédito* (by credit card); *con* = by/with for payment methods.
- **Demonstratives drilled** — *este/ese/aquel* and their feminine/plural forms across multiple office/shopping contexts.
