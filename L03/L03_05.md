## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Indirect-object pronouns for recommendation: *me*, *te*, *les***

*Recomendar* takes an indirect object (the person receiving the recommendation). The pronoun goes before the conjugated verb.

| Pronoun | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| me | to me | *¿Me recomiendas este vino?* — Do you recommend this wine to me? |
| te | to you (informal) | *Te recomiendo la especialidad.* — I recommend the specialty to you. |
| les | to you (plural) / to them | *Les recomiendo el café.* — I recommend the coffee to you (both). |

Note: *les* is used when addressing more than one person (*a ustedes*). This is the Latin American standard; Spain uses *os* for *vosotros*.

---

**2. *estar* (imperfect) for temporary past states: *estaba / estaban***

The imperfect of *estar* describes what *was* (temporarily) in a past state or location. This contrasts with the preterite *fue/fueron* (ser), which marks permanent or definitional past conditions.

| Form | Example | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| *estaba* (sg) | *Estaba aquí esta mañana.* | It was here this morning. (temporary) |
| *estaban* (pl) | *Estaban perdidos.* | They were lost. |
| *estaba* (sg) | *Estaba en la mesa.* | It was on the table. |

Contrast with permanent/definitional past:
- *Fue un regalo de mi hermano.* — It was a gift from my brother. (*ser* preterite — defining what it was)
- *Fue difícil aprender.* — It was difficult to learn. (*ser* preterite — characteristic)

---

**3. Prepositions of position: *debajo de* and *al lado de***

Two spatial expressions drilled extensively in the second half of the lesson:

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *debajo de* | under / beneath | *Está debajo de esos papeles.* — It's under those papers. |
| *al lado de* | next to / beside | *Mi lapicero estaba al lado de mis apuntes.* — My pen was next to my notes. |

Both take *de* and must agree with what follows — no gender agreement needed on the preposition itself.

---

**4. Direct-object pronoun *lo* with verbs of buying/selling/seeing**

*Lo* (it, him — masc.) appears consistently as the direct object replacing a previously mentioned masculine noun:

- *Entonces lo conozco.* — In that case I know him. (*lo* = Martín)
- *¿Lo vendes aquí?* — Do you sell it here? (*lo* = el vino)
- *Creo que lo he visto.* — I believe I have seen it. (*lo* = el libro)
- *Pero ahora los tengo.* — But now I have them. (*los* = los apuntes, plural)

---

**5. *que* as a relative pronoun (linking clauses)**

*Que* joins two clauses, equivalent to English "that" or "which":

- *Trabaja en una empresa que compra y vende vino.* — He works at a company that buys and sells wine.
- *Pensé que estaban perdidos.* — I thought that they were lost.
- *Creo que lo he visto.* — I believe that I have seen it.

## Verb Conjugations

### recomendar — to recommend
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie); -ar verb — stem changes all persons except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **recomiendo** | nosotros: recomendamos |
| 2nd | tú: **recomiendas** | vosotros: recomendáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **recomienda** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **recomiendan** |

Lesson drills: *¿Me recomiendas este vino?*, *Les recomiendo la especialidad de la casa.*

---

### conocer — to know (a person or place)
**Type:** irregular yo (*go*-verb pattern); otherwise regular -er

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **conozco** | nosotros: conocemos |
| 2nd | tú: conoces | vosotros: conocéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: conoce | ellos/ellas/Uds.: conocen |

Lesson drills: *Entonces lo conozco.* Note: *conocer* ≠ *saber*; *conocer* = knowing a person/place by acquaintance, *saber* = knowing facts/how to do something.

## Summary

- **Core theme:** Describing past locations and states (imperfect *estaba/estaban*) vs. permanent past identity (*fue* — ser preterite). Spatial prepositions *debajo de* and *al lado de* drilled heavily.
- **recomendar** — stem-changing (e→ie) -ar; indirect-object pronouns *me/te/les* used throughout restaurant and café scenarios.
- **conocer** — irregular yo (*conozco*); distinguish from *saber* (know facts). Drilled in acquaintance context (*lo conozco*).
- **vender / comprar** — both regular; paired repeatedly (*compra y vende*) to describe business activity.
- **aprender** — regular -er; preterite forms (*aprendí*, *aprendiste*) introduced in compliment exchange about learning Spanish.
- **estar imperfect** — *estaba/estaban* for temporary past conditions; key contrast with *fue* (ser) for definitional/permanent past.
- **Indirect-object pronoun *les*** — new for addressing plural *ustedes* audience; *Les recomiendo...* is a key restaurant phrase.
- **Direct-object pronoun review** — *lo* (it/him masc. sg.) and *los* (them masc. pl.) used to replace previously mentioned nouns (*el libro*, *los apuntes*, *el vino*).
