## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *¿Cómo te fue?* — "How did it go for you?" (Idiomatic past expression)**
This is a key new structure. *Ir* in the preterite (3rd person) is used to express how something went for someone:

| Spanish | English |
|---|---|
| *¿Cómo te fue?* | How did it go for you? |
| *¿Cómo te fue en la reunión?* | How did it go for you in the meeting? |
| *Me fue bien.* | It went well for me. |
| *Me fue muy bien.* | It went very well for me. |

Note: The indirect object pronoun (*me*, *te*, *le*) shifts depending on the person; the verb *fue* stays 3rd person singular because the "trip/meeting" is the subject.

**2. *cuál / cuáles* — "which / which ones"**
Introduced explicitly in the lesson intro. Distinct from *qué* (what):

- *¿Cuáles son las diferencias?* — Which are the differences?
- *¿Cuál es el problema?* — Which is the problem? / What is the problem?
- Use *cuál(es)* when selecting from a set; use *qué* + noun for definitions (*¿Qué es esto?*).

**3. *deber* (conditional) — soft obligation**
*Deberías* (you should / you ought to) is the conditional of *deber*. Used to give advice or polite obligation:

- *Deberías saber cuáles son las diferencias.* — You should know which the differences are.
- Pattern: *debería* / *deberías* / *debería* / *deberíamos* + infinitive

**4. *quizá(s)* + subjunctive — expressing uncertainty**
When *quizá(s)* is used to mean "maybe" with a sense of uncertainty, it triggers the present subjunctive:

- *Quizá tengas más tiempo.* — Maybe you'll have more time. (*tengas* = subj. of *tener*)
- *Quizá tu hermana tenga más suerte.* — Maybe your sister will be luckier. (*tenga* = subj. of *tener*)

**5. *hacer* in the preterite — "they asked me questions"**
*Hicieron* is the 3rd-person plural preterite of *hacer* (irregular). *Hacer preguntas* = to ask questions (lit. "to make questions"):

- *Me hicieron preguntas.* — They asked me questions.
- *Me hicieron muchas preguntas.* — They asked me many questions.

**6. *dedicarse a* — profession/occupation**
*¿A qué se dedica?* is the standard way to ask what someone does for a living in Mexican/Latin American Spanish (more formal than *¿en qué trabaja?*):

- *¿A qué se dedica tu sobrina?* — What does your niece do (for a living)?
- *Se dedica a escribir.* — She dedicates herself to writing.

**7. Absolute superlative: *-ísimo/a***
Adding *-ísimo/a* to an adjective creates an absolute superlative ("very/extremely ___"):

- *bueno → buenísimo* — very good / terrific
- *fácil → facilísimo* — extremely easy

## Summary

- **ir** — highly irregular; preterite *fui/fue* used extensively: *fui a una reunión*, *fue un éxito*, *me fue bien/muy bien*, *¿cómo te fue?*
- **hacer** — irregular (*go*-verb, preterite *hic-*); preterite 3rd pl. *hicieron* drilled in *me hicieron preguntas*
- **tener** — irregular; preterite *tuve* (had to, didn't have time); present subjunctive *tengas/tenga* after *quizá*
- **deber** — regular -er; conditional *deberías* = you should
- **vivir** — regular -ir; present perfect *has vivido* (you have lived)
- **regresar** — regular -ar; synonym of *volver*; *acabo de regresar* / *tuve que regresar*
- **terminar** — regular -ar; *terminar de* + inf. = to finish doing something
- **escribir** — regular -ir; irregular past participle *escrito*
- **casarse** — regular -ar, reflexive; *se casa* = is getting married
