## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Professions with *ser* — no article**
In Spanish, when stating a profession after *ser*, the indefinite article is dropped (unlike English):
- *Soy gerente.* — I'm a manager.
- *Es ingeniero.* — He's an engineer.
- *Es médico.* — He's a doctor.

Exception: when a modifier is added, the article returns — *Es un buen ingeniero.*

**2. *¿A qué te/se dedica(s)?* — asking about profession**
Literally "What do you dedicate yourself to?" This is the most natural way to ask *What do you do?* in Latin American Spanish:
- *¿A qué te dedicas?* — What do you do? (informal)
- *¿A qué se dedica su esposa?* — What does his wife do? (formal)

**3. *estar* + *casado/a* — married**
Marital state uses *estar* (changeable condition), not *ser*:
- *Mi hermano está casado con una española.* — My brother is married to a Spaniard.
- *¿Es casado?* — Is he married? (Note: *es casado* also appears in some Latin American usage, but *está casado* is standard)

**4. Preterite of *aprender* (and other regular -er verbs)**
Lesson drills three preterite forms of *aprender*:
| Person | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| yo | *aprendí* | Lo aprendí en Estados Unidos. |
| tú | *aprendiste* | ¿Dónde aprendiste español? |
| él/ella | *aprendió* | Aprendió italiano de ella. |

**5. Present perfect: *ha aprendido***
The lesson introduces the present perfect briefly:
- *Ya ha aprendido mucho español.* — He has already learned a lot of Spanish.
- Structure: *haber* (present) + past participle (-ado/-ido)
- *aprender* → past participle: *aprendido*

**6. Comparative: *más … que* / *más fácil / más difícil***
Standard Spanish comparison of inequality:
- *El inglés es más difícil que el español.* — English is more difficult than Spanish.
- *El español es más fácil.* — Spanish is easier.
- Note: languages take the definite article (*el inglés*, *el español*) when discussed as subjects.

**7. Conditional: *podríamos***
The lesson introduces one conditional form without full paradigm drilling:
- *Entonces, podríamos hablar inglés.* — In that case, we could speak English.
- *podríamos* = conditional 1st person plural of *poder*

**8. *ojalá que sí / seguro que sí / seguro que no***
High-frequency conversational expressions:
- *Seguro que sí.* — For sure. / Definitely.
- *Seguro que no.* — Surely not. / Definitely not.
- *Ojalá que sí.* — Hopefully. / Let's hope so.

## Verb Conjugations

### aprender — to learn
**Type:** regular -er

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: aprendo | nosotros: aprendemos |
| 2nd | tú: aprendes | vosotros: aprendéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **aprende** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: aprenden |

**Preterite (drilled in this lesson):**

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|

| 2nd | tú: **aprendiste** | vosotros: aprendisteis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **aprendió** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: aprendieron |

Lesson drills: *lo aprendí en Estados Unidos*, *¿dónde aprendiste español?*, *aprendió italiano de ella*, *pero lo aprendiste*.

### quedarse — to stay / to remain
**Type:** regular -ar (reflexive)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me quedo | nosotros: nos quedamos |
| 2nd | tú: te quedas | vosotros: os quedáis |

### ir — to go (preterite, irregular)
**Type:** irregular — preterite shares forms with *ser*

**Preterite:**

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: fui | nosotros: fuimos |
| 2nd | tú: fuiste | vosotros: fuisteis |

Note: *fue* serves as preterite for both *ir* (went) and *ser* (was); context disambiguates.

## Summary

- **Core theme:** Talking about family members' professions, residences, and relationships; discussing language learning difficulty.
- **Key grammar:** Professions after *ser* take no article; *estar casado/a con* for marital status; *¿A qué te/se dedica?* for asking profession.
- **Preterite focus:** Regular -er verb *aprender* fully drilled (*aprendí*, *aprendiste*, *aprendió*); irregular *fue* (from *ir*/*ser*) introduced in fixed phrases.
- **Present perfect:** *ha aprendido* introduced as a passive drill form; full paradigm not yet drilled.
- **New expressions:** *gracias por el cumplido*, *seguro que sí/no*, *ojalá que sí*, *en verdad*, *a veces*, *ya*.
- **Comparatives:** *más … que* structure fully drilled with *difícil* and *fácil*; languages always take definite article as subjects.
- **Conditional preview:** *podríamos* introduced in context without paradigm drilling — signals upcoming Level 3 content.
