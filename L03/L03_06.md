## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Preterite of *querer*: *quise* = "I meant to / tried to"**
In the preterite, *querer* shifts meaning. It does not simply mean "I wanted" — it implies an attempted or intended action that was undertaken (but may not have succeeded).

- *Quise llamarte.* — I meant to call you. / I tried to call you.
- *Quise llamarte antes.* — I meant to call you before.
- *Quise llamarlo antes.* — I meant to call him before.

Contrast with present perfect *he querido* ("I have wanted"), which expresses ongoing or recently held desire:
- *Siempre he querido ir allí.* — I've always wanted to go there.
- *He querido hacerlas.* — I've wanted to make them.

**2. Present perfect: *haber* + past participle**
Formed with the auxiliary *haber* (present tense) + past participle (-ado / -ido):

| Infinitive | Past Participle | Example |
|---|---|---|
| tener | tenido | No he tenido tiempo. |
| querer | querido | He querido hacerlas. |
| hablar | hablado | ¿Has hablado con José? |
| ver | visto (irregular) | ¿Lo has visto? / ¿Los has visto? |

Key lesson drills:
- *No he tenido tiempo.* — I haven't had time.
- *Pero no he tenido tiempo.* — But I haven't had time.
- *¿Has hablado con José?* — Have you spoken with José?

**3. Object clitics attached to infinitives**
When an infinitive follows a conjugated verb, the direct or indirect object pronoun attaches to the end of the infinitive:

| Pronoun | Example | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| -me | *esperarme* | to wait for me |
| -te | *llamarte* | to call you |
| -lo | *llamarlo* | to call him |
| -las | *hacerlas* | to make them (f pl) |
| -nos | *ayudarnos* | to help us |

- *Gracias por esperarme.* — Thanks for waiting for me.
- *Tengo que hacerlas muy pronto.* — I have to make them very soon.
- *Voy a hacerlas pronto.* — I'm going to make them soon.
- *Antes de llamarlo.* — Before calling him.

**4. *ni...ni...* — "neither...nor..."**
Used to negate two elements simultaneously. The main verb is also negated:

- *No puedo encontrar ni mi lapicero, ni mis apuntes.* — I can't find either my pen or my notes. / I can find neither my pen nor my notes.

**5. *antes de* + infinitive**
"Before [doing something]" — *antes de* is always followed by an infinitive, not a conjugated verb:

- *Antes de ir.* — Before going.
- *Antes de hacer.* — Before making.
- *Antes de hacer las reservaciones.* — Before making the reservations.
- *Pero antes de hacer las reservaciones.* — But before making the reservations.
- *Antes de llamarlo.* — Before calling him.

**6. *saber* vs. *conocer***
Both mean "to know" but serve different purposes:

| Use *saber* for | Use *conocer* for |
|---|---|
| Facts, information, how to do something | People, places, things (familiarity/acquaintance) |
| *Sé que vamos a Estados Unidos.* | *Conoces a José.* |
| *No sé, pero hace frío allí.* | *Conoce hoteles muy buenos.* |

Note: *personal a* is required before a person with *conocer* (*Conoces **a** José*).

**7. *hacer frío* — weather expression**
"It's cold" is expressed with *hacer*, not *estar* or *ser*:

- *Pero hace frío allí en enero.* — But it's cold there in January.
- *No sé, pero hace frío allí.* — I don't know, but it's cold there.

## Verb Conjugations

### encontrar — to find
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue) — stem changes all persons except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **encuentro** | nosotros: encontramos |
| 2nd | tú: **encuentras** | vosotros: encontráis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **encuentra** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **encuentran** |

Lesson drills: *no puedo encontrar mi lapicero*, *no puedo encontrar ni mi lapicero, ni mis apuntes*.

## Summary

- **Core theme:** Planning a trip to Boston — lost items, scheduling frustrations, making reservations, and coordinating help from José.
- **encontrar** — stem-changing (o→ue); lesson uses with *no puedo* (inability to find things)
- **sentir** — stem-changing (e→ie); fixed phrase *lo siento* = "I'm sorry"
- **querer** — stem-changing (e→ie) in present; irregular preterite (*quise*) = "I meant to / tried to" — a key semantic shift from the present tense meaning
- **haber** — irregular auxiliary for present perfect (*he*, *has*, *ha*, *hemos*, *habéis*, *han*); combined with past participles (*tenido*, *querido*, *hablado*, *visto*) to express recent/ongoing past actions
- **conocer** — irregular yo (*conozco*); used for people and places (familiarity); requires *personal a* before persons
- **saber** — irregular yo (*sé*); used for facts and information; contrasts with *conocer*
- **hacer** — irregular yo (*hago*); used for making/doing and in weather expression *hace frío*
- **Object clitics on infinitives** — *esperarme*, *llamarte*, *llamarlo*, *hacerlas*, *ayudarnos* — core drill pattern throughout the lesson
- **antes de + infinitive** — "before [doing]"; infinitive always follows, never conjugated verb
- **ni...ni...** — "neither...nor..."; main verb still negated
