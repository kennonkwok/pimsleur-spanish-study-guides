## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *acaba de* + infinitive (recent past)**
*Acabar de* + infinitive expresses that something *just happened*. The form of *acabar* matches the subject; the infinitive is always in base form.

- *Juan acaba de conseguir un ascenso.* — Juan just got a promotion.
- *El proyecto acaba de terminar.* — The project just ended.
- *Mi trabajo acaba de terminar.* — My work just ended.

**2. *perder* used reflexively for "to miss"**
*Perder* alone means "to lose." When used reflexively (*perderse*), it means "to miss" an event. The reflexive clitic (*me/te/se/nos*) can attach to the end of an infinitive, or precede a conjugated verb.

- *Vas a perderte la excursión.* — You're going to miss the excursion.
- *Voy a perderme la.* — I'm going to miss it.
- *Tengo que perderme la.* — I have to miss it.
- *No tienes que perderte la.* — You don't have to miss it.
- *Estoy triste por perdérmela.* — I'm sad about missing it. (*por* + infinitive)

**3. *me fue bien/mal* — "it went well/badly for me" (preterite)**
The expression uses the preterite of *ir* in the third person, paired with an indirect object pronoun:

- *Me fue muy bien.* — It went very well for me.
- *¿Cómo te fue?* — How did it go for you?

**4. *quizá/quizás* + subjunctive**
When *quizá(s)* expresses genuine doubt (uncertain outcome), it takes the present subjunctive:

- *Quizá vayamos el sábado.* — Maybe we'll go on Saturday. (*vayamos* = 1st pl. present subjunctive of *ir*)
- *Quizá vayamos otro día.* — Maybe we'll go another day.

This is the lesson's first active drill of a first-person plural present subjunctive form in production.

**5. *me hubiera gustado* — "I would have liked to" (past conditional)**
This is the past conditional/imperfect subjunctive construction:

- *Me hubiera gustado ir.* — I would have liked to go.

The lesson introduces it as a formulaic chunk; full subjunctive paradigm is not drilled here.

**6. Duration with *durar***
*Durar* expresses how long something lasts. It works like English "to last":

- *El viaje duró dos horas.* — The trip took two hours.

## Summary

- **conseguir** — stem-changing (e→i); key phrase: *acaba de conseguir* (just obtained/got)
- **durar** — regular -ar; expresses duration; preterite *duró* drilled
- **manejar** — regular -ar; Latin American Spanish for "to drive" (*conducir* in Spain)
- **perder** — stem-changing (e→ie); reflexive *perderse* = to miss an event; clitic attachment drilled extensively
- **terminar** — regular -ar; used with *acaba de* for recent completion
- **depender** — regular -er; always followed by *de*; drilled with *tráfico*, *clima*, *hora*, *día*
- **querer** — stem-changing (e→ie); drilled in tú-form (*quieres*) and infinitive constructions
- **ir** (subjunctive) — irregular; *vayamos* (1st pl. present subjunctive) introduced via *quizá vayamos*
