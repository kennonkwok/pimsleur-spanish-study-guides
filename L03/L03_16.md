## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *acabar de* + infinitive (recent past)**
*Acabar de* + infinitive expresses something that just happened — the English "have just done."

- *Acaba de terminar la universidad.* — She just finished the university.
- *Acaba de llamarme.* — She just called me.
- *El otro acaba de terminar la universidad.* — The other one just finished the university.

The verb *acabar* conjugates normally; only the tense of *acabar* changes to shift the timeframe. In this lesson it appears exclusively in present tense (immediate past).

---

**2. *ojalá* + present subjunctive**
*Ojalá* (I hope / hopefully) is always followed by the subjunctive. It cannot take the indicative.

- *Ojalá ella tenga suficiente tiempo.* — Hopefully she has enough time.
- *Ojalá tenga suficiente dinero.* — Hopefully she has enough money.
- *Ojalá ella pueda quedarse mucho tiempo.* — Hopefully she can stay a long time.

The subjunctive form of *tener* is *tenga*; of *poder* is *pueda*.

---

**3. *quizá* + subjunctive (possibility)**
*Quizá* (perhaps / maybe) may be followed by the subjunctive when the outcome is less certain:

- *Quizá puedas venir después.* — Maybe you can come later. (*puedas* = subjunctive of *poder*)
- *Bueno, quizá otro día.* — Well, maybe another day.

---

**4. *lástima que* + subjunctive (pity/regret)**
Expressions of emotion or reaction about another subject's action trigger the subjunctive:

- *Lástima que no puedas ir a la fiesta.* — What a shame you can't go to the party.

---

**5. Absolute superlative with *-ísimo/a***
Adding *-ísimo/a* to an adjective means "extremely / really / terrific." It must agree in gender and number:

| Base adjective | Superlative (m) | Superlative (f) |
|---|---|---|
| bueno | buenísimo | buenísima |

- *Un regalo buenísimo.* — A terrific gift.
- *Fue una estudiante buenísima.* — She was a terrific student.
- *Un trabajo buenísimo.* — A terrific job.

---

**6. *desde hace* + time expression (duration from past to present)**
Used to say how long a state has been going on:

- *Está divorciada desde hace dos años.* — She has been divorced for two years.

Pattern: *[present/state verb] + desde hace + [time span]*

---

**7. *hermanos* and *padres* as gender-inclusive plurals**
In Spanish, masculine plural nouns can include both genders:

| Singular | Meaning | Plural | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| hermano | brother | hermanos | brothers OR siblings |
| padre | father | padres | fathers OR parents |

- *¿Cuántos hermanos tienes?* — How many siblings do you have?
- *Va a visitar a mis padres.* — She's going to visit my parents.

---

**8. *casarse* — present vs. preterite**
The lesson drills both the present (ongoing/future) and preterite (completed past):

- *Se casa otra vez.* — She's getting married again. (present / near future)
- *Se casó el mes pasado.* — He got married last month. (preterite)
- *Se casó el año pasado.* — He got married last year. (preterite)

## Summary

- **casarse** — reflexive, regular -ar; present (*se casa*) and preterite (*se casó*) both drilled; *casarse con* = to marry someone
- **acabar** — regular -ar; used exclusively in *acabar de* + infinitive construction (recent past)
- **terminar** — regular -ar; always as infinitive after *acabar de* in this lesson
- **comenzar** — stem-changing (e→ie); *comienza* drilled
- **durar** — regular -ar; used in *cuánto va a durar*
- **poder** — stem-changing (o→ue); present indicative and present subjunctive (*puedas*, *pueda*) both drilled via *quizá* and *ojalá*
- **tener** — irregular; present indicative and present subjunctive (*tenga*) drilled via *ojalá*; *tener suerte* = to be lucky
- **pagar** — regular -ar (g→gu spelling in preterite); preterite *pagó* drilled
- **invitar** — regular -ar; used in *gracias por invitarme* and *quisiera invitarte*
