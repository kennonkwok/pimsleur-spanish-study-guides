## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Simple future — *repasar*, *hablar*, *tomar* (review and extension)**
The simple future endings attach to the full infinitive. Lesson 4 heavily drills the *nosotros* future for planning meetings:

- *Repasaremos el progreso del proyecto.* — We'll review the progress of the project.
- *Hablaremos del progreso.* — We'll talk about the progress.
- *Tomará por lo menos seis meses.* — It will take at least six months.

**2. *ya* + preterite / *ya* + estar + past participle**
*Ya* (already) combined with a perfect state:

- *El problema del calendario ya está arreglado.* — The schedule problem is already settled.
- *Ya está arreglado.* — It's already settled.

**3. Preterite of *llamar*, *viajar*, *quedarse***
All regular -ar preterites:

- *llamar*: *llamé, llamaste, **llamó**, llamamos, llamasteis, llamaron*
- *viajar*: *viajé, viajaste, **viajó**, viajamos, viajasteis, viajaron*
- *quedarse*: *me quedé, te quedaste, **se quedó**, nos quedamos, os quedasteis, se quedaron*

Examples from lesson:
- *Llamó la señora González.* — Ms. González called.
- *Ricardo viajó a los Estados Unidos.* — Ricardo traveled to the United States.
- *Se quedó seis meses en Texas.* — He stayed six months in Texas.

**4. *por* for geographic movement — through / across**
*Por* (not *a*) is used when traveling through or across a place:

- *Viajó por Texas.* — He traveled through Texas.
- *Viajó solo por Texas.* — He traveled alone through Texas.

**5. *me gustaría* + infinitive — conditional "I would like"**
The conditional of *gustar* expresses a polite wish:

- *Me gustaría leerla traducida al inglés.* — I would like to read it translated into English.

**6. *tratar de* — to be about (a book/story)**
- *¿De qué trata el libro?* — What is the book about?
- *El relato trata de su viaje por Texas.* — The story is about his trip through Texas.

## Verb Conjugations

### repasar — to review / to go over
**Type:** regular -ar (simple future introduced)

Present:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **repaso** | nosotros: repasamos |
| 2nd | tú: repasas | vosotros: repasáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: repasa | ellos/ellas/Uds.: repasan |

Simple future:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: repasaré | nosotros: **repasaremos** |
| 2nd | tú: repasarás | vosotros: repasaréis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: repasará | ellos/ellas/Uds.: repasarán |

Lesson drills: *repasaremos el progreso*, *repasaremos unas facturas nuevas*, *repasaremos las facturas*.

## Summary

- **repasar** — regular -ar; *repasaremos* (future nosotros) is the key business-meeting phrase
- **llamar** — regular -ar; preterite *llamó* introduced for "she/he called"
- **quedarse** — regular -ar reflexive; preterite *se quedó* = "he stayed [somewhere for X time]"
- **regresar** — regular -ar; *regreso pronto* uses present for near-future "I'm returning soon"
