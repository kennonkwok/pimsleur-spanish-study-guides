## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Double object pronouns — *te lo*, *me lo***
When both indirect and direct object pronouns appear together, the indirect comes first. *Le/les* before *lo/la* becomes *se*:

- *Voy a decirtelo.* — I'm going to tell you it. (*te* + *lo* — attached to infinitive)
- *Dígamelo.* (Lesson 6 preview) — Tell me it.

At this level the lesson drills the infinitive-attached form primarily.

**2. Reporting speech and redirecting: *eso no voy a decirte***
Fronting the object (*eso*) for emphasis is a natural Spanish construction:

- *Eso no voy a decirte.* — That I'm not going to tell you.
- *No voy a decirlo.* — I'm not going to tell you (it).

**3. Preterite of *vivir*, *pasar*, *ocurrir***
All regular preterites:

- *vivir*: *viví, viviste, **vivió**, vivimos, vivisteis, vivieron*
- *pasar*: *pasé, pasaste, **pasó**, pasamos, pasasteis, pasaron*
- *ocurrir*: *ocurrí, ocurriste, **ocurrió**, ocurrimos, ocurristeis, ocurrieron*

Examples:
- *Y después vivió allí.* — And afterwards he lived there.
- *¿Qué pasó?* — What happened?
- *Ocurrió algo emocionante.* — Something exciting occurred.

**4. *algo* + adjective — something [adjective]**
*Algo* before an adjective creates "something [adj]":

- *Algo interesante ocurrió.* / *Ocurrió algo interesante.* — Something interesting occurred.
- *Algo emocionante.* — Something exciting.

**5. Review: *ir a* + infinitive with clitic pronouns**
Clitic pronouns can attach to the infinitive in *ir a* constructions:

- *Voy a decirtelo.* (= *Te lo voy a decir.*) — I'm going to tell it to you.
- *No voy a decirlo.* — I'm not going to tell you (it).

## Verb Conjugations

### ocurrir — to occur / to happen
**Type:** regular -ir

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: ocurro | nosotros: ocurrimos |
| 2nd | tú: ocurres | vosotros: ocurrís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **ocurre** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: ocurren |

Preterite (drilled form):

| yo | tú | él/ella | nosotros | vosotros | ellos |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ocurrí | ocurriste | **ocurrió** | ocurrimos | ocurristeis | ocurrieron |

Lesson drills: *ocurrió algo emocionante*, *ocurrió algo interesante*.

## Summary

- **ocurrir** — regular -ir; preterite *ocurrió* = "something occurred"; always used with *algo*
- **vivir** — regular -ir; both present (*vive en*, *vivo en*) and preterite (*vivió allí*) drilled
- **pasar** — regular -ar; *¿qué pasó?* = "what happened?" is a high-frequency phrase drilled repeatedly
