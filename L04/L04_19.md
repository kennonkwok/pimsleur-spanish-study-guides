## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *primer(o)* and ordinal numbers with floors**
*Primero* shortens to *primer* before a masculine singular noun. Used with *piso* (floor/story) to indicate location in a building.

- *el primer piso* — the first floor (= 2nd floor in US counting)
- *el segundo piso* — the second floor
- Note: In Mexico and much of Latin America, *la planta baja* = ground floor; *primer piso* = one flight up.

**2. *a la vuelta de la esquina* — around the corner**
A fixed idiom. *Vuelta* = turn; *esquina* = (street) corner.

- *A la vuelta de la esquina.* — Around the corner.
- *Los ascensores están a la vuelta de la esquina.* — The elevators are around the corner.

**3. *se sirve* / *se sirven* — passive voice with *se***
Used to express "is served / are served" without specifying a subject. The verb agrees with what is being served.

- *El desayuno se sirve de las 6 a las 11.* — Breakfast is served from 6 to 11.
- Pattern: *se* + 3rd person verb

**4. *me llevo* — "I'm taking this one"**
*Llevarse* = to take something for oneself / to take away. Common in shopping contexts.

- *Me llevo esta.* — I'm taking this one (f). (Referring to *la maleta*)
- *Me llevo este.* — I'm taking this one (m).

**5. *tuve que* + infinitive — preterite obligation**
*Tener que* in the preterite (*tuve que*) = "I had to." Lesson introduces this as a key new form.

- *Hoy tuve que comprar una maleta.* — Today I had to buy a suitcase.
- *Tuve que llamar a mi madre.* — I had to call my mother.
- *Tuve que comprarla.* — I had to buy it. (clitic appended to infinitive)

**6. Adjective used as pronoun (color substitution)**
When the noun is understood from context, adjectives stand alone as pronouns, agreeing in gender with the dropped noun.

- *la maleta negra → la negra* — the black one (f)
- *el paraguas verde → el verde* — the green one (m)
- *No, la negra.* / *No, mi paraguas es el negro.*

### tener — to have (preterite focus)
**Type:** irregular (*go*-verb in present yo; preterite is fully irregular)

| | Singular | Plural |

| 2nd | tú: tienes | vosotros: tenéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: tiene | ellos/ellas/Uds.: tienen |

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **tuve** | nosotros: tuvimos |
| 2nd | tú: tuviste | vosotros: tuvisteis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: tuvo | ellos/ellas/Uds.: tuvieron |

Lesson drills: *tuve que comprar una maleta*, *tuve que llamar a mi madre*, *tuve que comprarla*.

## Summary

- **alquilar** — regular -ar; used in *ir a* + infinitive construction for renting a car
- **revisar** — regular -ar; key phrase for checking emails (*mensajes electrónicos*)
- **caminar** — regular -ar; used in *vamos a caminar* (let's go for a walk)
- **llevar** — regular -ar; reflexive *me llevo* = "I'm taking this one" (key shopping phrase)
- **reservar** — regular -ar; preterite *reservé* drilled for hotel reservation
- **tener** — irregular; preterite *tuve que* + infinitive is the major new grammar point (= "I had to")
