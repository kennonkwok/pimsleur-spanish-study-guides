## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *probarse* — reflexive + double object pronoun construction**
*Probar* (o→ue) = to try/taste. Reflexive *probarse* = to try on. When a direct object pronoun is also needed, both clitics attach to the infinitive: *probar + me + lo = probármelo*.

- *¿Puedo probármelo?* — Can I try it on? (referring to a masculine noun, *el traje*)
- *Quiero probármelo.* — I want to try it on.
- The accent mark (*probármelo*) falls on the stressed syllable of the original infinitive.

**2. *traer* — irregular preterite (homophone of *traje*)**
*Traer* (to bring) is irregular. Its preterite 1st person singular (*traje* = I brought) sounds identical to *el traje* (the suit). Context distinguishes them.

| Preterite of *traer* | |
|---|---|
| yo: traje | nosotros: trajimos |
| tú: trajiste | vosotros: trajisteis |
| él/ella/Ud.: trajo | ellos/ellas/Uds.: trajeron |

- *No traje un traje de baño.* — I didn't bring a bathing suit. (*traje* = I brought; *traje* = suit)
- *No traje.* — I didn't bring one.

**3. Clothing sizes: *talla* + size adjective**
*La talla* = clothing size. Add the size directly after *talla*:

- *talla mediana* — size medium
- *talla grande* — size large
- *talla pequeña* — size small

**4. *tener tiempo para* — to have time to**
*Tiempo* = time (available). Use *tener tiempo para* + infinitive for "have time to do something."

- *Hoy tuve tiempo para ir a la playa.* — Today I had time to go to the beach.
- *¿Tiene tiempo para ir a la playa?* — Do you have time to go to the beach?

**5. *hace* for weather expressions**
*Hacer* (irreg.) describes weather conditions. The verb is always 3rd person singular.

- *Hace mucho calor.* — It's very hot. (Lit. "It makes a lot of heat.")
- *Hace mucho calor y está muy húmedo.* — It's very hot and very humid.

## Verb Conjugations

### probarse — to try on
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue), reflexive; conjugate like *probar* with reflexive pronoun

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **me pruebo** | nosotros: nos probamos |
| 2nd | tú: **te pruebas** | vosotros: os probáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **se prueba** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **se prueban** |

Lesson drills: *¿puedo probármelo?* (*poder* + infinitive with double clitic); *quiero probármelo*.

---

### traer — to bring
**Type:** irregular (-go in yo present; preterite stem *traj-*)

**Present tense:**

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **traigo** | nosotros: traemos |
| 2nd | tú: traes | vosotros: traéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: trae | ellos/ellas/Uds.: traen |

**Preterite tense** (drilled in lesson):

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **traje** | nosotros: trajimos |
| 2nd | tú: trajiste | vosotros: trajisteis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: trajo | ellos/ellas/Uds.: trajeron |

Lesson drills: *no traje un traje de baño*, *no traje* (preterite: I didn't bring one).

## Summary

- **probarse** — stem-changing (o→ue), reflexive; key phrase *¿puedo probármelo?* (double clitic on infinitive)
- **traer** — irregular (*traigo* present yo; preterite stem *traj-*); *no traje* = "I didn't bring one" — homophones with *el traje* (suit) are a key lesson point
