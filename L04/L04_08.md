## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *me lo llevo* — double object pronoun construction**
When both a direct and indirect object pronoun appear together, the indirect comes first: IO + DO + verb. *Me lo llevo* = I take it (for myself / with me).

- *Me lo llevo.* — I'm taking it.
- *Solo me llevo los cuentos cortos.* — I'm only taking the short stories.
- *Me llevo el libro de cuentos cortos y el libro de Núñez.* — I'm taking the book of short stories and Núñez's book.

When the IO pronoun is 3rd person (*le/les*) and precedes another 3rd-person DO pronoun (*lo/la/los/las*), *le/les* changes to *se*:
- *Se lo llevo.* — I'll take it to him/her.

**2. *buscar* vs. *mirar* in a store**
Both involve looking, but they signal different intent:
| | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *buscar* | to look for (intending to buy/find) | *Busco un libro de cuentos cortos.* |
| *mirar* | to look at / browse (not necessarily buying) | *Solo estoy mirando.* |

**3. *¿Puede recomendarme...?* — indirect object clitic with infinitive**
The indirect object pronoun attaches to the infinitive when following a conjugated verb like *poder*.

- *¿Puede recomendarme un libro?* — Can you recommend a book to me?
- *¿Puede recomendarme uno?* — Can you recommend one to me?

**4. *llevar* vs. *traer***
Both mean "to take/bring" but from different perspectives:
- *llevar* = to take (away from speaker, or bring along with you)
- *traer* = to bring (toward speaker)

In the bookstore context: *Me lo llevo* = I'll take it (home with me).

## Verb Conjugations

### recomendar — to recommend
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie) — stem changes in all forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **recomiendo** | nosotros: recomendamos |
| 2nd | tú: **recomiendas** | vosotros: recomendáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **recomienda** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **recomiendan** |

Lesson drills: *¿Puede recomendarme un libro de cuentos cortos?*, *¿Puede recomendarme uno?*

## Summary

- **recomendar** — stem-changing (e→ie); used in the pattern *¿Puede recomendarme...?*
- **buscar** — regular -ar; "to look for" with intent to find/buy (contrast with *mirar*)
- **llevar** — regular -ar; key phrase *me lo llevo* (double object pronoun: I'm taking it)
- **mirar** — regular -ar; used as progressive *estoy mirando* to mean "just browsing"
