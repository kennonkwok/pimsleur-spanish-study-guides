## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *quedar* for clothing fit**
*Quedar* has two distinct uses in this series: fixed location (Lesson 19/20) and now clothing fit. In the fit sense, it works like *gustar* — the clothing is the subject.

- *La talla grande no me queda.* — The size large doesn't fit me. (Lit. "The size large doesn't remain on me.")
- *Me queda bien.* — It fits me well.
- *Me quedan bien.* — They fit me well. (plural subject → *quedan*)
- *Ambos me quedan bien.* — Both fit me well.

**2. *quisiera que* + imperfect subjunctive**
*Quisiera* (wish) + *que* introduces a subordinate clause using the imperfect subjunctive to express wishes about situations contrary to reality.

- *Quisiera que no lloviera.* — I wish it wouldn't rain. (*lloviera* = imperfect subjunctive of *llover*)
- *Quisiera que estuviera aquí.* — I wish she were here. (*estuviera* = imperfect subjunctive of *estar*)
- *Quisiera que mi hermana estuviera aquí.* — I wish my sister were here.

**3. *me gustaría* — conditional of *gustar***
*Me gustaría* = "I would like" (stronger politeness than *me gusta*). The conditional expresses hypothetical preferences.

- *Me gustaría ir a la playa.* — I would like to go to the beach.
- *Me gustaría ir a la piscina hoy.* — I would like to go to the pool today.

**4. *estar lloviendo* — present progressive of weather**
*Llover* used in present progressive describes ongoing rain.

- *Pero está lloviendo.* — But it's raining.
- *¡Está lloviendo otra vez!* — It's raining again!

**5. Ordinal *tercer* (shortened form)**
*Tercero* shortens to *tercer* before masculine singular nouns (same rule as *primero → primer*).

- *el tercer piso* — the third floor
- *el tercer día* — the third day

## Verb Conjugations

### llover — to rain
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue); impersonal — only used in 3rd sg.

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 3rd | **llueve** | (not used) |

**Present progressive:** *está lloviendo*

**Imperfect subjunctive** (new in this lesson):

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 3rd | **lloviera** | llovieran |

Lesson drills: *está lloviendo*, *quisiera que no lloviera*.

---

### quedar — to fit (clothing) / to be located
**Type:** regular -ar (used like *gustar* — reverse construction for fit)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: quedo | nosotros: quedamos |
| 2nd | tú: quedas | vosotros: quedáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **queda** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **quedan** |

Lesson drills (fit sense): *la talla grande no me queda*, *me queda bien*, *me quedan bien*, *ambos me quedan bien*.

---

### visitar — to visit
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: visito | nosotros: visitamos |
| 2nd | tú: visitas | vosotros: visitáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: visita | ellos/ellas/Uds.: visitan |

Lesson drills: *vendrá a visitarme* (infinitive with clitic after future of *venir*).

## Summary

- **llover** — stem-changing (o→ue), impersonal; present progressive *está lloviendo*; imperfect subjunctive *lloviera* in *quisiera que no lloviera*
- **quedar** — regular -ar; new use = clothing fit (reverse construction like *gustar*): *me queda bien* / *me quedan bien*
- **visitar** — regular -ar; used in *vendrá a visitarme* (future + infinitive with attached clitic)
