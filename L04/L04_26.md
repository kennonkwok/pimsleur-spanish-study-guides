## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *de veras* — "really?" (alternative to *¿verdad?*)**
This lesson introduces *de veras* as a colloquial reaction expressing surprise or seeking confirmation.

- *¿De veras? No lo sabía.* — Really? I didn't know that.
- *¿De veras?* can be used interchangeably with *¿verdad?* or *¿en serio?* in conversation.

**2. Imperfect of *saber* — "I knew / didn't know"**
*Sabía* (imperfect, yo/él/ella form) means "I knew" or "you knew" (Ud.). Used for ongoing or background knowledge in the past.

- *No lo sabía.* — I didn't know that (lit. "not it I knew").
- *Sabía que...* — I knew that... / Did you know that...
- The lesson drills this phrase extensively as a fixed reaction to surprising news.

**3. Future tense of *poder*: *podrán***
The future tense indicates what will happen. *Podrán* = "they will be able to / you all (Uds.) will be able to."

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | podré | podremos |
| 2nd | podrás | podréis |
| 3rd | **podrá** | **podrán** |

- *Podrán hablar de muchas cosas.* — You'll be able to talk about many things. (Uds.)
- The future of *poder* is formed on the irregular stem *podr-*.

**4. *hacer* + noun for activities**
Several key activity phrases use *hacer*:

- *hacer mandados* — to run errands
- *hacer unos mandados* — to do some errands
- *hacer copias* — to make copies (appears in Lesson 27)

**5. *ir a* + infinitive with appended clitics**
Object pronouns can attach to the infinitive in the *ir a* + infinitive construction:

- *Voy a verla la semana entrante.* — I'm going to see her next week. (*la* = her, appended)
- *Él va a ayudarme.* — He's going to help me. (*me* appended)

**6. *busco* + direct object — looking for (without *para*)**
*Buscar* does not take a preposition in Spanish; unlike English "look for."

- *Busco empleo.* — I'm looking for work.
- *Busco un regalo.* — I'm looking for a gift.

## Verb Conjugations

### saber — to know (a fact)
**Type:** irregular; yo present = *sé*; imperfect regular on stem *sab-*

Present tense:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **sé** | nosotros: sabemos |
| 2nd | tú: sabes | vosotros: sabéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: sabe | ellos/ellas/Uds.: saben |

Imperfect (drilled): *sabía* (yo/él/ella/Ud.) — I/you/he/she knew
Lesson drills: *No lo sabía.*, *Sabía que...*

---

### poder — to be able to / can
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue) in present; irregular future stem *podr-*

Present tense (review):

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **puedo** | nosotros: podemos |
| 2nd | tú: **puedes** | vosotros: podéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **puede** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **pueden** |

Future tense:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: podré | nosotros: podremos |
| 2nd | tú: podrás | vosotros: podréis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: podrá | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **podrán** |

Lesson drills: *podrán hablar de muchas cosas* (future, Uds./3rd pl.)

## Summary

- **saber** — irregular yo (*sé*); imperfect *sabía* = "I/you knew" — used heavily as a reaction to news (*No lo sabía.*)
- **poder** — stem-changing (o→ue) in present; future stem *podr-*; lesson drills *podrán* (future, 3rd pl./Uds.)
- **hablar** — regular -ar; *hablamos* used in preterite sense ("we talked a long time")
- **necesitar** — regular -ar; straightforward need expression
