## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *quedarse* — to stay (at a place)**
*Quedarse* is reflexive and means to stay, remain, or be lodged somewhere. Distinct from *quedar* (to be located) and *quedarse con* (to keep something).

- *¿Dónde se está quedando?* — Where are you staying?
- *Estoy en el Hotel Río Negro.* — I'm (staying) at the Hotel Río Negro.
- The progressive *se está quedando* emphasizes the ongoing stay.

**2. Future tense: *vendrá* (irregular stem *vendr-*)**
The future tense of *venir* uses the irregular stem *vendr-*.

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | vendré | vendremos |
| 2nd | vendrás | vendréis |
| 3rd | **vendrá** | vendrán |

- *Alguien vendrá a ayudarle con su equipaje.* — Someone will come to help you with your luggage.

**3. Ordinal numbers in hotel context**
*Cuarto* = fourth (ordinal adjective). Ordinals in Spanish agree in gender with the noun.

- *En el cuarto piso.* — On the fourth floor.
- Review: *primer(o)*, *segundo*, *tercer(o)*, *cuarto*, *quinto*...

**4. *ir de compras* — to go shopping**
This is a fixed phrase meaning "to go shopping." Uses *de*, not *a comprar*.

- *Voy a ir de compras.* — I'm going to go shopping.
- *Hoy fui de compras.* — Today I went shopping.
- *Así que fui de compras.* — So I went shopping.

**5. Preterite of *ir* → *fui***
*Fui* is the preterite first-person singular of both *ir* and *ser* (they share identical preterite conjugations). Context resolves ambiguity.

- *Hoy fui de compras.* — Today I went shopping. (*ir*)
- *Así que fui de compras.* — So I went shopping. (*ir*)

**6. *sirvase esperar* — polite imperative**
From *servirse* + infinitive. A formal and polite way to say "please wait." Seen on signs and in formal service contexts.

- *Sírvase esperar unos minutos.* — Please wait a few minutes.

**7. *por* + noun = "because of" / "for the purpose of"**
- *Estoy aquí por un congreso.* — I'm here for a conference. (purpose/reason)
- Compare *para* (intended recipient or goal): *un regalo para mi nieta* = a gift for my granddaughter.

## Verb Conjugations

### reservar — to reserve / to book
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **reservo** | nosotros: reservamos |
| 2nd | tú: reservas | vosotros: reserváis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: reserva | ellos/ellas/Uds.: reservan |

Lesson drills: *Reservé una habitación para cinco días.* (preterite yo — *reservé*)

---

### mostrar — to show
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue) in present; stem changes in all forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **muestro** | nosotros: mostramos |
| 2nd | tú: **muestras** | vosotros: mostráis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **muestra** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **muestran** |

Lesson drills: *Me gustaría mostrarle el acuario.* (infinitive with clitic)

---

### quedarse — to stay (at a place)
**Type:** regular -ar, reflexive

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me quedo | nosotros: nos quedamos |
| 2nd | tú: te quedas | vosotros: os quedáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **se queda** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se quedan |

Lesson drills: *¿Dónde se está quedando?* (Ud. — progressive form: *se está quedando*)

---

### venir — to come
**Type:** irregular; go-verb in yo (*vengo*); e→ie in present (except nosotros/vosotros); irregular future stem *vendr-*

Present tense:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **vengo** | nosotros: venimos |
| 2nd | tú: **vienes** | vosotros: venís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **viene** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **vienen** |

Future tense:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: vendré | nosotros: vendremos |
| 2nd | tú: vendrás | vosotros: vendréis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **vendrá** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: vendrán |

Lesson drills: *Alguien vendrá a ayudarle.* (future, 3rd sg.)

## Summary

- **reservar** — regular -ar; preterite *reservé* = I reserved/booked; key hotel check-in verb
- **mostrar** — stem-changing (o→ue); used with clitics: *mostrarle* = to show him/her/you
- **quedarse** — regular -ar reflexive; *¿dónde se está quedando?* = where are you staying; progressive form emphasizes ongoing state
- **venir** — irregular (*vengo*, e→ie); future *vendrá* = will come; irregular future stem *vendr-*
