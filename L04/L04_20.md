## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *estar* + gerund (present progressive) for machine states**
*Estar funcionando* = "is working/functioning." This construction emphasizes an ongoing state, especially for machines and devices.

- *No está funcionando.* — It's not working.
- *El ascensor no está funcionando hoy.* — The elevator isn't working today.
- *¿Todavía no está funcionando el ascensor?* — Is the elevator still not working?

**2. Possessive pronouns: *mío/mía*, *suyo/suya***
These stand alone (post-nominal) and agree in gender with the object possessed, not the owner.

| Object is masculine | Object is feminine |
|---|---|
| *No es mío.* — It's not mine. | *No es mía.* — It's not mine. (e.g., *la maleta*) |
| *Es suyo.* — It's yours/his/hers. | *Es suya.* — It's yours/his/hers. |

- *Ese paraguas es suyo, el verde.* — That umbrella is yours, the green one.
- *No, no es mío.* — No, it's not mine.
- *Sí, la negra es mía.* — Yes, the black one is mine.
- *Sí, es mía. Gracias.* — Yes, it's mine. Thank you.

**3. *la planta baja* vs. *el primer piso***
Floor vocabulary in Mexican/Latin American Spanish:

| Term | Meaning | US equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| *la planta baja* | ground floor | 1st floor |
| *el primer piso* | first floor | 2nd floor |
| *el segundo piso* | second floor | 3rd floor |

**4. Color *café* (invariable adjective)**
*Café* = brown (any shade). Unlike most adjectives, it does not change form for gender or number.

- *Me llevo esta café.* — I'm taking this brown one.
- *Me gusta el color café.* — I like the color brown.
- *Una bolsa café.* / *Bolsas café.* (same form)

**5. *que la pase bien* — subjunctive wish**
A fixed farewell expression. *Pasar bien* = to have a good time. The subjunctive expresses a wish.

- *Que la pase bien aquí en la ciudad de México.* — Enjoy your stay here in Mexico City.

## Verb Conjugations

### funcionar — to work / to function
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: funciono | nosotros: funcionamos |
| 2nd | tú: funcionas | vosotros: funcionáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: funciona | ellos/ellas/Uds.: funcionan |

Lesson drills: *no está funcionando* (present progressive), *no está funcionando hoy*.

---

### encontrar — to find
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue) — stem changes in all forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **encuentro** | nosotros: encontramos |
| 2nd | tú: **encuentras** | vosotros: encontráis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **encuentra** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **encuentran** |

Lesson drills: *¿dónde encuentro los anteojos de sol?*, *¿dónde encuentro bolsas de piel?*

---

## Summary

- **funcionar** — regular -ar; key in present progressive *no está funcionando* (elevator not working)
- **encontrar** — stem-changing (o→ue); *¿dónde encuentro…?* is the core department store navigation phrase
- **usar** — regular -ar; drilled in both present (*tengo que usar*) and preterite (*tuve que usar*) obligation
- **buscar** — regular -ar; shopping phrase *busco una bolsa de piel* (I'm looking for a leather handbag)
