## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *Hace* + time + preterite = "ago"**
Use *hace* + time expression before or after a preterite verb to say how long ago something happened.

- *Se mudó allí hace tres meses.* — She moved there three months ago.
- *Se mudó allí hace dos meses.* — He moved there two months ago.
- *Se mudó allí hace seis meses.* — She moved there six months ago.

Note: *hace* does not change form regardless of the time period.

**2. *¿A qué se dedica?* — asking what someone does for a living**
*Dedicarse a* (reflexive) = to be devoted to / to do for a living. This is a natural, common way to ask about someone's profession.

- *¿A qué se dedica ella?* — What does she do (for work)?
- *Es periodista.* — She's a journalist. (No article after *ser* + profession)

**3. *hubo* — preterite of *haber* for events**
*Hubo* is the preterite of *haber* used impersonally, meaning "there was" (a specific event). Contrast with *había* (imperfect) = "there used to be / there was (ongoing)."

- *Hubo un problema en el metro.* — There was a problem in the subway. (specific event)
- *Pues hubo un problema.* — Well, there was a problem.

**4. *tener lugar* — "to take place"**
A fixed phrase; *lugar* does not change. The subject of the sentence is the event or work being described.

- *Su novela tiene lugar en un pueblo.* — His novel takes place in a town.
- *Su novela también tiene lugar allí.* — His novel also takes place there.

**5. Relative clause with *que* + *estar parado***
*El/la [noun] que está parado/a* is the standard pattern for describing someone by their physical position.

- *El caballero que está parado allí.* — The gentleman who is standing over there.
- *La señora que está parada al lado de la mesa.* — The lady who is standing next to the table.
- Note gender agreement: *parado* (m) / *parada* (f).

**6. Progressive tense with irregular gerund: *leer → leyendo***
When a stem ends in a vowel, the -iendo suffix becomes -yendo.

- *Estoy leyendo una de sus novelas.* — I'm reading one of his novels.
- *Lo estoy leyendo ahora.* — I'm reading it now.

| Pattern | Example |

## Verb Conjugations

### mudarse — to move (residence)
**Type:** regular -ar (reflexive)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me mudo | nosotros: nos mudamos |
| 2nd | tú: te mudas | vosotros: os mudáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **se muda** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se mudan |

Lesson drills: *se mudó* (preterite) — *Se mudó allí hace tres meses.*

### encontrar — to find
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue) — stem changes in all forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **encuentro** | nosotros: encontramos |
| 2nd | tú: **encuentras** | vosotros: encontráis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **encuentra** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **encuentran** |

Lesson drills: *no puedo encontrarla*; preterite forms *encontró* (he/she found), *encontré* (I found).

## Summary

- **mudarse** — regular -ar reflexive; key phrase: *se mudó allí hace [time]* = moved there [time] ago
- **encontrar** — stem-changing (o→ue); preterite *encontró* / *encontré* drilled heavily
- **ocurrir** — regular -ir; syn. with *pasar*; preterite *ocurrió* used in lesson
- **leer** — regular -er; irregular gerund *leyendo* (vowel stem → -yendo); used in progressive
- **repasar** — regular -ar; future *repasaremos* introduced
