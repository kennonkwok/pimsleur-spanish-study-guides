## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *sentirse* — reflexive verb for expressing how one feels**
*Sentirse* (e→ie) is the reflexive form of *sentir*. It is used to express how a person feels physically or emotionally.

- *¿Cómo te sientes?* — How are you feeling? (tú form)
- *Me siento muy bien, gracias.* — I'm feeling very well, thank you.
- Contrast with *¿Cómo estás?* (general state) — *sentirse* focuses on a current physical/emotional feeling.

**2. Preterite of *conocer* — met (for the first time)**
*Conocer* in the preterite means "to meet" (to make someone's acquaintance for the first time), not "to know."

- *¿Conociste a su novio?* — Did you meet her boyfriend?
- *Lo conocí en el almuerzo.* — I met him at the lunch.
- *La conocí el mes pasado.* — I met her last month.
- Note: *a* is required before direct-object people (*conociste a su novio*).

**3. Preterite of *almorzar* — had lunch**
*Almorzar* is stem-changing (o→ue) in the present. In the preterite, the yo form has a spelling change (-c- → -c-): *almorcé* (not almorzé) to preserve the [s] sound.

- *Almorcé con mi prima María.* — I had lunch with my cousin María.
- *Almorzamos juntos ayer.* — We had lunch together yesterday.
- *Almorzamos* is identical in present and preterite for nosotros — context determines tense.

**4. *hace* + time period = "ago"**
To express how long ago something happened: *hace* + time + preterite verb.

- *Lo conocí hace dos semanas.* — I met him two weeks ago.
- *La conocí el mes pasado.* — I met her last month. (using *pasado* construction)

**5. *estar seguro/a de que* + clause**
"To be sure that..." uses *estar* (not *ser*) because it expresses a state.

- *Estoy segura de que ambos están muy ocupados en el trabajo.* — I'm sure that both are very busy at work.

**6. *salúdame a* — "greet [someone] for me"**
This is an imperative (*tú* form of *saludar*) with two pronouns: *me* (for me) + *a* + the person.

- *Salúdame a tu prima.* — Greet your cousin for me.
- *Salúdame a tu prima María.* — Greet your cousin María for me.

## Verb Conjugations

### sentirse — to feel (oneself)
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie), reflexive; forms below are present tense with reflexive pronouns

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me **siento** | nosotros: nos sentimos |
| 2nd | tú: te **sientes** | vosotros: os sentís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: se **siente** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se **sienten** |

Lesson drills: *¿Cómo te sientes?*, *Me siento muy bien.*

---

### almorzar — to have lunch
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue) in present; spelling change in preterite yo (*almorcé*)

Present tense:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **almuerzo** | nosotros: almorzamos |
| 2nd | tú: **almuerzas** | vosotros: almorzáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **almuerza** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **almuerzan** |

Preterite forms drilled: *almorcé* (yo), *almorzamos* (nosotros)

---

### conocer — to know / to meet (a person)
**Type:** irregular yo (*conozco*); otherwise regular -er in present; regular preterite

Present tense:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **conozco** | nosotros: conocemos |
| 2nd | tú: conoces | vosotros: conocéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: conoce | ellos/ellas/Uds.: conocen |

Preterite forms drilled: *conocí* (yo — I met), *conociste* (tú — you met)

---

### lucir — to look / to appear
**Type:** irregular yo (*luzco*); otherwise regular -ir in present

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **luzco** | nosotros: lucimos |
| 2nd | tú: **luces** | vosotros: lucís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: luce | ellos/ellas/Uds.: lucen |

Lesson drills: *Luces bien.* (tú form drilled only)

## Summary

- **sentirse** — stem-changing (e→ie), reflexive; key phrase for asking/saying how one feels
- **almorzar** — stem-changing (o→ue) in present; yo preterite *almorcé* (spelling change); *almorzamos* used for both present and preterite nosotros
- **conocer** — irregular yo (*conozco*); preterite means "met for the first time": *conocí*, *conociste*
- **lucir** — irregular yo (*luzco*); used for physical appearance: *luces bien* = you look well
