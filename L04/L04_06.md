## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Present perfect of *escribir* and *ver***
*Escribir* and *ver* have irregular past participles:

- *escribir* → **escrito**
- *ver* → **visto**

Examples:
- *Ernesto Muñoz ha escrito un buen relato.* — Ernesto Muñoz has written a good story.
- *¿Qué ha escrito él?* — What has he written?
- *No he visto traducción del libro.* — I haven't seen a translation of the book.

**2. Double object pronoun with formal imperative: *dígamelo***
*Dígame* (tell me — formal imperative) + *lo* (it) fuse into *dígamelo*:

- *Dígamelo.* — Tell it to me.
- Compare: *Dígame qué pasó.* — Tell me what happened. (no *lo* because the object is a clause)

The rule: indirect pronoun (*me*) + direct pronoun (*lo/la/los/las*) attach after the verb in imperative constructions.

**3. Preterite of *mudarse*, *encontrar*, *comprar***
All regular -ar verbs in the preterite:

- *mudarse*: *me mudé, te mudaste, **se mudó**, nos mudamos, os mudasteis, se mudaron*
- *encontrar*: ***encontré**, encontraste, **encontró**, encontramos, encontrasteis, encontraron* (note: preterite is regular even though present is stem-changing)
- *comprar*: *compré, compraste, **compró**, compramos, comprasteis, compraron*

Key point: **stem-changing verbs do not change in the preterite** (for -ar/-er verbs). *Encontré*, not *†encuentré*.

**4. *algo más* — anything else / something more**
*Algo más* functions as "anything else" in questions and "something more" in statements:

- *¿Ha escrito algo más?* — Has he written anything else?
- *Sí, ha escrito otros libros.* — Yes, he has written other books.

**5. *todavía* / *todavía no* — still / not yet**
- *Todavía hay un problema.* — There is still a problem.
- *Todavía no.* — Not yet.
- *No está arreglado todavía.* — It's not settled yet.

## Verb Conjugations

### mudarse — to move (residence)
**Type:** regular -ar (reflexive)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me mudo | nosotros: nos mudamos |
| 2nd | tú: te mudas | vosotros: os mudáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **se muda** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se mudan |

Preterite:

| yo | tú | él/ella | nosotros | vosotros | ellos |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| me mudé | te mudaste | **se mudó** | nos mudamos | os mudasteis | se mudaron |

Lesson drills: *mi hijo se mudó a Texas*, *se mudó a Houston*, *se mudó a un apartamento en Melbourne*, *mi hija se mudó a México*.

---

### encontrar — to find
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue) in present; **regular** in preterite

Present:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **encuentro** | nosotros: encontramos |
| 2nd | tú: **encuentras** | vosotros: encontráis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **encuentra** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **encuentran** |

Preterite (regular — no stem change):

| yo | tú | él/ella | nosotros | vosotros | ellos |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **encontré** | encontraste | **encontró** | encontramos | encontrasteis | encontraron |

Lesson drills: *encontró empleo*, *encontró un apartamento*, *encontré dos facturas viejas*, *encontró un buen empleo en contabilidad*.

---

### escribir — to write (present perfect focus)
**Type:** regular -ir; irregular past participle: *escrito*

Present perfect with *haber*:

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **he escrito** | nosotros: hemos escrito |
| 2nd | tú: has escrito | vosotros: habéis escrito |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **ha escrito** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: han escrito |

Lesson drills: *Ernesto Muñoz ha escrito un buen relato*, *ha escrito otros libros*, *¿ha escrito algo más?*

Note: *escribir* was introduced in L04_01 (present tense). This lesson introduces the present perfect with irregular past participle *escrito*.

---

## Summary

- **mudarse** — regular -ar reflexive; preterite *se mudó* = "he/she moved to [place]"
- **encontrar** — o→ue stem-changing in present only; preterite is fully regular (*encontré*, *encontró*)
- **escribir** — regular -ir (present drilled in L04_01); this lesson adds present perfect: *ha escrito* / *he escrito* using irregular past participle *escrito*
