## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Comparatives and superlatives with *más***
Spanish comparatives use *más* + adjective (+ *que* for "than"). Superlatives add the definite article:

| Degree | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Positive | bonito/a | *Estas estampillas son bonitas.* |
| Comparative | más bonito/a | *Estas son más bonitas.* — These are prettier. |
| Superlative | el/la más bonito/a de (all) | *Son las más bonitas de todas.* — These are the prettiest of all. |

Note: The article agrees with the noun in gender and number.

**2. *Un agua mineral* — feminine nouns starting with stressed *a-***
*Agua* is feminine, but takes *un* (not *una*) when immediately preceding the noun, to avoid the clash of two *a* sounds:

- *un agua mineral* — a mineral water (NOT *una agua*)
- *el agua* — the water (article used for same reason)
- *las aguas* — the waters (plural uses feminine article normally)

**3. *Me llevo* — taking something away**
*Llevarse* (reflexive *llevar*) means "to take (for oneself)" in a shopping context:

- *Me llevo estas estampillas.* — I'm taking these stamps.
- *Me llevo la bufanda azul.* — I'm taking the blue scarf.

**4. *Tener hambre / sed* — to be hungry / thirsty**
Both use *tener* + noun, not *estar*:

- *Tengo hambre.* — I'm hungry. (Lit. I have hunger.)
- *Tengo sed.* — I'm thirsty. (Lit. I have thirst.)
- *No tengo mucha sed.* — I'm not very thirsty.

**5. *Todavía* — still**
*Todavía* places emphasis on something ongoing:

- *Todavía tengo sed.* — I'm still thirsty.
- Opposite: *ya* (already / no longer)

## Verb Conjugations

### pedir — to order / ask for
**Type:** stem-changing (e→i) — changes in all forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **pido** | nosotros: pedimos |
| 2nd | tú: **pides** | vosotros: pedís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **pide** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **piden** |

Lesson drills: *voy a pedir enchiladas*, *voy a pedir otra agua mineral*

---

### acordarse — to remember
**Type:** reflexive; stem-changing (o→ue, except nosotros/vosotros)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **me acuerdo** | nosotros: nos acordamos |
| 2nd | tú: **te acuerdas** | vosotros: os acordáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **se acuerda** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **se acuerdan** |

Lesson drills: *no me acuerdo*

---

### llevar — to take / carry / wear
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **llevo** | nosotros: llevamos |
| 2nd | tú: llevas | vosotros: lleváis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: lleva | ellos/ellas/Uds.: llevan |

Lesson drills: *me llevo estas estampillas*, *me llevo la bufanda azul* (reflexive use: taking for oneself)

## Summary

- **pedir** — stem-changing (e→i); used for ordering food/items
- **acordarse** — reflexive, stem-changing (o→ue); *no me acuerdo* = I don't remember
- **llevar** — regular -ar; reflexive *llevarse* = to take (for oneself) in shopping contexts
- Key grammar: *más* + adjective for comparatives; *el/la más* + adjective + *de* for superlatives; *un agua* (not *una*) before stressed *a*
