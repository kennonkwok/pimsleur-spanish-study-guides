## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Future tense of *tener*: *tendrá que* — "he/she will have to"**
*Tener* has an irregular future stem: *tendr-*. Add regular future endings: *-é, -ás, -á, -emos, -éis, -án*.

| Person | Form |
|---|---|
| yo | tendré |
| tú | tendrás |
| él/ella/Ud. | **tendrá** |
| nosotros | tendremos |
| vosotros | tendréis |
| ellos/Uds. | tendrán |

- *Él tendrá que tomar un examen.* — He will have to take an exam.
- *No tendrá que tomar un examen oral.* — He won't have to take an oral exam.
- *Tendrá que tomarlo pronto.* (L4_10 preview) — He'll have to take it soon.

**2. *explicarle a él/ella* — clarifying indirect object with *a + pronoun***
Spanish often adds *a + prepositional pronoun* after an indirect object clitic to remove ambiguity (since *le* can mean "to him," "to her," or "to you").

- *¿Puede explicarle a él cómo prepararse?* — Can you explain to him how to prepare himself?
- The *a él* is not redundant — it specifies that *le* = to him, not to her or to you.

**3. *cómo prepararse* — infinitive after interrogative**
After verbs of explaining/knowing, *cómo* + infinitive describes the manner of doing something.

- *Puede explicarle cómo prepararse para el examen.* — You can explain to him how to prepare himself for the exam.
- This is an embedded indirect question; the infinitive replaces a conjugated clause.

**4. *saber* yo form: *sé***
*Saber* (to know facts/information) has an irregular yo form: *sé*. All other present forms are regular.

- *Sé que Susan Dart también escribe libros de no ficción.* — I know that Susan Dart also writes nonfiction.
- *No sé.* — I don't know.

**5. *nuevo/a en* — new to a place**
*Nuevo en* + noun = new to [a place or situation]. Not the same as "brand new."

- *Es nuevo en la escuela.* — He's new at the school.

| Pattern | Example |

## Verb Conjugations

### salir — to go out / come out
**Type:** irregular (*go*-verb in yo)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **salgo** | nosotros: salimos |
| 2nd | tú: sales | vosotros: salís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: sale | ellos/ellas/Uds.: salen |

Lesson drills: *salió* (preterite: it came out) — *La novela nueva salió hace un mes.*

---

### explicar — to explain
**Type:** regular -ar (spelling change c→qu in preterite before *e*, but present is regular)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **explico** | nosotros: explicamos |
| 2nd | tú: explicas | vosotros: explicáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: explica | ellos/ellas/Uds.: explican |

Lesson drills: *¿Puede explicarle a él cómo prepararse?*, *Voy a explicarle a él.*

---

### prepararse — to prepare oneself
**Type:** regular -ar (reflexive)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: me preparo | nosotros: nos preparamos |
| 2nd | tú: te preparas | vosotros: os preparáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **se prepara** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: se preparan |

Lesson drills: *cómo prepararse para el examen*, *puede prepararse*, *cómo debería prepararse*.

---

### tomar — to take (a course/exam) / to drink
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **tomo** | nosotros: tomamos |
| 2nd | tú: tomas | vosotros: tomáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: toma | ellos/ellas/Uds.: toman |

Lesson drills: *tomar un curso de inglés*, *tomar un examen*, *tendrá que tomarlo*.

## Summary

- **salir** — irregular yo (*salgo*); preterite *salió* = "it came out / was released"
- **explicar** — regular -ar; key pattern: *explicarle a él/ella* to disambiguate indirect object
- **prepararse** — regular -ar reflexive; used in *cómo prepararse* (how to prepare oneself)
- **tomar** — regular -ar; covers "take a course," "take an exam," "have a drink"
