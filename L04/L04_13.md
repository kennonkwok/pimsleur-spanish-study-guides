## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *Se me olvidó* — accidental / unintentional "forgot"**
Spanish expresses accidental forgetting with the construction *se me olvidó* + infinitive or noun. The thing forgotten is the grammatical subject; the person is the indirect object.

- *Se me olvidó comprar las entradas.* — I forgot to buy the tickets. (Lit. "The buying of the tickets forgot itself to me.")
- *Se me olvidó el paraguas.* — I forgot my umbrella.

This pattern parallels *me gusta* — the subject is the thing, not the person.

**2. Progressive construction with reflexive verbs: *me estoy resfriando***
Reflexive verbs use *estar* + reflexive gerund to form the present progressive. The reflexive pronoun may precede *estar* or attach to the gerund:

- *Me estoy resfriando.* = *Estoy resfriándome.* — I'm getting a cold.
- *Se está resfriando.* — He/she is getting a cold.
- *Quizás me estoy resfriando.* — Maybe I'm getting a cold.

**3. *Ojalá (que)* — expression of hope**
*Ojalá* expresses hope or wish. It is followed by the subjunctive but at this level students hear it as a fixed phrase:

- *Ojalá que no.* — Hopefully not.
- *Ojalá que sí.* — Hopefully so.

**4. Indirect object with *enviar*: *le envié / le envío***
*Enviar* takes an indirect object pronoun for the recipient:

- *Le envié unas fotos a mi hija.* — I sent some photos to my daughter.
- *Le envío mensajes electrónicos.* — I send her email messages.
- The prepositional phrase (*a mi hija*) clarifies who *le* refers to.

**5. *Si no es molestia* — polite request opener**
This fixed phrase softens requests:

- *Si no es molestia, puede comprarme aspirinas.* — If it's no bother, can you buy me aspirin?

## Verb Conjugations

### resfriarse — to get a cold
**Type:** reflexive; stem-changing (e→ie in present, except nosotros/vosotros)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **me resfrío** | nosotros: nos resfriamos |
| 2nd | tú: **te resfrías** | vosotros: os resfriáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **se resfría** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **se resfrían** |

Lesson drills: *me estoy resfriando*, *se está resfriando*, *estoy resfriado/a* (result state)

---

### sentarse — to sit down
**Type:** reflexive; stem-changing (e→ie, except nosotros/vosotros)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|

| 2nd | tú: **te sientas** | vosotros: os sentáis |

### enviar — to send

## Summary

- **resfriarse** — reflexive, stem-changing (e→ie); key phrases: *me estoy resfriando* (progressive), *estoy resfriado/a* (result state)
- **sentarse** — reflexive, stem-changing (e→ie); used with infinitive: *quiero sentarme*
- **enviar** — regular -ar with written accent shift; *envío* (present) vs. *envié* (preterite)
- **deber** — regular -er; *deber* + infinitive = ought to / should
