## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *ir a* + infinitive — near future ("going to...")**
This construction expresses immediate plans or the near future. Use the conjugated form of *ir* + *a* + infinitive:
- *Voy a pagar.* — I'm going to pay.
- *Vas a comer.* — You're going to eat.
- *No vas a pagar.* — You're not going to pay.
- Negation: place *no* before the conjugated *ir*: *No voy a pagar.*

**2. *también* — also / too**
*También* follows the verb or phrase it modifies:
- *Quiero tomar un refresco también.* — I want to drink a soda too.
- *Yo también.* — Me too.

**3. *¿Y tú?* — And you? / What about you?**
A short follow-up question using *y* (and/what about) + the subject pronoun:
- *Quiero comer algo. ¿Y tú?* — I want to eat something. And you?

**4. *no es nada* — it's nothing / you're welcome**
Used to deflect thanks or describe something as trivial:
- *¡Muchas gracias! — No es nada.* — Thank you! — It's nothing.

**5. Numbers 38, 39 and *ocho*/*nueve***
- *ocho* (8) → *treinta y ocho* (38)
- *nueve* (9) → *treinta y nueve* (39)
- Note the *v* in *nueve* and the soft *b/v* distinction (both sound similar in Spanish).

**6. *dinero* — money; *tiempo* — time**
Two important masculine nouns introduced or reinforced:
- *No tengo dinero.* — I don't have money.
- *No tengo tiempo.* — I don't have time.
- *No tengo mucho dinero/tiempo.* — I don't have much money/time.

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 9 uses predominantly **informal** register (*tú*). Key informal patterns: *¿Vas a comer? / ¿Tienes dinero? / Tú no vas a pagar.* Formal forms from prior lessons continue to be reviewed (*¿Quiere usted...?*).

### Pronunciation Notes
- *También*: stress on *bién* — *tam-BIÉN*; the *b* and *v* in Spanish are pronounced the same.
- *Dinero*: *dee-NE-ro*; the *d* between vowels softens toward an English "th" sound.
- *Ocho*: *O-cho*; the *ch* sounds like English "ch" in "church."
- *Nueve*: *NWE-ve*; the *ue* is a diphthong, and *v* sounds like *b*.
- *También* vs. *tampoco*: *también* = also/too; *tampoco* = neither/not either (not yet drilled).

## Summary
- **pagar** — regular -ar; used with *ir a* + infinitive throughout the lesson
- **ir** — irregular; yo: *voy*, tú: *vas*; *ir a* + infinitive = near future
- **querer** — stem-changing (e→ie); yo: *quiero*
- **tener** — irregular (go-verb); yo: *tengo*
- **saber** — irregular; yo: *sé*
- **estar** — irregular; used for location
- **comer** — regular -er
- **tomar** — regular -ar; "to drink" (beverages) in Latin American Spanish
