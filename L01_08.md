## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *al* — contraction of *a* + *el***
When the preposition *a* (to) is followed by the definite article *el* (the, masculine singular), they contract to *al*. This is mandatory — you cannot say *a el*.
- *Vamos al restaurante.* — Let's go to the restaurant.
- *Al hotel.* — To the hotel.
- This contraction does NOT apply to *la*, *los*, or *las*: *Vamos a la tienda.*

**2. *tomar* = to drink (beverages)**
In Latin American Spanish, *tomar* (literally: to take) is the standard verb for drinking beverages. *Beber* (to drink) exists but is less commonly used in conversation.
- *Quiero tomar algo.* — I want to drink something.
- *Pero quiero tomar un refresco.* — But I want to drink a soda.

**3. Gender of nouns: *el refresco* vs. *la cerveza***
- *el refresco* — masculine → use *un*, *el*
- *la cerveza* — feminine → use *una*, *la*
Both are introduced and distinguished in this lesson.

**4. *cuánto* / *cuánto cuesta* — how much / how much does it cost**
- *¿Cuántos pesos tienes?* — How many pesos do you have? (counts discrete items → plural)
- *¿Cuánto cuesta?* — How much does it cost? (amount → singular)
- *Cuesta doce pesos.* — It costs twelve pesos. (*costar* conjugated as 3rd sg.)

**5. *no quiero nada* / *no quiero comer nada* — double negation**
Spanish requires the negative word after a negated verb (no + verb + nada). This is grammatically correct — NOT a double negative error.
- *No quiero nada.* — I don't want anything.
- *No quiero comer nada.* — I don't want to eat anything.

### Patterns Drilled

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Say you want to drink something | *Quiero tomar algo.* |
| Ask what someone wants to eat/drink (informal) | *¿Qué quieres comer? / ¿Qué quieres tomar?* |
| Ask how many pesos someone has (informal) | *¿Cuántos pesos tienes?* |
| State how many pesos you only have here | *Pero aquí solo tengo doscientos.* |
| Suggest going somewhere | *Vamos al restaurante. / Vamos al hotel.* |
| Ask how much something costs | *¿Cuánto cuesta el refresco?* |
| Answer with a price | *Cuesta doce pesos.* |
| Say you don't want anything | *No quiero nada. / No quiero comer nada.* |
| Say you want to drink (specific drink) | *Quiero tomar una cerveza. / Quiero tomar un refresco.* |
| Ask where the bathroom is | *¿Dónde está el baño?* |

### Formal vs. Informal
Lesson 8 uses **informal** register (*tú*) in the opening dialogue (*Carlos, ¿qué quieres comer? / ¿Cuántos pesos tienes?*) and the main practice scenarios. The lesson continues to build both formal and informal patterns from prior lessons.

### Pronunciation Notes
- *Cerveza*: the first *c* and *z* are both pronounced as the *s* sound in Latin American Spanish (not the "th" of Castilian). *ser-VE-sa*.
- *Refresco*: stress on second syllable — *re-FRES-co*.
- *Cuánto*: the *cu-* combination makes a *kw* sound — *KWAN-to*.
- *Doce*: two syllables, stress on first — *DO-se*.

### costar — to cost
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue)
The stem vowel *o* changes to *ue* in all forms except *nosotros* and *vosotros*.

| 2nd | tú: cuestas | vosotros: costáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **cuesta** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: cuestan |

## Summary
- **tomar** — regular -ar; primary verb for drinking beverages in Latin American Spanish
- **costar** — stem-changing (o→ue); lesson uses only 3rd sg. *cuesta*
- **querer** — stem-changing (e→ie); yo: *quiero*
- **tener** — irregular (go-verb); yo: *tengo*
- **ir** — irregular; *vamos* = let's go; *vamos al* + place = let's go to the (place)
- **estar** — irregular; used for location (*¿Dónde está el baño?*)
- **saber** — irregular; yo: *sé*; used for factual knowledge
- **comer** — regular -er
