## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Conditional of *gustar*: *me/te/le gustaría* — "I/you/he/she would like"**
The conditional tense of *gustar* expresses a polite wish. The conditional suffix is *-ía* added to the infinitive stem.

| Person | Form |
|---|---|
| a mí | me gustaría |
| a ti | te gustaría |
| a él/ella/Ud. | **le gustaría** |
| a nosotros | nos gustaría |
| a vosotros | os gustaría |
| a ellos/Uds. | les gustaría |

- *¿Le gustaría sentarse adentro?* — Would you like to sit inside?
- *Me gustaría sentarme afuera.* — I would like to sit outside.

Note: When the person sitting is the subject, the reflexive infinitive is *sentarse* → *sentarme/sentarte/sentarse* (with pronoun matching).

**2. *llover* — impersonal stem-changing verb**
*Llover* (o→ue) is used only in the 3rd person singular because weather verbs have no personal subject.

- *Está lloviendo.* — It's raining. (progressive)
- *Llueve.* — It rains / It's raining.
- *Así que lo necesitaré.* — So I'll need it. (future)
- *Casi nunca llueve en febrero.* — It almost never rains in February.

**3. *adentro* vs. *afuera* — inside vs. outside**
These adverbs are used with verbs of position or movement. They are often interchangeable with *dentro* and *fuera*.

- *¿Le gustaría sentarse adentro o afuera?* — Would you like to sit inside or outside?
- *Voy a estar fuera del país.* — I'm going to be out of the country. (reviewed from earlier)

**4. *¿Qué le pasa?* — "What's wrong?"**
*Pasarle* + *a alguien* = to happen to someone. When asking what's wrong:
- *¿Qué le pasa?* — What's wrong (with you)? (Ud./3rd person)
- *¿Qué te pasa?* — What's wrong (with you)? (tú)

Response: *No sé. Quizás solo estoy cansada.* — I don't know. Maybe I'm just tired.

**5. Gender agreement with *cansado/a***
*Cansado* agrees with the speaker's gender:
- *Estoy cansada.* — (a woman speaking)
- *Estoy cansado.* — (a man speaking)

## Verb Conjugations

### sentarse — to sit down
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie), reflexive

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **me siento** | nosotros: nos sentamos |
| 2nd | tú: **te sientas** | vosotros: os sentáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **se sienta** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **se sientan** |

Lesson drills: *¿Le gustaría sentarse adentro?*, *Me gustaría sentarme afuera.*, *Hoy prefiero sentarme adentro.*

---

### llover — to rain
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue), impersonal (3rd person singular only)

| | Form |
|---|---|
| Present | **llueve** |
| Progressive | está **lloviendo** |

Lesson drills: *Está lloviendo.*, *Casi nunca llueve en febrero.*, *Así que lo necesitaré.*

---

### necesitar — to need
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |

| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: necesita | ellos/ellas/Uds.: necesitan |

Lesson drills: *Necesito aspirinas.*, *Necesito dos cosas.*, *Hoy necesitamos un paraguas.*

## Summary

- **sentarse** — stem-changing (e→ie) reflexive; *me siento / te sientas / se sienta*; key pattern with *gustaría*
- **llover** — stem-changing (o→ue); impersonal (3rd sg only); progressive *está lloviendo*
- **necesitar** — regular -ar; straightforward "to need"; drilled heavily with *aspirinas*, *paraguas*, *tarjeta*
- **tener** (future) — irregular future stem *tendr-*; *tendrá que* + inf. = will have to
