## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *vos* — Argentine/Central American informal pronoun**
*Vos* replaces *tú* in Argentina, Uruguay, Costa Rica, and Nicaragua. It uses distinct verb endings but you don't need to produce it — passive recognition is enough:

| *tú* form | *vos* form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| *tú comes* | *vos comés* | *¿Vos recomendás?* |
| *tú quieres* | *vos querés* | |
| *tú bebes* | *vos bebés* | |

All South Americans understand *tú*. You'll hear *vos* but won't be drilled on producing it.

**2. Adjective position shift: *viejo* and *gran* before a noun**
Several Spanish adjectives change meaning depending on whether they precede or follow the noun:

| Adjective | Before noun | After noun |
|---|---|---|
| *viejo* | old (long-standing, figurative) | old (in age) |
| *gran(de)* | great / large | big (size) |
| *nuevo* | new (different, another) | new (brand new) |

- *Es un viejo amigo mío.* — He's an old friend of mine. (long-standing friendship)
- *Es un amigo viejo.* — He's an elderly friend.

**3. *para ti / para mí* — informal prepositional pronouns**
After prepositions, subject pronouns change to prepositional pronouns:

| Person | Prepositional form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| yo | *mí* (with accent) | *Una botella para mí.* |
| tú | *ti* (no accent) | *Una es para ti.* |
| él/ella/Ud. | *él/ella/usted* | *Para él.* |

Note: *mi* (my) vs. *mí* (me) — the accent distinguishes them.

**4. Double object pronouns attached to infinitive: *cambiármela***
When both an indirect and direct object pronoun attach to an infinitive:

- *cambiar* + *me* (IO: refers to speaker) + *la* (DO: refers to *la camisa*)
- *Ahora tengo que cambiármela.* — Now I have to change it (on myself).
- Order: IO (*me/te/le/nos/os/les*) + DO (*lo/la/los/las*), both after the infinitive.

**5. Present counterfactual with plural subject: *si no fueran***
The present/future counterfactual uses the imperfect subjunctive in the *si* clause. When the subject is plural, the form changes:

- *Si las botellas no fueran tan pesadas, habría comprado más vino.* — If the bottles weren't so heavy, I would have bought more wine.
- *fueran* = 3rd pl. imperfect subjunctive of *ser* (agrees with *las botellas*)
- Compare (L5/9): *si no fuera* (singular subject)

**6. Social expressions: *buen provecho* and *salud***
- *¡Buen provecho!* — Enjoy your meal! (said when someone starts eating; like *bon appétit*)
- *¡Salud!* — Cheers! / To your health! (toast when raising glasses)

## Verb Conjugations

### trabajar — to work
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: trabajo | nosotros: trabajamos |
| 2nd | tú: trabajas | vosotros: trabajáis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: trabaja | ellos/ellas/Uds.: trabajan |

Imperfect: *trabajaba, trabajabas, trabajaba, **trabajábamos**, trabajabais, trabajaban*
Lesson drills: *trabajábamos juntos* — we used to work together.

---

### deber — to owe / should / must
**Type:** regular -er

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: debo | nosotros: debemos |
| 2nd | tú: debes | vosotros: debéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: debe | ellos/ellas/Uds.: deben |

*Deber* + infinitive = should/must: *debes comer carne*, *debemos compartir*
Lesson drills: *debes comer carne*, *entonces debemos compartir una botella de vino tinto*.

---

### caminar — to walk
**Type:** regular -ar

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: camino | nosotros: caminamos |
| 2nd | tú: caminas | vosotros: camináis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: camina | ellos/ellas/Uds.: caminan |

Lesson drills: *caminar es buen ejercicio*, *voy a ir a pie*.

## Summary

- **vos** — Argentine/Central American informal pronoun; passive recognition only; *tú* is always understood
- **viejo** before noun = long-standing/figurative "old"; after noun = old in age
- **para mí / para ti** — prepositional pronouns (note accent on *mí*)
- **cambiármela** — double object pronoun (IO + DO) attached to infinitive
- **si no fueran** — imperfect subjunctive (plural), present counterfactual *si* clause
- **buen provecho** = enjoy your meal; **salud** = cheers
- **trabajar** — regular -ar; imperfect *trabajábamos* = we used to work
- **deber** — regular -er; *deber* + inf. = should/must
- **caminar** — regular -ar; *caminar es buen ejercicio*
