## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. *dar* preterite — *dio, le dio***
*Dar* (to give) has an irregular preterite identical in form to *ser/ir* but with different context:

| Person | *Dar* preterite |
|---|---|
| yo | *di* |
| tú | *diste* |
| él/ella/Ud. | ***dio*** |
| nosotros | *dimos* |
| ellos/Uds. | *dieron* |

- *El médico le dio una receta.* — The doctor gave her a prescription.
- *Me dio su número.* — He gave me his number.
- *Le* = indirect object (to him/her); the thing given = direct object.

**2. *debía haber aprendido* — "should have done" (imperfect of *deber*)**
Another way to express past obligation not fulfilled:

- *Debía haber aprendido un poco de portugués antes de ir.* — I should have learned a little Portuguese before going.
- *Debía haber aprendido* vs. *debí haber comido* (L5/11) — both express regret about past obligation; imperfect *debía* sounds slightly more reflective.

**3. *nos fue bien hasta que* — "it went well for us until"**
Extends the *irle bien/mal* construction with a time clause:

- *Nos fue bien hasta que mi esposa se enfermó del estómago.* — It went well for us until my wife got an upset stomach.
- *hasta que* = until; triggers past tense when describing what happened.

**4. *durante* — "during" + time span**
*Durante* precedes a time span without an article change:

- *Durante unos días no pudo salir del hotel.* — During a few days she couldn't leave the hotel.
- *Durante el viaje* = during the trip
- *Durante* does not trigger subjunctive; it's a preposition, not a conjunction.

**5. *ser aburrido* vs. *estar aburrido***
- *ser aburrido* = to be boring (characteristic): *Mi trabajo es aburrido.* — My job is boring.
- *estar aburrido* = to feel bored (state): *Estoy aburrido.* — I'm bored.

**6. *¿a nombre de quién?* — reservation language**
Standard phrase when making reservations:
- *¿A nombre de quién hago la reserva?* — Under whose name should I make the reservation?
- *A nombre de Anne Martin.* — Under Anne Martin's name.
- *me llamo* = my name is (conversational); *mi nombre es* = my name is (formal/written)

## Verb Conjugations

### dar — to give
**Type:** highly irregular; preterite has no accent marks (same pattern as *ir/ser*)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **doy** | nosotros: damos |
| 2nd | tú: das | vosotros: dais |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: da | ellos/ellas/Uds.: dan |

Preterite: ***di**, diste, **dio**, dimos, disteis, **dieron***
Lesson drills: *el médico le dio una receta*, *¿quién te dio la bufanda? — mi esposo me la dio*.

---

### parar — to stop

| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: para | ellos/ellas/Uds.: paran |

Lesson drills: *el autobús tiene que parar en la frontera* — the bus has to stop at the border.

| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: aprende | ellos/ellas/Uds.: aprenden |

## Summary

- **dar** — highly irregular; preterite *dio* (3rd sg.); *le dio una receta* = gave her a prescription
- **parar** — regular -ar; *tiene que parar en la frontera* = has to stop at the border
- **aprender** — regular -er; *debía haber aprendido* = I should have learned
- ***durante*** = during (preposition + time span); *durante unos días* = for a few days
- ***debía haber aprendido*** — imperfect *deber* + perfect infinitive = should have done (regret)
- ***ser aburrido*** = to be boring (characteristic); ***estar aburrido*** = to feel bored (state)
- ***¿a nombre de quién?*** = under whose name? (reservation language)
- ***nos fue bien hasta que*** = it went well for us until (*irle bien* + time clause)
