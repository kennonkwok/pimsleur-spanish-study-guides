## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Hotel vocabulary and requests**
This lesson introduces a new setting — checking into a hotel. Key question-and-answer patterns:

- *¿Hay fax en el hotel?* — Is there a fax machine in the hotel?
- *¿Hay fotocopiadora?* — Is there a photocopier?
- *¿A qué horas sirve el desayuno?* — At what time is breakfast served?
- *Hay fax en nuestro centro de negocios.* — There's a fax machine in our business center.

**2. *sacar copias* vs. *hacer copias***
*Sacar copias* is the standard phrase used with a photocopier. Literally "to extract/pull out copies."

- *Tengo que sacar copias de unos documentos.* — I have to make copies of some documents.
- *Puede usarla para sacar copias.* — You can use it to make copies.

**3. *se sirve* — passive reflexive construction**
The reflexive *se* + verb creates an impersonal or passive meaning ("is served," "one serves").

- *El desayuno se sirve de las 7 a las 10.* — Breakfast is served from 7 to 10.
- This construction avoids naming the subject; it's very common in Spanish for signs, menus, and service contexts.

**4. *desde allí* — "from there"**
*Desde* = from (a place or starting point). *Desde allí* = from there.

- *Puede enviar el fax desde allí.* — You can send the fax from there.
- *Puede usarla desde allí.* — You can use it from there.

**5. Preterite of *comprar* and *ir***
This lesson reviews preterite forms in context:

- *Compré esta camisa.* — I bought this shirt.
- *Esta mañana fui al mercado.* — This morning I went to the market.
- *fui* = preterite of *ir* (yo) — the same form is also preterite of *ser* (I was); context distinguishes.

**6. *se divorció* — preterite reflexive**
*Divorciarse* is a reflexive verb. Third-person preterite: *se divorció* (she/he got divorced).

- *¿Sabes? Se divorció.* — You know? She got divorced.

## Verb Conjugations

### servir — to serve
**Type:** stem-changing (e→i) in all present forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **sirvo** | nosotros: servimos |
| 2nd | tú: **sirves** | vosotros: servís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **sirve** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **sirven** |

Lesson drills: *El desayuno se sirve de las 7 a las 10.* (3rd sg. passive reflexive)

---

Lesson drills: *Se divorció.* (preterite, 3rd sg.)

## Summary

- **servir** — stem-changing (e→i); *se sirve* = passive reflexive "is served" — key pattern for service contexts
- **pedir** — stem-changing (e→i); used for ordering food, room service, or requesting items
- **enviar** — regular -ar with written accent in stressed stem forms (*envío, envías*...); used for sending faxes/messages
- **divorciarse** — regular -ar reflexive; preterite *se divorció* = she/he got divorced
