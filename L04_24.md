## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Weather expressions with *hacer***
Weather in Spanish often uses *hacer* (lit. "to make/do") + noun. There is no subject equivalent to English "it."

| Expression | Meaning |
|---|---|
| *Hace sol.* | It's sunny. |
| *Hace viento.* | It's windy. |
| *Hace calor.* | It's hot. |
| *Hace frío.* | It's cold. |

- *Ayer llovió, pero hoy hace sol.* — Yesterday it rained, but today it's sunny.
- *No hace viento hoy.* — It's not windy today.

**2. *Estar* + gerund (present progressive) vs. preterite of *llover***
The lesson contrasts ongoing action (in progress) with completed past action:

- *Está lloviendo.* — It's raining. (right now, in progress)
- *Ayer llovió.* — Yesterday it rained. (completed event, preterite)

*Llover* is a stem-changing verb (o→ue) used only in 3rd person singular (impersonal). Preterite: *llovió*.

**3. *Quisiera* + infinitive or *que* + imperfect subjunctive**
*Quisiera* is the imperfect subjunctive / conditional of *querer*, used to express polite wishes:

| Structure | Example | English |
|---|---|---|
| *quisiera* + infinitive | *Quisiera tener más trabajo.* | I would like to have more work. |
| *quisiera que* + imperfect subjunctive | *Quisiera que no lloviera.* | I wish that it wouldn't rain. |
| *quisiera que* + imperfect subjunctive | *Quisiera que estuviera aquí ya.* | I wish that she were here already. |

Key imperfect subjunctive forms drilled:
- *lloviera* (from *llover*) — it would rain
- *estuviera* (from *estar*) — she/he were

**4. *¿A qué te dedicas?* — asking what someone does for a living (informal)**
The formal equivalent drilled previously is *¿A qué se dedica (usted)?*. *Dedicarse a* = to devote oneself to / to work as.

- *¿A qué te dedicas?* — What do you do (for a living)? (informal, *tú*)
- *Soy abogado / periodista.* — I'm a lawyer / journalist.

No article is used with professions after *ser*.

**5. *Quedar* for clothing fit**
*Quedar* appears in two distinct uses in this course: fixed location (*¿Dónde queda el cine?*) and clothing fit:

- *No me quedan bien.* — They don't fit me well.
- *Me gustan estos pantalones.* — I like these slacks.

The subject is the clothing item (plural *pantalones* → *quedan*/*gustan*).

**6. Possession: *son suyos/as***
*Suyo/a/os/as* = his/her/your (formal)/their; used as a standalone possessive pronoun after *ser*:

- *¿Son suyos?* — Are they yours? (formal, or his/hers)
- *No, no son míos.* — No, they're not mine.

Agrees in gender and number with the object possessed, not the owner.

**7. Preterite of *traer*: *traje***
Irregular preterite — *traer* has a completely irregular preterite stem (*traj-*):

| Person | Form |
|---|---|
| yo | **traje** |
| tú | trajiste |
| él/ella/Ud. | trajo |
| nosotros | trajimos |
| vosotros | trajisteis |
| ellos/Uds. | trajeron |

Lesson drill: *No traje suficientes pantalones.* — I didn't bring enough slacks.

## Verb Conjugations

### llover — to rain
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue); impersonal — used only in 3rd person singular

| | Singular |
|---|---|
| 3rd (impersonal) | **llueve** (present) / **llovió** (preterite) / **lloviera** (imperf. subj.) |

Notes: *Está lloviendo* (present progressive). Not conjugated in other persons.

---

### querer — to want / to wish
**Type:** stem-changing (e→ie); also irregular yo: *quiero*; imperfect subjunctive = *quisiera*

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **quiero** | nosotros: queremos |
| 2nd | tú: **quieres** | vosotros: queréis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **quiere** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **quieren** |

*Quisiera* (imperfect subjunctive / conditional form): used for polite wishes. Lesson drills: *quisiera tener más/menos trabajo*, *quisiera estar de vacaciones*, *quisiera que no lloviera*.

---

### gustar — to please / to like
**Type:** regular -ar; used in reverse construction (thing = subject)

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 3rd singular | (me/te/le/nos/os/les) **gusta** | — |
| 3rd plural | — | (me/te/le/nos/os/les) **gustan** |

Lesson drills: *me gustaría ir a la playa* (conditional), *no me gusta el color*, *me gustan estos pantalones*.

## Summary

- **llover** — impersonal stem-changer (o→ue); *llueve* / *llovió* / *lloviera*; used only in 3rd person singular
- **querer** — stem-changer (e→ie); *quisiera* (imperfect subj./conditional) = polite wish; *quisiera* + infinitive OR *quisiera que* + imperfect subjunctive
- **gustar** — regular -ar, reverse construction; singular *gusta* / plural *gustan*; *gustaría* = conditional ("would like")
- **venir** — irregular (*vengo*, e→ie, future stem *vendr-*); *vendrá* = she/he will come
- **lucir** — *go*-verb in yo (*luzco*); used for appearance (*luces / luce cansada*)
- **conocer** — *go*-verb in yo (*conozco*); requires personal *a* before persons
- **traer** — *go*-verb in yo (*traigo*); irregular preterite *traje* (stem *traj-*)
- **seguir** — stem-changer (e→i), *go* in yo (*sigo*); formal imperative *siga*
- **doblar** — regular -ar; formal imperative *doble*; used for directions (turn)
- **quedar** — regular -ar; two distinct uses: clothing fit (*no me quedan bien*) and fixed location (*¿dónde queda?*)
- **Key phrases:** *hace sol/viento/calor* (weather with *hacer*); *¿a qué te dedicas?* (informal profession question); *son suyos/míos* (possessive pronouns); *a decir verdad* (to tell the truth); *de vacaciones / de viaje de negocios* (on vacation / on a business trip)
