## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Impersonal weather expressions with *hacer***
Spanish uses *hacer* (literally "to make/do") for many weather conditions. The verb is always third-person singular — it has no real subject.

| Expression | Meaning |
|---|---|
| *Hace calor.* | It's hot. (Lit. "It makes heat.") |
| *Hace frío.* | It's cold. |
| *Hace sol.* | It's sunny. |
| *Hace buen tiempo.* | The weather is good. |
| *Hace demasiado calor.* | It's too hot. |
| *Hace mucho calor.* | It's very hot. |

**2. *ni… ni…* — double negative "neither… nor…"**
Spanish uses *ni… ni…* to express "neither… nor…" The verb stays negative as well.

- *Ni azúcar ni leche.* — Neither sugar nor milk.
- *Sin azúcar ni leche.* — Without sugar or milk.

**3. *tampoco* — "neither / not either"**
*Tampoco* is the negative counterpart of *también*. It means "neither" or "not… either."

- *Sin leche tampoco.* — Without milk either.
- *Yo tampoco.* — Me neither.

**4. Direct object pronouns with *ni… ni…* and *tampoco***
These are drilled in negation patterns. Compare:
- *Me gustan las cosas dulces.* (positive)
- *No me gustan las cosas dulces.* (negative)
- *No me gustan las cosas con azúcar.* (specific negative)

**5. *del* contraction — *de + el***
When *de* precedes the definite article *el*, they contract to *del*:
- *No quiero salir del edificio.* — I don't want to leave the building.
- *Cerca del banco.* — Close to the bank.

**6. *irse* — reflexive "to leave / to go away"**
*Nos vamos* is from *irse* (reflexive *ir*), meaning to go away/leave, distinct from *ir* (to go somewhere).
- *Nos vamos esta noche.* — We're leaving tonight.

## Verb Conjugations

### leer — to read
**Type:** regular -er

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **leo** | nosotros: leemos |
| 2nd | tú: **lees** | vosotros: leéis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **lee** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **leen** |

Lesson drills: *leo el periódico*, *tú lees*, *leo mucho*, *lees todos los días*.

---

### encontrar — to find
**Type:** stem-changing (o→ue) — changes in all forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **encuentro** | nosotros: encontramos |
| 2nd | tú: **encuentras** | vosotros: encontráis |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **encuentra** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **encuentran** |

Lesson drills: *necesito encontrar un periódico*.

---

## Summary

- **leer** — regular -er; heavily drilled: *leo*, *lees*, *lee* for newspapers, documents, tablet
- **encontrar** — stem-changing (o→ue); drilled in *necesito encontrar*
- **salir** — irregular yo (*salgo*); drilled in *no quiero salir del edificio*
- **estudiar** — regular -ar; *estudia medicina*, university context
- **tomar** — regular -ar; drilled for imperative "take/here you go" and "I take my coffee…"
- **hacer** (weather) — impersonal: *hace calor/frío/sol* — no stem change needed in 3rd person weather use
