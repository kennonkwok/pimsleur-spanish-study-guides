## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Formal vs. informal object pronouns in introductions**

*Presentar* uses an indirect object pronoun that matches the register:

| Register | Phrase | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Formal | *Le presento a mi esposa.* | *le* = indirect object (Ud.) |
| Informal | *Te presento a mi esposa.* | *te* = indirect object (tú) |

The infinitive ending also shifts in pleasantries:
- Formal: *Mucho gusto en conocer**la**.* (knowing her/you — Ud.)
- Informal: *Mucho gusto en conocer**te**.* (knowing you — tú)

**2. *¿A qué se dedica?* vs. *¿A qué te dedicas?* — "What do you do?"**

The verb *dedicarse* is used reflexively to ask about someone's occupation. The preposition *a* is always present:

- Formal: *¿A qué se dedica usted?* / *¿A qué se dedica en el banco?*
- Informal: *¿A qué te dedicas?* / *¿Y tú, a qué te dedicas?*

No article is used before profession nouns after *ser*:
- *Soy gerente.* — I'm a manager. (NOT *un gerente*)
- *Es gerente de una empresa mexicana.*

**3. *estar* + *casado/a/os/as* — marital state**

*Casado* is a past participle used as an adjective with *estar* (state, not permanent identity). It must agree in gender and number with the subject:

| Subject | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Mi hermano (m sg) | casado | *Mi hermano está casado.* |
| Mi hermana (f sg) | casada | *Mi hermana está casada.* |
| They (m pl) | casados | *Están casados.* |

The partner is introduced with *con*: *casado con una española* / *casada con un norteamericano*.

**4. *desde hace* + time — duration up to now**

To express how long something has been ongoing (equivalent to English "for X time"), Spanish uses *desde hace* + time expression in the **present tense** (not past):

- *Viven en España desde hace dos años.* — They have lived in Spain for two years.
- *Viven allí desde hace dos años.* — They have lived there for two years.

The question form: *¿Desde cuándo viven en España?* — How long have they lived in Spain? (Lit. "since when do they live in Spain?")

**5. *seguro/segura* — gender agreement with *estar***

*No estoy seguro/a* changes form based on the **speaker's** gender:
- Male speaker: *No estoy seguro.*
- Female speaker: *No estoy segura.*
- Third-person plural: *No están seguros.* / *Ellos no están seguros.*

**6. *depender de* — "to depend on"**

*Depender* is a regular -er verb. It always takes *de*:
- *Depende de muchas cosas.*
- *Depende de mi empresa.*
- *Depende del clima.* (*del* = contraction of *de + el*)

**7. *en verdad* vs. *de verdad***

Both mean "really," but the lesson distinguishes usage:
- *¿De verdad?* — Really? (one-word question, standalone)
- *En verdad hablas muy bien.* — You really do speak well. (in statements, for emphasis)

**8. Seasons with *en* — no article**

- *en verano* — in summer
- *en otoño* — in autumn/fall
- No article: *en el verano* is less common in these drilled phrases.

## Summary

- **Core topic:** Social introductions (formal and informal registers) + talking about family, work, and travel
- **presentar / presentarse** — regular -ar; key distinction: *le presento* (formal) vs. *te presento* (informal); clitic on infinitive shifts too (*conocerla* vs. *conocerte*)
- **dedicarse** — regular -ar reflexive; the go-to phrase for "what do you do": *¿A qué se dedica?* (formal) / *¿A qué te dedicas?* (informal); no article before professions after *ser*
- **estar + casado/a** — marital state, not permanent trait; adjective agrees with subject gender/number
- **desde hace** — key structure for ongoing duration: present tense verb + *desde hace* + time span (no past tense needed)
- **seguro/a** — agrees with speaker's gender when used with *estar*; watch *seguro* (m) vs. *segura* (f)
- **depender de** — regular -er; *depende del clima* (*del* = *de + el*)
- **en verdad** — used in statements for emphasis; *de verdad* preferred as a standalone question
- **viajar** — regular -ar; heavily drilled; seasonal travel context with *en otoño / en verano* (no article)
- **quedarse** — regular -ar reflexive; appeared only in *ir a* + infinitive constructions
