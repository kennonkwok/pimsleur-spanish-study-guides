## Grammar Review

### New Concepts

**1. Passive *se*: *se sirve / se sirven***
The impersonal *se* + verb creates a passive meaning "is/are served" or "it is [done]":

- *El desayuno se sirve de las seis a las once.* — Breakfast is served from six to eleven.
- *La cena se sirve de las ocho a la medianoche.* — Dinner is served from eight to midnight.
- *¿A qué horas sirven el desayuno?* — At what hours do you serve breakfast? (active)
- *No se sirve hasta las ocho y media.* — It isn't served until eight thirty.

**2. *Me gustaría* — I would like (conditional)**
*Me gustaría* is the conditional (polite) form of *me gusta*:

- *Me gustaría, pero no puedo.* — I'd like to, but I can't.
- More polite than *quiero*; equivalent to "I would like"

**3. *Tuve que* — I had to (preterite)**
The preterite of *tener que* expresses obligation that was fulfilled in the past:

- *Tuve que comprar una maleta nueva.* — I had to buy a new suitcase.
- *Así que tuve que comprar una maleta.* — So I had to buy a suitcase.

Compare: *tengo que* (I have to, present) vs. *tuve que* (I had to, past)

**4. *A qué horas sirven...* — asking meal hours**
Standard hotel/restaurant question pattern:

- *¿A qué horas sirven el desayuno?* — At what hours do you serve breakfast?
- *¿A qué horas sirven la cena?* — At what hours do you serve dinner?
- Response: *De las seis a las once de la mañana.* — From six to eleven a.m.
- Response: *De las ocho a la medianoche.* — From eight to midnight.

**5. *Vamos a caminar* — let's go for a walk**
*Vamos a* + infinitive as a suggestion ("let's"):

- *Vamos a caminar.* — Let's go for a walk.
- *¿Por qué no me acompaña a caminar en el parque?* — Why don't you join me for a walk in the park?

**6. Checking out: *Aquí está mi llave***
*Aquí está* = "here is" — used when handing something over:

- *Aquí está mi llave.* — Here is my key.
- Contrast: *¿Dónde está mi llave?* — Where is my key?

## Verb Conjugations

### servir — to serve
**Type:** stem-changing (e→i) — changes in all forms except nosotros/vosotros

| | Singular | Plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | yo: **sirvo** | nosotros: servimos |
| 2nd | tú: **sirves** | vosotros: servís |
| 3rd | él/ella/Ud.: **sirve** | ellos/ellas/Uds.: **sirven** |

Lesson drills: *¿a qué horas sirven el desayuno?*, *el desayuno se sirve*, *la cena se sirve*

## Summary

- **servir** — stem-changing (e→i); key construction: *se sirve / sirven* for meal hours; passive *se* + *servir*
- **acompañar** — regular -ar; polite invitation: *¿por qué no me acompaña...?*
- **caminar** — regular -ar; *vamos a caminar* = let's go for a walk
- Key grammar: passive *se* (*se sirve*); conditional *me gustaría* = I'd like; preterite *tuve que* = I had to; meal hour question pattern *¿a qué horas sirven...?*
